5 Government Intermediate Bond Mutual Funds To Buy | Seeking Alpha Aug. 11, 2015 2:35 AM ET  |  Includes: DRGIX , FSTGX , HAUSX , MGIDX , VFITX by: Zacks Funds Mutual funds investing in government debt securities are among the most secure investment options which provide regular income while protecting capital invested. Funds which are part of this category bring a great deal of stability to portfolios with a large proportion of equity, while providing dividends more frequently than individual bonds. U.S. government bonds funds usually invest in Treasury bills, notes and securities issued by government agencies. They are considered to be the safest in the bond fund category and are ideal options for the risk-averse investor. Meanwhile, intermediate-term funds usually provide a safer option for investors, when compared to short-term funds. Fixed income securities having average maturity period between 3 and 10 years are classified as intermediate securities. These funds are believed to assure more stability and provide higher return than what short-term offer. Below we will share with you 5 buy-ranked government intermediate bond mutual funds. Each has earned either a Zacks Mutual Fund Rank #1 (Strong Buy) or a Zacks Mutual Fund Rank #2 (Buy) as we expect these mutual funds to outperform their peers in the future. Vanguard Intermediate-Term Treasury Investor (MUTF: VFITX ) seeks modest and consistent level of income over the long term. VFITX invests a large chunk of its assets in instruments of U.S. Treasury including bills, bonds, and notes. VFITX generally has a dollar-weighted average maturity between 5 and 10 years. The Vanguard Intermediate-Term Treasury Investor fund has returned 1.4% in the year-to-date frame. Hartford U.S. Government Securities HLS IA (MUTF: HAUSX ) invests a lion's share of its assets in securities that are affiliated by the US government or its entities. HAUSX invests in the US treasury instruments and other securities of the US government. HAUSX may also invest in mortgage-backed securities of the US government. The Hartford US Government Securities HLS IA fund has returned 1.5% in the year-to-date frame. Fidelity Intermediate Government Income (MUTF: FSTGX ) seeks high current income with capital preservation. FSTGX invests a major portion of its assets in derivatives of the US government and repurchase agreements. FSTGX may invest in securities of Congress sponsored entities that are not guaranteed by the US government. FSTGX is expected to have an average maturity duration of 3-10 years. FSTGX invests across a wide range of sectors through Fidelity's central funds. The Fidelity Intermediate Government Income fund has returned 1.1% in the year-to-date frame. Dreyfus U.S. Treasury Intermediate Term (MUTF: DRGIX ) invests a majority of its assets in securities issued by the US Treasury. Though DRGIX may invest in securities irrespective of their maturity period, it is expected to maintain a dollar-weighted average maturity of 3 years to 10 years. DRGIX normally has an effective duration of 2.5 years to 6 years. The Dreyfus US Treasury Intermediate Term fund has returned 1% in the year-to-date frame. AMG Managers Intermediate Duration Government (MUTF: MGIDX ) seeks total return more than that of market indices related to mortgage-backed securities. MGIDX primarily invests in debt securities of the US government or other agencies authorized by the government. MGIDX invests in securities having impressive credit quality to reduce risk.
