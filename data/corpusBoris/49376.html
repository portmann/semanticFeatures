The focus of this article is to provide a detailed projection of FSFR's NAV per share as of 3/31/2015. Prior to results being provided to the public after the market close on 5/11/2015 (via the company's quarterly press release), I would like to analyze FSFR's NAV as of 3/31/2015 and provide readers a general direction on how I believe this recent quarter has panned out. I will also include my quarterly "net investment income" ("NII") and "net increase (decrease) in net assets resulting from operations" (also known as "earnings per share" ("EPS")) projections in this article. Before we begin the company's NAV projection analysis, I wanted to provide readers my current thoughts in regards to FSFR as a possible equity investment. This should help some readers better understand what is currently occurring within FSFR regarding several key metrics and also assist when reading the NAV projection analysis. My current BUY, SELL, or HOLD recommendation for FSFR will be at the bottom of this section of the article. Currently, I believe there are six reasons why FSFR is an attractive equity investment. First, FSFR trades at a material discount to my projected NAV as of 3/31/2015 (per share figure provided at the end of the analysis below). As of 5/1/2015, FSFR's stock price was $10.80 per share. This calculates to a discount of ($1.58) per share for a price to NAV ratio of 0.8724 (a 12.76% discount). While FSFR's discount to NAV has modestly decreased over the past several months, I still believe investors should see this material discount as an attractive opportunity to currently own this stock. Second, even though FSFR had an annual forward yield to the company's NAV as of 12/31/2014 of 9.58%, the company had an annual forward yield to its stock price as of 5/1/2015 of 11.11%. I believe this current yield, which is currently in excess of 10%, is another positive factor of owning FSFR. Third, FSFR announced it has formed a joint venture ("JV") partnership with members of the Glick Family (GF Funding) to expand the company's investment portfolio. This newly created JV partnership is called "FSFR Glick Family JV LLC" (FSFR Glick JV) and was formed during the fiscal first quarter of 2015. FSFR's affiliate, Fifth Street Finance Corp. (NASDAQ: FSC ), recently created a similar JV partnership with a subsidiary of the Kemper Corporation (NYSE: KMPR ) called "Senior Loan Fund JV 1" (SLF JV 1). FSC's SLF JV 1 continues to generate a higher rate of return when compared to the weighted average of the rest of company's investment portfolio. This is due to the fact management fees are based on the net assets of SLF JV 1 (not gross assets) which is one strategic advantage. By utilizing JV partnerships, FSC can circumvent the business development company ("BDC") debt-to-equity leverage ratio threshold of 1.0x while still maintaining the company's Regulated Investment Company ("RIC") status per the Internal Revenue Code ("IRC"). Similar advantages should occur within FSFR Glick JV when fully funded. Regarding FSFR Glick JV, both parties have committed to provide a combined $100 million of debt and equity to this recently created entity. FSFR will provide $87.5 million and GF Funding will provide $12.5 million. During the fiscal second quarter of 2015, FSFR was in discussions with several lenders regarding debt financing for FSFR Glick JV. As such, I'm not anticipating this JV was fully operational as of 3/31/2015. Even though FSFR Glick JV did not materially affect earnings for the fiscal second quarter of 2015, the benefits of this type of investment structure should begin to have a cumulative effect at some point during 2015. By the end of April 2015, $86.6 million of funding has been provided to invest in a portfolio of senior secured loans. This funding has been provided through equity of $60 million, in accordance with the proportional percentages discussed above, and through FSFR Glick JV's recently secured $200 million revolving credit facility with Credit Suisse AG, Cayman Islands (Credit Suisse). When calculated, management anticipates FSFR Glick JV will have an investment capacity of approximately $300 million. Management has also stated FSFR will likely have future discussions with other interested third parties to create additional JV partnerships over the next several years. I believe these types of entities should be seen as a positive factor. Fourth, FSFR has recently continued to expand the company's available debt financing. On 10/16/2014, FSFR expanded the company's available debt financing by amending its $100 million Natixis Revolving Credit Facility. FSFR doubled the financing capability of the existing credit facility which now includes a $100 million revolver and a $100 million term loan. The $100 million Natixis Revolving Credit Facility and $50 million Natixis Term Loan are both priced at the commercial paper rate plus 1.9%. The $50 million Fifth Third Bank Term Loan is priced at LIBOR plus 2%. I believe the revolving credit facility and term loans have attractive borrowing rates and have allowed FSFR to continue to expand the company's investment portfolio. Furthermore, during January 2015, management announced FSFR had also closed a $175 million revolving credit facility with Citibank, N.A. (Citi). The Citi Senior Secured Revolving Credit Facility is priced at LIBOR plus 2% - 2.25%. I believe this new revolving credit facility is also attractively priced and will allow FSFR to continue to expand the company's investment portfolio over the foreseeable future. I believe the expansion of FSFR's available debt financing, at attractive interest rates, should be seen as a positive factor. Fifth, management has continued to reiterate FSFR does not anticipate issuing shares of common stock below CURRENT NAV for the foreseeable future. This notion has recently been confirmed when, on 3/18/2015, FSFR held the company's 2015 Annual Meeting of Stockholders where there was no proposal for authorization to issue common stock at prices below NAV (unlike 2014's meeting). Even though I have stated I was against the August 2014 equity offering (due to the fact it was materially dilutive to existing shareholders; at the time I did not own the stock), I am comforted management will not perform any further dilutive actions for the foreseeable future. I believe this notion has been further solidified from the recent expansion of FSFR's available debt financing which was discussed earlier. As such, I believe FSFR not performing any dilutive equity offerings for the foreseeable future is another positive factor. Finally, the material decrease in the price of oil during FSFR's fiscal first quarter of 2015 has caused "distress"/speculation to surround most of the oil and gas sector (including parts of the energy sector) regarding falling margins and potential struggling/bankrupt entities. While I would agree markets should have some sort of generalized "cause for concern" within the high-yield debt market, I also believe one needs to "drill down" to each BDC's debt and equity investments to see which portfolio companies could be affected by this event. It should also be noted there has been a recent modest "spike" in oil prices over the past several weeks which generally bodes well for the entire sector. As of 9/30/2014, FSFR had 1.58% of the company's investment portfolio in the oil and gas sector (including certain investments in the energy sector which had "oil and gas" characteristics). As of 12/31/2014, only 0.77% of FSFR's portfolio had debt and equity investments within the oil and gas sector. When compared to the nine other BDC peers I currently cover, this was one of the lowest percentages at potential risk for investment depreciation. As of 12/31/2014, I calculated Apollo Investment Corp. (NASDAQ: AINV ), Main Street Capital Corp. (NYSE: MAIN ), Medley Capital Corp. (NYSE: MCC ), and Prospect Capital Corp. (NASDAQ: PSEC ) had a 18.61%, 6.39%, 6.81%, and 5.16% exposure to the oil and gas sector, respectively (again, including certain investments in the energy sector which had "oil and gas" characteristics). I believe FSFR's recent extremely low exposure to the oil and gas sector (percentage wise) should be seen as a positive sign. FSFR recently closed at $10.80 per share as of 5/1/2015. This was a ($1.58) per share discount to my projected NAV of $12.38 per share as of 3/31/2015. This calculates to a price to NAV ratio of 0.8724 or a discount of (12.76%). From the analysis provided above, including additional factors not analyzed within this article, I currently rate FSFR as a HOLD when I believe the company's stock price is trading at a minor (less than 5%) discount to NAV as of 3/31/2015, a STRONG HOLD when trading at a modest (at or greater than 5% but less than 10%) discount to NAV as of 3/31/2015, and a BUY when trading at a material (at or greater than 10%) discount to NAV as of 3/31/2015. As such, I currently rate FSFR as a BUY. Each investor's BUY, SELL, or HOLD decision is based on one's risk tolerance, time horizon, and dividend income goals. My personal recommendation may not fit each investor's current investing strategy.
