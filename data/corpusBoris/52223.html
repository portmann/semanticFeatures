Before we begin the company's NAV projection analysis, I wanted to provide readers my current thoughts in regards to FSC as a possible equity investment. This should help some readers better understand what is currently occurring within FSC regarding several key metrics and also assist when reading the NAV projection analysis. My current BUY, SELL, or HOLD recommendation for FSC will be at the bottom of this section of the article. I believe there are several positive and negative aspects to FSC right now. Let us first discuss a few positive factors. First, FSC had an annual forward yield to the company's stock price as of 5/1/2015 of 10.10%. This is one reason why investors are attracted to this stock and to the business development company ("BDC") sector in general. Some investors are looking for dividend income in this continued low interest rate environment. I believe FSC could be an enticing proposition for some investors looking for a monthly income stream while having a somewhat lower risk tolerance when compared to other high-yielding, more leveraged sectors. The new lower monthly dividend per share rate should have a greater probability of being maintained. This conclusion was provided in a recent BDC comparison article . For this reason, I believe some long-term investors could see the recent dividend cut as a potential positive factor once this reduction is behind the company. Second, I like the following aspects of FSC's business operations: 1) new executive management team including Chief Executive Officer ("CEO") Todd Owens who was an employee of Goldman Sachs & Co, for 24 years (partner in 2008); 2) expansion of offices across the U.S.; and 3) highest proportion of the investment portfolio within the healthcare services/technology sector. In addition, FSC has continued to "ramp up" loan syndications. As such, FSC has recently recognized minor net realized gains on syndicated loans that have appreciated in value. FSC has also been able to generate fee income from the transactions that should enhance overall yields on the company's investment portfolio. Third, FSC has recently formed a "joint venture" ("JV") partnership with a subsidiary of the Kemper Corp. (NYSE: KMPR ), Trinity Universal Insurance Company (Trinity). This recently formed JV partnership is called "Senior Loan Fund JV 1" (SLF JV 1). When SLF JV 1 is fully funded, management believes this investment will increase NII and generate higher rates of returns when compared to the rest of investment portfolio. This is due to the fact management fees are based on the net assets of SLF JV 1 (not gross assets) which is one strategic advantage. Management has continued to state SLF JV 1's internal rate of return ("IRR") remained steady in the mid-teens over the past two fiscal quarters. This includes the notion this specific JV had not reached its full operating capacity going into the fiscal second quarter of 2015. Using JV partnerships allows FSC to circumvent the BDC debt-to-equity leverage ratio threshold of 1.0x (including the notion of remaining a RIC per the IRC). In October 2014, it was announced FSC and Trinity agreed to double its combined equity commitment from $100 million to $200 million with the same proportional ownership. As such, FSC anticipated continuing to expand SLF JV 1 thus increasing this strategic advantage. As of 12/31/2014, SLF JV 1 had total investments of $265 million. Investments within SLF JV 1 include one-stop/unitranche and senior secured loans which should directly lead to strong returns for the fiscal second quarter of 2015. Furthermore, due to the benefits of this type of investment structure, FSC has stated the company is in active discussions with other third parties to create additional JV partnerships over the next several years. I believe these types of entities should be seen as a positive factor. Fourth, FSC has continued to reiterate the company does not anticipate issuing shares of common stock below CURRENT NAV for the foreseeable future. This notion has recently been confirmed when, on 3/18/2015, FSC held the company's 2015 Annual Meeting of Stockholders where there was no proposal for authorization to issue common stock at prices below NAV. I believe this is another positive factor (no dilution for existing shareholders over the foreseeable future). Fifth, the material decrease in the price of oil during FSC's fiscal first quarter of 2015 has caused "distress"/speculation to surround most of the oil and gas sector (including parts of the energy sector) regarding falling margins and potential struggling/bankrupt entities. While I would agree markets should have some sort of generalized "cause for concern" within the high-yield debt market, I also believe one needs to "drill down" to each BDC's debt and equity investments to see which portfolio companies could be affected by this event. As of 9/30/2014, FSC had 3.71% of the company's investment portfolio in the oil and gas sector (including certain investments in the energy sector which had "oil and gas" characteristics). As of 12/31/2014, only 1.77% of FSC's portfolio had debt and equity investments within the oil and gas sector. When compared to the nine other BDC peers I currently cover, this was one of the lower percentages at potential risk for investment depreciation. As of 12/31/2014, I calculated Apollo Investment Corp. (NASDAQ: AINV ), Main Street Capital Corp. (NYSE: MAIN ), Medley Capital Corp. (NYSE: MCC ), and Prospect Capital Corp. (NASDAQ: PSEC ) had an 18.61%, 6.39%, 6.81%, and 5.16% exposure to the oil and gas sector, respectively (again, including certain investments in the energy sector which had "oil and gas" characteristics). I believe FSC's recent low exposure to the oil and gas sector (percentage wise) should be seen as a positive factor. Now let us discuss several "cautionary"/negative factors. First, what was disappointing news to most investors (including myself) was the fact that FSC recorded material investment depreciation during the fiscal first quarter of 2015. This directly led to a material decrease in FSC's NAV as of 12/31/2014 when compared to 9/30/2014 ($9.17 per share versus $9.54 per share). Due to material depreciation within several of FSC's portfolio companies, management disclosed the company put four debt investments on "non-accrual" status during the fiscal first quarter of 2015. This directly led to less accrued interest and "payment-in-kind" ("PIK") income as recognized per Generally Accepted Accounting Principles ("GAAP"). I believe these specific portfolio companies, which include Transtrade Operators, Inc. (Transtrade), CCCG, LLC ("CCCG"), Phoenix Brands Merger Sub., LLC (Phoenix), and JTC Education, Inc. ("JTC"), experienced varying levels of investment depreciation during the fiscal second quarter of 2015. I believe this investment depreciation should be seen as a negative sign. Second, FSC's Board of Directors ("BoD") recently announced a material reduction to the company's monthly dividend per share rate. For the month of January 2015, FSC declared a dividend of $0.0917 per share. However, after declaring no monthly dividend for February 2015, FSC declared a monthly dividend rate of only $0.06 per share for the months of March - August 2015. As noted above, the new monthly dividend per share rate has a high probability of being maintained. However, even though FSC reported NII of $0.229 per share while reporting net investment company taxable income ("ICTI") of $0.239 per share for the fiscal first quarter of 2015, the company's BoD reduced dividends by (35%). In my professional opinion, this was an extremely cautious move. Yes, along with FSC's disappointing NII/net ICTI per share figures for the fiscal first quarter of 2015 grew the need to reduce the dividend. However, with that being said, I would have preferred to have seen a (10%) - (15%) dividend reduction with no special dividends versus a (35%) dividend reduction and the possibility of periodic special dividends. To get a better understanding of FSC's change in dividend policy, the following explanation was provided by Mr. Owens during FSC's earnings conference call for the fiscal first quarter of 2015 : "â€¦The decision to realign the dividend was driven by two things, the challenging operating environment and a broader change in our dividend policy. On the operating side we continue to execute on our initiatives to increase net investment income but the progress has been slower than expectedâ€¦On the policy side, as a newly installed management team at FSC, we believe that it's important to set our dividend at a level that is covered by our sustainable net investment income which should provide us a flexibility to make decisions that are in the best interest of our shareholders over the long term..." Simply put, I believe management assuming "sustainable net investment income" will be in the neighborhood of $0.18 per share should be seen as a cautionary/negative factor. In the future, I will continue to analyze FSC's results to see if the company's dividend per share rate can be sustained by matching distributions with NII and net ICTI. Third, prior to FSC's results for the fiscal first quarter of 2015, the company had a relatively stable annualized weighted average yield on its debt investments. FSC had an annualized weighted average yield on the company's debt investments of 12.0% as of 12/31/2012. This percentage decreased to only 11.1% as of 9/30/2014. When calculated, this was a weighted average yield decrease of only (0.9%) over the prior seven quarters. However, FSC's annualized weighted average yield on the company's debt investments was only 10.4% as of 12/31/2014. When calculated, this was a weighted average yield decrease of (0.7%) during the fiscal first quarter of 2015. For just one quarter, I believe this was a material decrease. Due to management's amended investment strategy focusing on a greater proportion of first lien senior secured floating-rate loans, I anticipate FSC's annualized weighted average yield on the company's debt investments to remain "flat" at best during the calendar year 2015. More likely, this yield percentage will slightly-modestly decrease as LIBOR continues to remain suppressed. I believe this should be seen as a cautionary factor.
