I would like to start by reviewing our fourth quarter financial performance. For the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2015 our net sales were 110.2 million an increase of 11.6% from 98.7 million for the prior year period. Net sales from our Nutritional Food segment which includes branded powdered formula as well as branded baby and adult food products, were 105.1 million, or 95.4% of net sales, in the fourth quarter. This represents an increase of 18.8% over the fourth quarter last year in which we recorded sales of 88.4 million, or 89.6% of net sales. This increase was primarily driven by a 12.6% increase in average selling price, supported by a 5.5% increase in milk formula sales by volume over the prior year period. Sales of powdered formula products increased to 7,356 tons in the fourth quarter, compared to 6,974 tons in the prior year period. Average selling price was 14,283 per ton, compared to 12,682 per ton in the prior year period. Net sales from the Nutritional Supplement segment were 4.6 million, or 4.1% of sales which was in line with 4.1 million in the prior year which also accounted for 4.1% of sales. This segment is primarily comprised of sales of chondroitin sulfate materials to international pharmaceutical companies. Net sales from the Other Business segments, which mainly consists of imported whole milk powder and whey protein powder sold to industrial customers, was 0.6 million, or 0.5% of net sales, compared to $6.2 million, or 6.3% of net sales, in the prior year period. These sales are always opportunistic as we sell excess raw milk powder and raw milk and raw whey powder that cannot be used in our formula products due to defecting color or consistency that make it not suitable to be put directly into an infant formula product under our dry mixing production method, but is otherwise fresh and safe to use for general food industries purposes. During the fourth quarter we started using infant-grade whole milk powder from Fonterra, which conforms to our dry mixing standard better method and thus contains smaller percentages that are unusable for infant formula dry mixed products. As such we have much less active milk powder for resale. Year-over-year unit production cost for our powdered formula products decreased by 10% as the price of whole milk powder used in production decreased by 45% and whole milk powder represents approximately 23% of overall cost. The decrease however was largely offset by an inventory provision for our imported super series of products which we launched in 2003 to meet the market demand for premium products. However we discontinued that product by the fourth quarter of calendar 2014 as the OEM supply of this product has experienced several disruptions and we were able to launch the advanced super series as our new premium product offering. Given this overall unit cost of goods sold for our IMS product remained flat year-over-year. Gross margin Nutritional Food segment however increased from 44.8% in the fourth quarter last year to 51.4% in this year due to higher ASPs. Gross profit was 54.2 million in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2015 compared to 38.2 million in the prior year period. Gross margin was 49.2% an increase from 38.7% in the prior year. This increase was primarily due to the increased contribution to total net sales from the nutritional food segment and supported by the increased gross margin of that segment.
