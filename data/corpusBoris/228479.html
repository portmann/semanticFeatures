Fiscal 2016 began with very strong revenue growth, closing of an attractive acquisition following the close of first quarter, and we finished September with a strong and higher margin backlog. The revenue growth this quarter was driven our ongoing Dover Air Force project. As we’ve noted previously, at over $98 million, this is a strategically important project for us, as we continue to broaden our portfolio capabilities. Because Dover is a construction project and we are the prime contractor, we subcontract much of the work adding a minor fee. As a result, our Dover contract contributes lower margin revenues as compare to the balance of work and during the quarter we saw the anticipated impact on overall gross margin performance. During the first quarter of our fiscal year, we are focused on bid and proposal activity as the U.S. government ended fiscal year. Due to such seasonality, our first quarter is typically our least profitable one. Additionally, during our first quarter we posted non-reoccurring expenses related to our acquisition of Johnson Controls Security Systems or JCSS. The acquisition closed just after the end of the quarter and is now operating as Versar Security Systems or VSS. This is an excellent acquisition for us, bringing new, higher margin, classified construction capabilities. We believe that our marketplace advantage lies in our ability to broaden and strengthen our portfolio of solutions and we expect to generate higher margins as we integrate VSS and grow this business. We remain focused on managing our expenses. And while SG&A on an absolute dollar basis was relatively flat this quarter, as a percentage of sales, SG&A expense decline to 6% as compared to 9% in the first quarter of fiscal 2015. In addition to executing our core business over the past several years, we have strategically acquired businesses that have allowed us to enhance our portfolio of capabilities and product offerings. Our strength and ability to deliver customizable solutions has resulted in more contracts won, a growing pipeline of opportunities in both the government and private sectors and significant backlog growth. As of October 1, 2015, our funded backlog was $225 million, a 26% increase compared to the funded backlog of $179 million at June 26, 2015. Our quarter closed on September 25, 2015 while significant contract activity was ongoing. So, always we provide our October 1st backlog because it includes funded awards received during the last five days of the government’s fiscal year. And additionally, the October 1st backlog this year includes projects from JCSS acquisition. During the first quarter, our SATOC joint venture with Johnson Controls Federal Systems was awarded $2.5 million contract from the Air Force Civil Engineer Center to provide construction services at Mountain Home Air Force Base in Idaho. Additionally, this week, we announced two new task orders with a combined value of $17 million from the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers Middle East district under our existing personal services contract in Afghanistan. With working capital of $22.6 million, we continue to prudently manage our balance sheet. Subsequent to the close of the quarter, we entered into a new credit facility with Bank of America that has both a term and a revolver components. This improvement to our debt and financing structure will allow us effectively manage our ongoing liquidity needs. I will now turn it over to our Executive Vice President and CFO, Cynthia Downes, to review our financial results for the first quarter of 2016. Thank you, Tony. Gross revenue for first quarter of fiscal 2016 grew 52% to $44.9 million as compared to $29.6 million in first quarter of fiscal 2015. The revenue increase was primarily related to our Dover Air Force base project. Gross margins for the first quarter of 2016 were 5% compared to 9% in the prior year. The margin decrease is primarily due to revenue mix related to lower margin we got from Dover project. Purchase service and materials for the first quarter increased significantly to $29.8 million, again as a result of our higher project costs primarily related to Dover. Direct costs of services and overhead decreased approximately 13% or $1.9 million related to our realignment within ESG and realized contract cost savings across all of our business segments. SG&A expense in the first quarter of fiscal 2016 increased to $2.9 million as compared to $2.7 million in the first quarter of fiscal 2015. Included in this number was approximately $200,000 of one-time expenses related to Company’s acquisition of JCSS. Additionally, as commented on earlier, as a percentage of sales, SG&A decreased to 6% of sales compared to 9% of sales in the first quarter of last year. The Company recorded an operating loss of $552,000 for the first quarter fiscal year 2016 compared to operating loss of 85,000 in the first quarter of the same prior fiscal year. Net loss in the quarter of 2016 was $431,000 or loss $0.04 per share as compared to a net loss of same quarter last year of 89,000 or loss of $0.01 per share. Net loss in the first quarter of fiscal 2016 included the $200,000 in one-time expenses related the Company’s acquisition of JCSS, mentioned earlier. Now focusing on operating results of our business segments for the first quarter of 2016. Engineering and construction management: In the first quarter of fiscal 2016, gross revenue was $13 million, an increase of a 130% compared to the first quarter of last year. This increase primarily resulted from a $18.5 million revenue from the Dover project offset by decreases in revenues from Title II work in Afghanistan and decreased revenue contribution from our PPS subsidiary in the UK. While we expect our Title 2 work for the U.S. Air Force to be completed in fiscal 2016, we continue to perform work on our personal services contract, U.S. Army Corps of Engineers in Afghanistan. First quarter margin in the segment was 4% compared to 12% last year. Margin decline in the segment because the majority of revenue contribution projects were some of the lower margin work in Dover project. Environmental services: First quarter revenue was relatively flat at $10 million compared to the first quarter of fiscal 2015. Gross profit for the segment was $665,000, an increase of more than 100% as compared to gross profit in first quarter of fiscal 2015. Gross margin in the segment was 7% compared to 3% last year. As the recent higher margin contract wins begin to ramp up in the second quarter, we expect to keep profit improvement. Gross revenue for the first quarter of fiscal 2016 was $4.8 million, a decrease of 23% compared to the first quarter of fiscal 2015. The Company continued to see a decline in contract positions to the shift towards more contracts towards to companies that qualify for small business program. The Company is focusing on strategies to partner with the small business, as currently transitioning from a prime contractor role to a subcontractor role on several of its recently [indiscernible] Army installations. Gross margin for the segment in the first quarter was 8% compared to 12% last year. The decrease is a result of the decline in revenue due to a loss of task orders and associated direct labor as detailed above. Thank you, Cynthia. Let me just clarify something.
