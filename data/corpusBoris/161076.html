3M: A Fortress To Weather The Storm - 3M Company (NYSE:MMM) | Seeking Alpha 3M is on sale due to concerns over global growth and customer de-stocking. However, the company is well positioned for the rebound. 3M has executed well with steady growth and margin expansion and its strong balance sheet will provide M&A opportunity. 3M (NYSE: MMM ) is a diversified technology company serving customers and communities with innovative products and services. The company operates its business through five segments: Industrial, Safety and Graphics, Electronics and Energy, Health Care and Consumer. 3M is a steady, low-single-digit revenue grower with high-single-digit EPS growth given its steady margin improvements. The company pays an above-market dividend and has an excellent balance sheet. The stock is trading at a discount to its 200-day moving average due to concerns over global growth. However, 3M stands to benefit given that it has significant flexibility to engage in M&A due to its rock-solid balance sheet. In this environment, cash is king, and the strong will come out stronger. Wall Street is too focused on near-term issues such as customers de-stocking products like abrasive and tapes. What will happen after an inventory correction? Customers will restock. MMM is currently trading at $142.14 per share, a market value of $88.80 billion, and an enterprise value of $92.99 billion. The stock's current dividend yield is 2.9%, 84 bps above the dividend yield for the S&P 500. MMM performed OK over the past year, returning 78 basis points relative to the S&P 500, or 1.2% in absolute return. The stock is trading 11.0% below its 200-day moving average and over the past 52 weeks have traded at a high of $170.50 and a low of $130.60 per share. Short interest is low at 1.6%. MMM's revenue grew by a CAGR of 2.4% over the past 3 full fiscal years. Sell-side consensus is forecasting revenues to contract by 3.1% this fiscal year, reaching $30.8 billion and to grow by 4.9% the following fiscal year, reaching $32.3 billion. Over the past three fiscal years, MMM's EBIT margin increased by 151 basis points from 20.9% to 22.4%. Street consensus is forecasting EBIT margin to expand by 86 basis points this fiscal year to 23.3%, and expand by 47 basis points the following fiscal year to 23.7%. Over the past 3 years, management decreased its diluted outstanding common shares by 10.7%. As a result of the revenue, margin, and share dynamics, EPS grew at a CAGR of 7.9% over the past 3 fiscal years. Going forward, consensus is forecasting EPS is to increase by 4.3% reaching $7.81 this fiscal year and to increase by 10.9% reaching $8.66 the following fiscal year. Profitability metrics are strong. ROA is 13.9%, ROE is 27.6%, and ROIC is 21.5%. The balance sheet looks solid. With net debt of $4.2 billion, MMM is 0.5 times levered to its trailing 12 month EBITDA of $8.4 billion. Debt to Equity is 34%, current ratio is 2.2 and Altman's Z Score is 5.3. However, expected FCF conversion from EBITDA this fiscal year is low at only 48.9%. Using consensus estimates for next fiscal year's results (FY2), MMM is currently trading at an EV/Sales multiple of 2.9, an EV/EBIT multiple of 12.1, a P/E multiple of 16.4, and a FCF multiple of 16.7. Relative to the S&P 500, MMM is trading at EV/Sales premium of 77.5%, an EV/EBIT discount of 7.3%, P/E premium of 10.0%, and FCF discount of 4.9%. MMM's PEG ratio is currently 1.9 compared to the S&P 500's PEG ratio of 1.4 - this is a premium of 35.2%. Focusing in on the key EV/EBIT, P/E, and FCF metrics, MMM deserves a 15.0% premium to the S&P 500 due to its solid execution, strong balance sheet and M&A potential. Applying such a premium to these valuation metrics implies a price target of $166.18 or a 16.9% upside potential from the current price.
