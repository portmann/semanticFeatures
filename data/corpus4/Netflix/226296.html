Disney posts lower-than-expected quarterly revenue | Reuters Disney posts lower-than-expected quarterly revenue Walt Disney Co reported lower-than-expected revenue for the fourth quarter and said it lost subscribers in some of its cable networks. Cable networks such as Disney have been hurt by consumers switching to online streaming services such as Netflix Inc and Hulu - a trend known as "cord-cutting". Disney's shares fell about 1 percent in extended trading on Thursday. The company's total revenue rose 9.1 percent to $13.51 billion, but missed the average analyst estimate of about $13.57 billion, according to Thomson Reuters I/B/E/S. However, revenue in Disney's media networks unit, which includes ESPN, rose 12 percent to $5.83 billion in the quarter ended Oct. 3. The business accounted for 43 percent of the company's total revenue. Disney's net income rose to $1.61 billion, or 95 cents per share, from $1.50 billion, or 86 cents per share, a year earlier. [nBw4rPyqDa] Excluding items, the company earned $1.20 per share, beating analysts' expectations of $1.14. Disney's shares were trading at $112.20 after the bell.
