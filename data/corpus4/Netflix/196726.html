European stocks just broke a one-month high and are poised for their best weekly gain since late January on renewed hopes central banks will keep monetary policy loose for longer. Overnight, Asian equities and currencies also marched higher following yesterday's gains on Wall Street (the Dow ended above 17K for the first time since August, while the S&P 500 closed well past its 50-day MA of 1,995). Oil prices are advancing into the $50s , pushing past a key level to record a gain of nearly 9% this week. Support appears to be coming from a variety of factors, including recent OPEC comments on tackling the global overhang, expectations of shrinking U.S. production and concerns about Russia's military operations in Syria. Crude futures +2.5% to $50.64/bbl, the commodity's highest level since July 22. Just days before the first Democratic debate , presidential candidate Hillary Clinton has published a series of proposals aimed at cracking down on Wall Street, pledging that individual bankers who violate the law will be "prosecuted and imprisoned." The plan would impose a graduated "risk fee" on large financial institutions and strengthen the Volcker Rule. A separate tax would be levied on high-frequency trading, targeting strategies that involve a large number of order cancellations, a practice her campaign says makes markets "less stable and less fair." Christine Lagarde has opened the door to a second five-year term as the head of the IMF, stating she is "prepared to serve" if the fund's shareholders keep her as managing director. "I'm certainly open to the fact that it could not be my last annual meeting," she declared. The former French finance minister's current five-year term ends in July 2016. Europe's "migrant crisis" could allow some countries wiggle room in terms of their budgetary commitments, Valdis Dombrovskis, European Commissioner for the Euro and Social Dialogue, told CNBC. The stability and growth pact's fiscal rules (such as budget deficits no greater than 3% of GDP) have to "be respected," but the laws could provide a certain amount of flexibility "in extraordinary circumstances." Eurozone countries are due to submit their draft budgets to the commission on October 15. Embattled mining giant Glencore is slashing its zinc production by a third in reaction to a 30% plunge in the commodity's price over the past few months. The company will cut 500K tonnes of zinc production, 4% of the world's total supply, including closing its Lady Loretta mine in Australia and Iscaycruz mine in Peru. The new tentative labor agreement between Fiat Chrysler (NYSE: FCAU ) and the United Auto Workers union will be one step closer to being ratified following the UAW Chrysler Council's meeting in Detroit today which will discuss the agreement and vote on it. The proposed four-year contract would increase maximum worker wages over time and eventually eliminate the much-maligned two-tier pay structure. Looking to reverse a two-year profit slide , Standard Chartered's ( OTCPK:SCBFF ) new Chief Executive Bill Winters plans to cut up to a quarter of the bank's most senior staff. According to an internal memo seen by Reuters, the layoffs will result in the loss of around 1,000 top jobs. Winters, who took over as CEO in June, said the bank had already reduced the number of employees and the other affected staff will be told by the end of November. Bill Gross is suing Pimco for at least $200M, claiming he was wrongfully pushed out of the company he founded by a "cabal" of managing directors seeking a bigger piece of the bonus pool. According to the complaint, Gross had been "on track" to receive a bonus topping $250M, with most of it to be paid late in the year. Pimco ( OTCQX:AZSEY ) announced his ouster on September 26, 2014. Combining two vaccine components from Crucell Holland and Janssen Pharmaceutical, Johnson & Johnson (NYSE: JNJ ) is beginning clinical trials of a preventive Ebola vaccine regimen in Sierra Leone. Is the Ebola outbreak finally over? For the first time since the disease was reported in March 2014, the World Health Organization reported no new cases over the past week. According to the WHO, this is part of a trend: The number of cases in West African countries has remained below 10 per week over the past three months, but that doesn't mean the virus can't surface again. A total of 11,297 are said to have died since the start of the epidemic. Danish transport company DSV is buying California-based logistics company UTi Worldwide (NASDAQ: UTIW ) for $1.35B. In a wide-ranging interview with Handelsblatt , Tesla (NASDAQ: TSLA ) CEO Elon Musk had some trash talk to unleash on Apple (NASDAQ: AAPL ). Netflix is hiking the price of its standard streaming service by $1/month to $9.99 for new customers in the U.S., Canada, and parts of Latin America. The streaming service rolled out a similar increase across its European territories in August, increasing the cost of its standard plan by a euro. Existing customers will have grace periods of varying lengths. NFLX ended 6.3% higher on the news yesterday. PC sales dropped sharply again in the third quarter. IDC estimates global PC shipments fell 10.8% Y/Y to 71M units, a drop nearly as large as Q2's 11.8%. However, the market's four biggest players all grabbed share from smaller firms with less scale. IDC calculates market leader Lenovo's ( OTCPK:LNVGY ) unit share rose 130 bps Y/Y to 21%, HP's (NYSE: HPQ ) increased 110 bps to 19.6%, Dell's jumped 120 bps to 14.3%, and Apple's ( AAPL ) climbed 60 bps to 7.5% . Walt Disney is making some moves in its long-term schedule, pushing its Toy Story sequel a year later and rearranging animation, live action and Marvel schedules along with setting an Ant-Man sequel. The news comes as Disney (NYSE: DIS ) says it's crossed the $4B mark at the global box office ($1.4B domestic, $2.6B international) for the third straight year, and nearly six weeks faster than in 2014.
