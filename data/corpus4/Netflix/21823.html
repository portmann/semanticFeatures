ETF Hub Apr 2 2015, 18:53 ET | By: Jason Aycock , SA News Editor Among a spate of original-content news at Netflix (NASDAQ: NFLX ), the company just announced that it's ordered a fourth season of its flagship drama House of Cards, to begin production this summer and air in 2016. The show, Netflix's highest-profile entry of what has been a rapidly growing slate of content, has won a number of Golden Globe and Primetime Emmy awards. The other "House" news trending among viewers of a certain age: Netflix is reportedly close to ordering a new season of Full House, the ABC family comedy that ended its run in 1995, to be called Fuller House. ("Just a rumor," says a Netflix representative for now.) More orders: a sketch-comedy show, With Bob and David, reuniting Mr. Show creators David Cross and Bob Odenkirk; and supernatural drama Montauk, from the creators of Wayward Pines. Just want to know how much Kevin Spacey has taken in this deal. Plus, the series is already getting a bit stale, not sure they can maintain a growing audience.
