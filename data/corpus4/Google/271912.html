Welcome to the Self-driving edition of M&A Daily. Google ( GOOG / GOOGL ) and Ford (NYSE: F ) are creating a JV to build self-driving cars. The announcement is being prepared for next month's Consumer Electronics Show. Former Ford CEO and Google board member Alan Mulally is one driving force behind the move. Google is also separating out its self-driving car business into a more autonomous unit early next year. Long term, they plan to offer rides for hire in competition with Uber (Pending: UBER ) and Lyft (Pending: LYFT ). John Krafcik, the head of Google's self-driving car business, is a fourteen-year Ford veteran who brought a number of other Ford people with him to Google, so the teams know each other well already. Krafcik is likely to be named head of the new JV. Western Refining (NYSE: WNR ) agreed to acquire Northern Tier (NYSE: NTI ) in a cash and stock deal valued around $2.4 billion. NTI unitholders get $15 in cash and 0.2986 WNR per NTI unit. NTI unitholders may elect to get either $26.06 in cash per unit or 0.7036 WNR subject to proration. The $2.44 net arbitrage spread offers a 19% annual return if the deal closes by next June. Bridgestone ( OTCPK:BRDCY ) is in the process of modifying its bid for Pep Boys (NYSE: PBY ), after the bid from Icahn (NASDAQ: IEP ) was deemed superior. Bridgestone Corp. will negotiate with Pep Boys on a bid this week. ValueAct entered into forward transactions in Baker Hughes (NYSE: BHI ) for 10.5 million shares from December 2nd-December 21st. The net arbitrage spread is currently $12.32.
