ETF Hub Oct 12 2015, 09:50 ET | By: Yoel Minkoff , SA News Editor Back from the dead? India's low-cost carrier SpiceJet is making money again and plans to order up to 250 aircraft by the end of March. The airline is in advanced discussions with Boeing ( BA ) and Airbus ( OTCPK:EADSY -3.2%) for passenger jets and is talking to Avions de Transport Regional, Bombardier (( OTCQX:BDRAF ), ( OTCQX:BDRBF +1.6%)) and Embraer ( ERJ -1.2%) for turboprop planes. The move comes as SpiceJet looks to up its presence across India and ride expected air travel growth in Asia's third-largest economy.
