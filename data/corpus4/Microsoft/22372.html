11.21% Advantage To 5 Dow Highest Yield, Lowest Price, April Dogs With Merck | Seeking Alpha Apr. 5, 2015 2:13 AM ET  |  Includes: CAT , CVX , GE , KO , MCD , MRK , PFE , PG , T , VZ , XOM by: Fredrik Arnold Top ten Dow Industrial dividend stocks by yield showed 11.21% higher net gain from a $5k investment in the lowest priced five than from the same investment in all ten. Annual dividends estimated by Yahoo.com divided by closing stock prices as of April 2 determined yields. Analyst net target prices were used to calculate gains one year out. The five lowest priced high yield Dow stocks were: GE; PFE; KO; VZ; and MRK. Five higher priced stocks completing the Dow Yahoo top ten were: CAT; PG; XOM; MCD; and CVX. This article refines and distinguishes an earlier report that revealed Dow Industrial 30 bargain stocks to buy and hold one year. See Dow 30 article for explanation of the term "dogs" for stocks reported based on Michael B. O'Higgins book "Beating The Dow" (HarperCollins, 1991), now named Dogs of the Dow . O'Higgins system works to find bargains in any collection of dividend paying stocks. Utilizing analyst price upside estimates expanded the stock universe to include popular growth equities, if desired. Yield (dividend / price) results from Yahoo.com for 30 Dow industrial stocks as of market closing prices April 2 did the ranking. Top ten Dow dogs by yield from Yahoo.com Finance after March included seven of nine business sectors: technology; basic materials; industrial goods; services; conglomerates; healthcare; and consumer goods. (Financials were excluded and Dow rates Utilities in a separate index.) The Yahoo Finance healthcare firm, Merck & Co., Inc. (NYSE: MRK ) in the top ten, instead of Microsoft. The surviving technology telecom firm showed the best dividend yields according to Yahoo.com: Verizon (NYSE: VZ ) [1] following the removal of AT&T (NYSE: T ) from the list as of market close March 16. One of two dogs in basic materials placed second, Chevron Corp. (NYSE: CVX ) [2]. The other basic materials outfit was sixth, Exxon Mobil Corp. (NYSE: XOM ) [6]. A single industrial goods firm placed third, General Electric (NYSE: GE ) [3]. A single services representative was fourth, McDonald's (NYSE: MCD ) [4]. The lone conglomerate representative placed fifth, Caterpillar (NYSE: CAT ) [5]. One healthcare firm ranked seventh per Yahoo: Pfizer (NYSE: PFE ) [7]. Yahoo seeded eighth place to Coca-Cola Co. (NYSE: KO ) [8]. Finally, Yahoo put a healthcare giant in ninth place, Merck & Co. [9]. Then, Yahoo seeded tenth place to Coca-Cola Co. [10] to complete their March top ten Dow dividend dog list. (2) 10.13% Net Gains by All Ten by March 4, 2016 $5000 invested as $1k in each of the five Lowest priced stocks in the top ten Dow dividend kennel by yield were predicted by analyst 1-year targets to deliver 11.21% more net gain than the same amount invested in all ten. The very lowest priced Dow dividend dog, General Electric, was projected to deliver the best net gain of 15.96%. Lowest priced five Dow dividend dogs for April 2 were: General Electric; Pfizer Inc.; Coca Cola Co.; Verizon Communications; and Merck & Co., with prices ranging from $24.94 to $57.10. Higher priced five Dow dividend dogs for April 2 were: Caterpillar Inc.; Procter & Gamble (NYSE: PG ); Exxon Mobil Corp.; McDonald's Corp.; Chevron Corp. whose prices ranged from $80.24 to $105.28. This distinction between the five low priced dividend dogs and the general field of ten reflected Michael B. O'Higgins "basic method" for beating the Dow. The scale of projected gains based on analyst targets added a unique element of "market sentiment" driving upside potential. It provided a here and now equivalent of waiting a year to find out what could happen in the market. Analysts were historically 20% to 80% accurate on the direction of change and about 0% to 20% accurate on the degree of the change.
