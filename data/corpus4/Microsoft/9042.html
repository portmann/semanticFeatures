That is, just sitting at my desk, reading filings and believing I understood the business. But today, I'm going to look at the flipside. When it's okay to be an armchair investor. With many small cap stocks, you need to put in the extra effort because there isn't as much information available. Many small caps are under-followed, misunderstood, had solvable issues and cheap. These are the stocks where you can get One Up On Wall Street. But for blue chips, it's never under followed and rarely misunderstood. The minute something happens, you will be one of the last to know. If you have special insight, that's called insider trading. Otherwise, it's already been analyzed, scrutinized and argued to death. That's why when I invest in a company like Apple (NASDAQ: AAPL ) or Microsoft (NASDAQ: MSFT ), I don't bother trying to predict what the next product will be. Who cares what screen size the iPhone will be.... This is one of the times where it's okay to be an armchair investor. It's better than spending days or weeks trying to find information about whether sapphire crystal or Corning glass is better. After all, does it really matter? When investment articles talk about things like the future of 4K screens and how Apple will be the leader, I take it as noise because it's not just Apple that will benefit. The entire industry will get a lift from such technology.
