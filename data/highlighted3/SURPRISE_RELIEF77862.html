(Surprise & Uncertainty) The "final" privilege logs provided to Fairholme are not seen to contain confidential information and are the exact same types of information in earlier privilege logs.This sort of behavior appears to be last ditch effort type stuff on behalf of the government, and if the government loses, private shareholders win big.Fannie Mae (OTCQB: FNMA ) and Freddie Mac (OTCQB: FMCC ) are government sponsored housing businesses that are privately owned and are currently in conservatorship where for the time being all of the earnings are seen as United States Tax Revenue.There are three courts that have active cases in them, DC District, Court of Claims, and Iowa.The DC District court case is on appeal.The discussion below relates directly to the Court of Claims, where it would appear that the government thinks that they can make Fairholme lawyers look bad by engaging in Bush League tactics.Judge Sweeney, a few months ago had the following exchange with Mr. Schwind, a government lawyer: THE COURT: Well, then, if you don't know what it is, then you're just making it up as you go along.MR. SCHWIND: We're definitely not, Your Honor.And - THE COURT: Well, of course you are.You're saying you don't know what they've asked for, you don't know what's been produced.Oh, by the way, but it simply goes to merits, and it can't possibly help them with respect to their jurisdictional defenses or their jurisdictional allegations.In the plaintiff's most recent filing , as a shareholder I get to celebrate that the government has finally provided to them final privilege logs!The battle has gotten intense lately and the judge just ruled in favor of the government's recent motion.What is interesting to me is that the Government seems to be trying so hard to make Fairholme lawyers look bad.The government also appears to have alleged that the Plaintiffs have, by stating publicly what is already public information that "the Government has asserted privilege with respect to thousands of documents" constitutes "a breach of the Court's protective order".Judge Sweeney has made great quotes on this type of behavior in the past that just goes to show you that the government's defense continues to be meritless.THE COURT: And so far, I haven't gotten - I haven't received a good answer from the Government.Counsel is very able.But counsel has expressed concern of what could happen if certain documents are released, which I do not want to see happen, but counsel didn't answer to my satisfaction the discrepancy between sort of using the deliberative process as sword and shield.On one hand, FHFA is a government entity, you know, for purposes of booting the Plaintiffs out of court and not part of the Government, but for purposes of forwarding discovery, all of a sudden deliberative process is appropriate because they are part of the Government.So, it's a schizophrenic approach and I'm just waiting to hear a reasonable explanation.The new Saxton v.
