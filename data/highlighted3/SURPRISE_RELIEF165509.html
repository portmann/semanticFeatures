Matrix Service Company: From Humility To Confidence - Matrix Service Company (NASDAQ:MTRX) | Seeking Alpha Matrix Service Company reported fiscal 2015 full-year results on August 31st.The report closes the chapter on a highly challenging year for the company.Fiscal 2015 derailed track records of double-digit revenue growth and 20+% EPS growth established previously by the company.The culprit was an EPC joint venture project inherited with an acquisition.Looking toward 2016, the company expects to get back on track.As well, Matrix is not shy about proclaiming its intention to pursue acquisitions in fiscal 2016.In December 2013, Matrix Service Company (NASDAQ: MTRX ) acquired Kvaerner's North American Construction segment.The acquisition brought with it an EPC (engineering, procurement, construction) joint venture project under the Electrical Infrastructure segment that demolished the previous Matrix track records.Before fiscal 2015, Matrix had a five-year performance of double-digit growth in revenue and a three-year streak of annual growth over 20% in EPS.Then came four quarters of missed expectations primarily due to the EPC JV project, since identified as the Calpine Garrison Energy Center.When the company offered fiscal-year 2016 guidance in July 2015, the company expected to return to its prior EPS track and to double-digit revenue growth.The company reported full-year results for fiscal 2015 on August 31st.The fourth quarter revenue increased year over year by 7.6% to $370.5 million.However, this missed analysts' average expectation of $378.5 million.On the other hand, analysts expected EPS of $0.27 in the fourth quarter.The company delivered $0.40.For the full year, revenue tallied $1.35 billion and EPS was $0.63.The company starts fiscal 2016 with a record-high backlog of $1.41 billion.This helps validate the company's projections of $1.4 billion to $1.6 billion in 2016 revenue.At the midpoint of $1.5 billion, the company's revenue growth returns to a double-digit clip.Earnings per share is projected in a range of $1.45 to $1.75.Considering the Calpine EPC project had a negative impact of $0.67 per share in fiscal 2015, it is more appropriate to compare the 2016 projection to $1.30 (2015 actual of $0.63 plus impact of $0.67).Using the midpoint of $1.60, it appears Matrix Service Company should return to its previous growth rate of 20+% for EPS.The company's discussion in July, as it forecast fiscal 2016, was focused on acquisitions.With over $79 million in cash, only $8.8 million in long-term debt obligation, and available borrowing capability of over $95 million, the company is well positioned to be on the hunt.During the fourth quarter earnings call , Matrix CEO, John Hewitt, confirmed: Acquisitions are not the only purchases Matrix Service is willing to make.Matrix put $5 million in cash toward repurchasing shares in the 2015 fiscal fourth quarter while the stock price was depressed.In June 2014, Matrix's share price topped $35.It saw a steady decline with the Kvaerner EPC project challenges.It wasn't until the fiscal 2016 guidance was offered that the share price broke $20.It is difficult to grant any company the benefit of a high multiple during the recent volatility.Yet, with Matrix's return to double-digit growth, it is certainly reasonable to grant Matrix a multiple of at least 15.At a midpoint of $1.60 for 2016 estimated EPS, a minimal value for Matrix is at least $24.00.Still, the company has not broken through that mark yet.Considering the fiscal 2016 EPS midpoint represents growth of 23% over an adjusted-for-negative-impact fiscal 2015 EPS of $1.30, a multiple above 15 would not be absurd.It's probably too soon to expect the share price to break the $35 peak.But, as Matrix Service Company reproves it can achieve double-digit growth, the company will likely approach $30.Further, it is highly probable the company will succeed with its acquisition plans.Matrix Service Company uses the terminology of "bolt-on" acquisitions to describe its preference.By that term, the company is targeting either geographic expansion, specialty services, or additional product support.It would not be impractical for shareholders to wonder whether Matrix could potentially again inherit and face challenges similar to the ones inherited on the Calpine EPC project from the Kvaerner acquisition . (Surprise & Uncertainty) In the fiscal 2015 second quarter earnings call, Matrix management provided some clarity: "We reviewed the estimate.We did site visits.We reviewed the schedule.We analyzed who our joint venture partners were going to be and the client.So we were, at that time, fairly comfortable with the project.It was only -- the project at the time of the diligence, was approximately 17% complete,.The construction being less than that and the other portions of the project being more.But on average, it was only 17% complete.Frankly, we thought that the estimate was, while it was aggressive, was doable.But the other members of the joint venture, the owner, because they were providing some key pieces of equipment, they all had to do their jobs.As we know, we would do ours.And so, in an EPC arrangement, in this case, for this project, which is -- EPC, for those who don't know that, that's engineering, procurement and construction, Matrix is providing the construction services and our partners and our client are providing engineering and procurement services.So when pieces of those engineering and procurement break down, as they have on this project, it makes the construction piece of the job that much more difficult.So anyway, so we -- there was a full diligence on the project.We appreciated the risk associated with it as well as the rest of the organization.And the acquisition of the company took into account those associated risks and benefits, obviously, that we saw in this acquisition.So again, this is, like I said, is a lump-sum project.We are today approximately 89% complete.We are beginning commissioning and startup of the project.We've got a commercial completion date and obligation to the owner by the end of our fiscal year of 2015.And, sometimes, when you get into the commissioning and turnover of these projects is when some of the issues related to the technical aspects of the job begin to raise their head and create complexities for the contractor to pull all the pieces together.And so that's kind of where we are related to this project."Could Matrix Service end up in the same predicament again?It is possible but not likely probable.There should be opportunities to forego such challenges in the future based on lessons learned on the Calpine Garrison Energy Center.To gain confidence in whether the lessons were indeed learned, backtracking through the closing remarks of the fiscal 2015 quarterly earnings calls seems to show a humility and then a regaining of confidence.Q1 : "We expect to be the best at everything we do.So when you have a goal to be the best as we do, you have to set very high expectations for yourself.And we have confidence in our ability to achieve those expectations."Q2: "The performance in the balance of the business is on track with our expectations and we remain excited about our position in the marketplace."Q3 : "Bottom line, our market position is as strong as
