(Uncertainty) Most of the Netflix (NASDAQ: NFLX ) bulls with whom I have spoken justify the currently sky-high valuation by pointing out that - as a market disruptor with seemingly little competition - Netflix has enormous growth potential both domestically and abroad.Thankfully, as a subscription-based product, Netflix's revenue growth is actually quite simple to model - so let's put the proverbial pen to the paper and find out by modeling growth over the next five years.Netflix justifies its $40 billion valuation through growth, and what will the next five years look like?Let's find out.Given that Netflix managed $1.57 billion in revenue last quarter, we can infer that the average subscriber pays $8.99/month.(61.93 million total subscribers * 8.99 per month 3 months per quarter = roughly 1.6 billion). (Uncertainty) As previously mentioned, Netflix currently has 41.4 million U.S. subscribers paying, on average, $8.99 per month.Given that the population of the United States is 319 million (Source: United States Census Bureau) and that there are 123 million households in the U.S. (source: Statista.com ), it can be said that around 1 in 7 people in the U.S. currently have a Netflix subscription, or 1 in 3 households.With 1 in 3 households already having a subscription, it is difficult to argue against potential market saturation, but let's be optimistic here and assume Netflix is capable of growing that figure to 1 in 2. (Uncertainty) Assuming the population grows by 5% in that time, this would result in Netflix having 64.6 million U.S. subscribers. (Uncertainty) (Source: The Prosperity Active Yield Fund) Let's also assume Netflix is capable of doing this while also raising the average monthly price by 45% to $12.99. (Uncertainty) This would result in 2020 domestic subscriber-based revenue of $10.07 billion.Estimating ex-U.S. Growth Potential: As previously mentioned, Netflix is currently available in 79 countries, excluding the United States.Here 's a list of those countries : Using data provided by Google Public Data Explorer , I was able to calculate that the aggregate population of those countries is roughly 965 million . (Uncertainty) Therefore, 1 in 18 (5.5%) of all ex-U.S. households that have access to Netflix currently are subscribers.Let's again be optimistic here and assume that - by 2020 - Netflix is capable of doubling that percentage. (Uncertainty) What's more, let's assume this is possible while simultaneously raising the average subscription to $12.99 per month and expanding into large markets like China, Russia and India. (Uncertainty) This expansion would double the potential ex-U.S. subscriber base from 965 million to 1.9 billion by 2020, giving Netflix 82.5 million annual ex-U.S. subscribers.If true, this would result in $12.86 billion in ex-U.S. annual revenue.Combined Optimistic 2020 Growth Potential: The purpose of this exercise has been to quantify the growth potential that Netflix bulls are so quick to point out whenever questions of valuation are raised.
