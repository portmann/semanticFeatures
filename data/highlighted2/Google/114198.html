Disney , like Google , or like Apple ( NASDAQ : AAPL ) , is highly liquid . It's practically a form of currency in its own right.(Don't you wish you'd shorted Apple a week ago?)If you believe in the pair trade, you might want to try putting Disney up against Comcast, which it resembles in many ways. (Uncertainty) Disney has been rising in part on optimism about its movie franchises. (Uncertainty) Comcast has a movie coming out today called "Minions," testing whether a spin-off cartoon of "Despicable Me" has legs on its own.Comcast has been fairly innovative in marketing this movie, essentially sticking the characters into others' ads, which delivers more bang for the buck.They've even bought Amazon.com (NASDAQ: AMZN ) boxes.If Minions is a hit, I'll bet Comcast stock gets a bounce.The problem with shorting a popular stock is that you might have to wait an unreasonably long time to be proven right. (Uncertainty) In this case, shorting around a bad movie would seem to make sense.But Disney has already sold some dogs this year, including Tomorrowland , McFarland USA and Monkey Kingdom .Maybe you think Ant Man or The Jungle Book could be another John Carter , but even that dog did well internationally, and you may find yourself short when the next Star Wars comes out. (Uncertainty) So if you really fear what's ahead for Disney in the short term, sell what you have and walk away. (Uncertainty) Hope for another entry point that's lower down the road.
