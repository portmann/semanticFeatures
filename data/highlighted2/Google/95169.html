Amazon launched an electronic payment service to replace eBays NASDAQ EBAY PayPal and to gain additional revenues from transactions However while PayPal is widely used across the web and is a synonym for secure online payment Amazon Payments remain primarily within the Amazon turf and is hardly used in other sites as a secure payment processor Unlike eBay that leveraged PayPal to become an industry leader Amazon Payment lags behind PayPal Stripe and Braintree as shown in Chart below Last year Amazon expanded its payments portfolio and launched Amazon Local Register an inperson pointofsale service that allows merchants to accept credit cards in their brickandmortar stores Amazon Local Register competes with Square and PayPal Here and completes Amazons comprehensive offering for merchants that includes an ecommerce marketplace payment processing service and instore credit cards transactions Amazon suffers from the inherent problem that merchants consider Amazon as their rival and dont rush to do business with the company Unlike Square Stripe or PayPal which are perceived as standalone independent players that dont compete with the local merchants many retailers consider Amazons ecommerce platform as a risk to their business Amazon doesnt reveal how many transactions are paid using Amazon Payments but based on the fact that its not raising any questions and Inc This subsidiary owns Amazons Payment service Amazon Checkout and Amazon Local Register quite an impressive portfolio that can be easily compared to PayPal Theoretically PayPal which is owned by eBay for now could have faced the same problems as Amazon Payments however unlike Amazon Payments merchants are not intimidated by PayPal as they dont consider it a threat to their business PayPal is branded and perceived as a standalone payments company and not as an eBay subsidiary Amazon should copy that model and rebrand Amazon Payments without the Amazon to something else lets call it ZonPay
