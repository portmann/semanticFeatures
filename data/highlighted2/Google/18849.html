categorized as an operating expense and not a capital expense But wait a minute Didnt Google just announce its superduper cloud storage called Nearline And isnt it supposed to sink Amazons Glacier the equates to retrieval at MB per second and TB yields a MB rate That sounds just dandy But wait In realtime application its not so hot If my company has TB of information stored on Nearline my download to hours Moreover I can retrieve up to of my data stored on Glacier for free Googles Nearline costs me one cent per GB along with one cent per month for storage per GB In other words Nearline picks my ways The final insult offered by Nearline is that if I overwrite or change my data then Nearline considers this an early deletion and hits me with an additional fee That means Nearline is similar to
