Google 's Nearline service , which offers 1GB of storage for $ 0.01 , is just the latest development in this spiral . The money lies in the services and applications that companies can offer on top of these storage platforms.Enterprise IoT is a particularly nice opportunity for Amazon (and other cloud providers), because it requires a large stack of platforms to work fully.Enterprises need to connect their devices and machines together, which is where 2lemetry's technology comes into play.They need a way to store all of the resulting data, which is where AWS storage is key (this will likely one day be provided as a free value-add).Then, enterprises need a way to sort and analyze all this data in order to help them make business decisions (the original promise of IoT).Amazon will now be able to directly offer two of these three services in an integrated offering.And while it doesn't currently offer a true business intelligence product, it does provide the infrastructure for some of the most popular ones.When assessing the viability and market position of AWS, investors should consider Amazon's IoT services an integral component of that strategy.2lemetry's platform allows Amazon to establish a foothold in the growing enterprise IoT market, which can be leveraged to later sell additional AWS services.In this way, the acquisition really isn't about expanding into a new vertical, but rather, strengthening the company's current offerings.In next few weeks, I'll be writing more about the opportunities that IoT (both consumer and business-facing) presents for new and established companies.This will include a larger overview of the general IoT landscape that assesses which established
