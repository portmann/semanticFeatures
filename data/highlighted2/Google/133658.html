profitability Judging by Qualcomms NASDAQ QCOM recent earnings call both Apple and Samsung are considered dominant in the upper tier of the smartphone market This is arguably due to the greater level of vertical integration that these companies have achieved relative to their competition Both companies design and engineer their own smartphones and tablets Both companies go beyond this to design and engineer their own systems on chip SOCs that power these devices Custom SOCs constitute an important discriminator for both Apple and Samsung mobile devices Beyond custom SOCs the vertical integrations of the two companies diverge in important ways Samsung has long maintained its own semiconductor fabrication capability and builds its own SOCs Apple is dependent on outside foundries GOOG NASDAQ GOOGL Android for its smartphones and tablets Does the competitive advantage conferred by Samsungs foundry capability outweigh the competitive advantage of Apples selfdeveloped iOS The table above clearly indicates that it does not The mobile device operating system is the key to the user experience The mobile OS brings together all the fundamental elements of the modern mobile device ecosystem including apps services and entertainment content Samsungs dependence on Google for Android is in many ways a carryover of the commodity PC paradigm The OS developer for commodity computing devices develops a generic OS and then throws it over the fence to the hardware makers The resultant user experience is less than optimal In the PC world users put up with it because it lowered the cost of owning a PC Obviously thats still true in the commodity mobile device market but the usability penalties for the approach are more severe Partly this has to do with the greater variety of mobile device hardware platforms Partly this has to do with the technical challenges of tinkering with a mobile device operating system Most mobile device users never tinker with either their hardware or OS but simply live with what theyve bought In assessing Apples competitive position going forward the advantage conferred by iOS is often overlooked Apple critics continue to maintain that Android or Windows Phone is just as good as iOS If that were really true surely Samsung would be in a much better position than it is Part of the problem is really not the fault of Android as a mobile OS but is due to the structural relationship between Google and its manufacturing partners To some extent Google siphons off profit from the manufacturers in the form of Android advertising and services revenue Google keeps the profit from Google Play for instance and Samsung gets nothing If we accept that Apples control of iOS provides it a competitive advantage then this says something very important about Apples longterm prospects in mobile devices This is because the situation with respect to Android is unlikely to change The current Android device makers are unlikely to successfully develop and deploy their own operating systems or even switch to an alternative Android device makers have made Windows Phone devices but these hardly constitute a viable alternative to Android in terms of sales volume The device makers are pretty much stuck with Android for the foreseeable future This means that no manufacturer in the Android ecosystem has a path
