of an Altera NASDAQ ALTR and Intel NASDAQ INTC merger are a little unsettling so I decided to do additional research on the topic as I believe the two companies could have some synergies Admittedly I dont know everything about field programmable gate arrays other than the fact that theyre very useful and reprogrammable via a hardware design language or HDL for short Theres a decent likelihood that these reports are accurate however the upside from such a deal isnt exactly clear Intel is in the CPU and GPU business and it tends to stay away from FPGAs The addition of FPGAs gives the combined company a separate lineup of ICs to market and sell Admittedly developing the kind of capabilities inhouse would cost a lot of money and would take a lot of time Hence Intel seems willing to pay a premium to buy out Altera On the one hand the deal on the table sounds pretty generous Despite the generous offer Altera had refused the deal earlier in the year according to earlier reports This has resulted in quite a bit of controversy especially from institutional investors who wanted the immediate exit plan Intel Corp is in talks to buy fellow chipmaker Altera Corp in a deal likely to top billion according to a source familiar with the matter making it Intels biggest purchase ever and the latest merger in the quickly consolidating semiconductor sector The acquisition of Altera which makes programmable chips widely used in cellphone towers the military and other industrial applications would underscore Intel Chief Executive Officer Brian Krzanichs determination to expand into new markets as the personal computer industry loses steam The deal could be sensible for Intel shareholders While it will take a while to make the deal accretive theres potential to create value postacquisition Intel would obviously have to explain to shareholders how it will go about accomplishing this But in the meantime Im going to try and make a couple educated guesses Alteras FPGAs tend to win designs at the older process nodes So my guess is that the cost to performance is maximized on older manufacturing technology which is why Intel is so worked up over the deal because it gives some of its older fabs higher utilization rates This will lead to better rates of profitability due to higher gross margins So Intel could potentially increase the gross margin and operating margins of Altera It also may have a way of bundling FPGAs with its serverclass CPUs so it gives enterprise users more customizability with their data centers Admittedly these are all very hypothetical
