robust growth rates in the Data Center Group Internet of Things Group and NAND businesses which we expect to offset the decline in the Client Computing Group Intel anticipates that its growth will be flat yearoveryear and will offset the negative growth from client computing group with NAND data center and Internet of things Clearly AMD has limited exposure to mobile IoT NAND and data centers so I find AMDs reasoning of back half growth pretty erroneous In fact AMD is almost nonexistent in servers and its semicustom business is on the frontend of a downward cycle due to falling ASPs for its APUs in console gaming devices I anticipate console gaming devices to slow in growth as competing consumer electronics will tighten the available discretionary spending for premium gaming devices Also portable gaming devices are a rapidly shrinking market due to the rapid emergence and adoption of smartphone devices AMD wont find niche opportunities worth expanding into with its current portfolio of semicustom designs I cant imagine a scenario where AMD will generate enough volume from smaller markets to offset the decline in pricing and volume it has experienced in consumer CPUs and GPUs Also the company forecasts an increase in gross margins it plans to accomplish this with a mixshift to premium products in servers and workstation graphics However it has been shown that both Intel and Nvidia NASDAQ NVDA have developed superior enterprise grade hardware solutions Intel plans to launch another generation of Xeon series chips pretty soon And needless to say Nvidia has plans of improving its architecture to better utilize Direct X which is projected to improve frame rates per second by a considerable amount going into calendar year AMD plans to improve its platform efficiency which is likely to be driven by improvements in the instruction per cycle and improvement in manufacturing process It certainly helps that GlobalFoundries and Samsung OTC SSNLF are working in conjunction to deliver nm technologies The combined scale of a joint fab gives AMD a reasonable shot at reasonable yields assuming Global Foundries can meet the requirements of AMDs wafer supply agreement The improvement in performance from Zen to Zen might not be as significant as the performance gain from Excavator to Zen Much of the performance gains from improving CPU architecture already occurred between Zen and Excavator due to simultaneous multithreading which is similar to Intels hyper threading So its more likely that Zen based on the naming cadence is just a die shrink of the preexisting architecture Intel wasnt able to accomplish significant IPC gains from Haswell to Broadwell with a simple die shrink but was able to squeeze in some power efficiency gains due to duty cycling control Skylake will directly confront Zen on a more advanced node with superior architecture when working under the assumption of a second half launch Early reports on Intels upcoming lineup of CPUs are extremely promising AMD may hold onto some of the PC market with Zen and Zen AMD certainly is not dead and its update on the roadmap is pretty helpful for investors and speculators However
