Qualcomm Faces Wall Of Doubts Qualcomm Inc NASDAQQCOM Seeking Alpha Qualcomms first quarter has generated doubts about the company It faces challenges in acquisitions in China in its dealings with Apple and Intel Most of the folks writing here at Seeking Alpha about Qualcomm NASDAQ QCOM remain bullish on the stock but the wall of doubt surrounding the company is a real one Over the last year the shares are down and the most recent quarterly report was filled with red flags Net income fell by nearly half from the same period a year ago to billion cents per share against almost billion per share in Profit margins were down operating margins were down and so was operating cash flow Revenue fortunately was up at billion against nearly billion in the yearago period and the bulls attributed it to writing down old assets and a onetime gain the previous year from the sale of its Omnitracs unit Still the storm clouds gathered Qualcomm is a fabless company with Intel NASDAQ INTC actually making its chips and some analysts warned this necessary agreement may constrain Qualcomms ability to acquire other companies Its main customer Apple NASDAQ AAPL is getting more aggressive in cutting off royalty payments for old technology and analysts worry about that Its policy of share buybacks is under criticism and its decision to sell some spectrum assets in the United Kingdom is also seen as bearish Then theres China Qualcomm is highly dependent on China China represents about half its revenue The companys respected head of China operations Wang Xiang just got hired away by phone maker Xiaiomi forcing the company to bring back Frank Meng China recently had to pay a fine of million over anticompetitive practices there and to lower its royalty rate going forward Qualcomm was one of the few tech companies to take Taiwans CompuTex show seriously this year announcing plans to make WiFi faster advance the Internet of Things its display featured watches as well as tablets and to make the PC obsolete But all this was not enough for some who insist theyre dumping Qualcomm in favor of Intel Thats a little like closing the barn door after the horse is out since Intel is up over the last year while Qualcomm stock has struggled The solution acquisitions could be an expensive one Buy Skyworks Separate chips from licensing as activist investor Jana Partners wants Or why not make a run at MediaTek the Taiwanese company that has been taking share from it Such a move would also give Qualcomm an alternate foundry partner in Taiwan Semiconductor NYSE TSM The point is its a complicated chess board perhaps the most fraught since Steven Mollonkopf stepped into the CEO chair in March Its not an easy call but Mollonkopfs compensation is nearly all in stock so he is bound to
