bit of advantage over Samsungs However there are news in the architecture front as well AMD has long lagged Intel in that regard with outdated and rebadged CPUs being particularly famous for their low IPCs Instructions Per Cycle AMD tried compensating for this by adding cores and using higher frequencies but the truth is that AMDs offerings simply havent been competitive for years However the Zen core besides using a process node which will match Intels will also be changing AMDs course on IPC AMD promises a higher IPC for it A cuttingedge process node plus a large improvement in architecture akin to Intels Hyperthreading I think theres a good chance that even with the improvements AMD wont surpass Intel However theres also a good chance that the fight will again become close enough instead of Intel keeping its presently massive lead Close enough might mean AMD will be able to compete on value Which it really cant at present While the story I described above should give AMD a chance during there is still an entire desert to cross during where AMD has little if any competitive product in the market Even in GPUs where the fight with nVidia NASDAQ NVDA used to be close AMD is now also just playing catch up with its Fury Fury X GPUs These GPUs match nVidias best But they dont surpass them even with their innovative HBM stacked indie memory promising much higher bandwidth
