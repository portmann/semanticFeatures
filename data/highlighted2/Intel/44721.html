Why Now May Be The Time To Buy Intel Intel Corporation NASDAQINTC Seeking Alpha Intel still has a belowmarket earnings multiple despite a sharp rise during CEO Brian Krzanich is fixing the problems with OEMs that bedeviled predecessors I used to own Intel NASDAQ INTC but Ive been down on it for years now Their problems in mobile Chinese OEMs preferring anything to Intel chips were obvious The team of insiders Brian Krzanich and Renee James brought in to fix things seemed timid I questioned the stocks momentum last year First the Internet of Things which I wrote about over a decade ago is going to explode demand for chips of all kinds Even before Krzanich became CEO the company was moving firmly into lower and lower power designs to cheaper and cheaper chips which the IoT demands Second I was impressed by James efforts to cater to the OEMs It wasnt just her speech at Computex last year but the companys followthrough that I found impressive Third despite last years runup in the share price theyre still cheap as chips Youre paying times earnings for a company that can take in every it brings in to the bottom line Despite an incredibly capital intensive manufacturing process barely in every of capital is subject to debt The company delivers a steady and rising dividend which at current prices yields How can you not like that Of all the four companies that still make chips Samsung OTCSSNLF Taiwan Semiconductor NYSE TSM and privatelyheld Global Foundries are the others Intel has done the best over the last year Its not depending entirely on its own branded product for sales but it is looking for opportunities there I thought for years Intel should split the foundries from the design given what I call Moores Second Law which is that manufacturing costs rise in line with chip complexity I now believe that when that time comes Intel will know it and if Krzanich sees the necessity I believe he will do it Its time for me to shut up about it The latest rearrangement of the deck chairs under Mike Bell focuses all of the companys research on new products with an aim at shortening the time between discovery and OEMs delivering product It tells me that the company understands the limits of its own brand name and that Krzanich understands his is primarily an ingredient brand Finally there remains a lot of bearish sentiment around the stock The company is deliberately downplaying the near term which makes sense given the strength of the dollar The company seems at last to have its eyes wide open concerning the broader economy no longer relying on both Moores Law and its own reputation to carry it forward I made a lot of money on Intel during the s as its stock split repeatedly and kept gaining This is not the same Intel This is now an income stock
