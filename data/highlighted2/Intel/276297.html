As Hardware Becomes Software Intel Goes Shopping Intel Corporation NASDAQINTC Seeking Alpha The billion Altera acquisition makes Intels strategy clear Intel will try to buy the best design concepts while they are young to fill its fabs Under CEO Brian Krzanich Intel NASDAQ INTC has found a new way to adopt to the requirement of Moores Second Law Instead of using its fabrication plant to drive chip designs it is using chip designs to fill its fabrication plants Its an historic reversal made possible and necessary by the increasingly high capital costs of making semiconductors Moores Law holds that chips can pack more circuits on the same piece of silicon every year or so Moores Second Law is the corollary the fact that each new generation of chipmaking equipment also costs that much more to make As a result there are fewer and fewer players in the capital intensive chip production game and its imperative that those factories be kept humming with contracts from fabless chip designers Of course doing that only gives you a tiny piece of the designers profit Thus the new strategy buy the best designers those whose concepts can produce profitable products for years to come Altera makes FPGAs Field Programmable Gate Arrays Its one of the big new trends in chip design and a big key to the Internet of Things The chip isnt just waiting for software it contains software and the software it contains defines its function Intel will pair Alteras FGPAs with its Xeon microprocessors to create complete highlycomplex products like security systems in data centers or processing systems in airplanes But thats not the big story here The big story is that Intel is now on the hunt for other similar acquisitions It has already bought itself the numberone position in the booming Internet of Things market You can expect it to buy its way into other markets as well like mobile All this will place further strain on Intels oncepristine balance sheet but that was inevitable due to the rising costs of building and maintaining fabrication plants My view for years has been that Intel needs to separate the manufacturing part of the company from the design or software part but Intel has chosen a different path buying up the designs it needs to keep the fabs operating while those designs are on the leading edge of their growth path What this means to me is that Intel is going to become less a technology and more a mergersandacquisitions story going forward The most important events of its year wont be its developer conferences or trade shows showing off products with Intel inside but press releases where it announces new acquisitions It can work but Intels roadmap needs to identify the right targets it has to acquire those targets while they remain relatively small and
