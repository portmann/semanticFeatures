I consider owning Netflix or Amazon primarily a momentum trade while Google and Facebook are better seen as growth investments . Amazon has been taking the long view on reinvesting in its business for 20 years: It could keep spending on more warehouses and drones forever and never get around to directly returning cash to shareholders. (Uncertainty) Amazon Web Services could be a great business, but I don't know how large a portion of Amazon's revenue it could ever become. (Uncertainty) Netflix could continue ramping up its content creation budget , but may not be able to replicate the extraordinary string of hits it has had so far . (Netflix: Uncertainty) These companies might continue to prioritize those investments over putting cash directly in shareholders' pockets for the foreseeable future. (Uncertainty) I wonder if investors will someday take the same cynical approach with these companies they've had with Google and drop their valuations.
