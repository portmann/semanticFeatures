through the Jones academic paper the two main factors that really stood out was the underlying characteristic of the stocks that were split and the lag time for analysts looking to adjust their outward growth models at a bigger outstanding share base These two factors imply that over the shortrun market pricing can be inefficient because analysts are slow to adjust financial models and over the long haul stock splits are a reaction to overwhelming momentum in the stock My guess is that while the share split does little to do add or negate value from a financial context it does add confirmation to the fact that top performing stocks tend to experience stock splits So if we average the performance of stocks that split shares the next months tend to be pretty good So as long as the underlying fundamentals that drove the stock price higher continue to unfold Well in the case of Netflix growth is starting to accelerate In an age where Internet bandwidth is becoming increasingly more abundant and at lower cost the advantages to OTT in comparison to linear television broadcast is driven by cost Furthermore the increasing base of subscribers makes it substantially easier for Netflix to fund its own inhouse content or buy content from thirdparty producers In other words Netflix can have just as much scale as the linear TV providers which has a circular effect of furthering its own economic advantage in comparison to lower scale providers that have to charge more Also foreign markets have lower barriers of entry due to the falling cost of bandwidth which expands the addressable market Given the expansion of Netflix into foreign markets and improving profitability domestically one can argue that Netflix is a growth stock and that the underlying
