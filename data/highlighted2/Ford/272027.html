Ford recalls cars for lighting failures Reuters Related Regulatory News Breakingviews Cars are lined up for sale at a Ford dealer in Lakewood Colorado in this September file photo WASHINGTON Ford Motor Co said on Tuesday it will recall older cars for headlight failures linked to crashes as well as vans pickups and midsized trucks for assorted issues The automaker said it is recalling Ford Crown Victoria and Mercury Grand Marquis vehicles to replace the lighting control module The modules solder joints could crack and interrupt power to the headlights increasing the risk of crash at night The Ford Crown Victoria was often used as a police vehicle or taxis before the automaker phased it out in The US National Highway Traffic Safety Administration opened a new investigation of more than Ford Crown Victoria and Mercury Grand Marquis cars in August after reviewing more than complaints of lighting failures and reports of crashes and one injury The agency previously investigated the same lighting issue in without demanding a recall after finding no reports of crashes linked to the issue NHTSA said some drivers were able to turn the lights back on while some said lights flickered before turning off completely The recall covers cars in the United States in Canada and in Mexico Dealers will replace the lighting control module Ford separately issued three other small recalls covering about vehicles It is recalling Ford F and F trucks to replace the rear air brake chamber due to a defect that could cause a leak and inadvertent application of the parking brake or diminished braking performance Ford is also recalling Ford Transit dualrearwheel vehicles to replace rear axle shafts which could crack and cause a loss of power or unintended vehicle movement when the vehicle is in park without the parking brake engaged The automaker is calling back Ford F SuperCrew vehicles to replace the front safety belt retractor and pretensioner assembly which might not restrain an
