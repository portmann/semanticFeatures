Ford Market Dislocation Creates Extraordinary Opportunity Ford Motor Company NYSEF Seeking Alpha Ford is being pummeled because of fears about China But at just a fraction of sales China doesnt matter nearly as much as investors think it does Over the last few trading days like most any other stock in the US Ford NYSE F has been absolutely pummeled The culprit has been a purported slowdown in China and a commensurate crash in its stock markets Of course the company is involved in selling vehicles in China and investors have indiscriminately sold Ford shares as a result But Im here to challenge the reason behind the selloff as a way to justify a much higher valuation for the company As a note all data for this article was extracted from Fords most recent Q Below is a chart included in the Q that shows the AsiaPacific region for Ford which of course includes China We can see that revenue in Q was billion and that as operating margins rose so did pretax profits One could assume that since China is slowing down some of that revenue and profit is now at risk and while that conceptually makes sense I think the fears are overblown Does a stock market crash mean people will stop buying cars Perhaps it will on the margin but for people who need a vehicle they have to buy something The same fear was present during the financial crisis here which proved to be overblown and while I agree that Ford needs to be revalued Id argue the revaluation should have been a fraction of what weve seen AsiaPacific AP provided billion in revenue during Q which is just of Fords total revenue for the quarter In addition AP provided a tiny fraction of operating profits That means that even if the company were to just exit China right now the maximum drawdown should have been or so If we assume Q and Q will be rough in China even if we assume a volume reduction the operating loss would still be moderate in comparison with Fords total operating profits North America provided billion in operating profits in Q in comparison to the million AP provided so while a slowdown in China could be painful the idea that the stock should lose a third of its value or more is ridiculous and reeks of panic to me The thing is that China gets a lot of press and attention from investors in Ford but there is no reason for that to be the case Why do we care if the company loses or bps of revenue and a fraction of its operating profits I dont want a slowdown any more than the next Ford long but the reaction to Chinas slowdown has been asinine I believe this market dislocation has created a fantastic opportunity in Ford because when it reports Q results were likely to see a small drop in operating profits in the AP region and perhaps then investors will understand just how cheap the stock is once more The companys forward earnings multiple of is likely lower than what it will actually end up being but even if we write off China Ford is still in the range or so in terms of forward earnings That sounds pretty cheap to me The fact is that Ford has always been and will always be a North America story and that region is plenty healthy Sales numbers continue to favor Ford for the long term despite a noisy but nearly meaningless slowdown in AP Investors dumping shares at and change because of China dont understand the companys business and I think well see the stock higher into
