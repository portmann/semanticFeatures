sales going strong major automakers are returning capital to shareholders through multiple avenues In a previous article I discussed the capital return plans from General Motors NYSE GM and here I will discuss the plans from Ford NYSE F Auto investors are well aware of Fords position as the only member of the Detroit Three not to go through reorganization during the financial crisis But prior to the crisis the automaker took action to revamp itself including selling off Volvo Land Rover and Jaguar to raise cash Ford also has the bestselling pickup truck in the US the Ford F however sales of the F lag the combined sales of the Chevrolet Silverado and GMC Sierra For the model year the F gained an aluminum body helping to shave off weight boosting performance and fuel economy While there have been some concerns over the move to aluminum Fords August sales numbers looked encouraging for the F remodel Alongside its automotive side Ford also has a financial side Ford Motor Credit that extends credit to Ford buyers Worth noting is that this part carries the vast majority of Fords total debt with much of it being backed by vehicles themselves This makes for a significantly safer investment than its debt to equity ratio would imply Unlike GM with its billion share buyback Ford has not launched a stock buyback to reduce outstanding shares Last year Ford launched and completed a billion share buyback however this was only to balance out dilution from share based compensation and convertible notes In its annual report Ford noted that it had completed the buyback and gave no indication of launching a new buyback While Ford has mostly avoided share buybacks in recent years it has made major efforts to return capital to shareholders via dividends Currently the quarterly per billion buyback after being pressured by activist investors Similar action is less likely to be successful at Ford due to the power of the Ford family in the voting structure Although I would like the company to add a buyback the dividend still makes the capital return plan a positive one overall Fortunately for investors significant earnings growth is expected with EPS expected to more than double pursue debt reduction While I do believe that less debt could earn the stock a higher valuation I also believe the company should be selective in which debt it targets High interest debt is worth eliminating but the company has better uses for cash than eliminating low interest debt Its also important to note that since shares have a dividend yield Ford could reduce ongoing cash commitments by repurchasing shares Despite the low current valuation and large dividend Ford shares continue to remain volatile and this will likely keep happening as broader market turbulence continues Fords exposure to the cyclical auto industry could impact its ability to paygrow dividends in the future so investors should be willing to assume some risk before adding Ford to their portfolio Ford has focused almost exclusively on dividends over buybacks making it better positioned for income investors However the lack of a buyback misses an opportunity for management to reduce the share count at this valuation as well as saving on dividends from having fewer outstanding dividend paying shares Despite the lack of a buyback Ford is still a worthwhile investment for dividend value investors to
