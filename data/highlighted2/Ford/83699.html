Angeles California June LOS ANGELES Civil rights activists called on Wednesday for criminal charges against one of two Los Angeles police officers in the shooting death of an unarmed black man after the citys police commission found the officer had violated department policy The August shooting death of yearold Ezell Ford who a family lawyer says suffered from bipolar disorder and schizophrenia led to sporadic protests in the city and increased tension between police and some members of the AfricanAmerican community Police officials say officers Sharlton Wampler and Antonio Villegas who are Asian and Latino respectively shot Ford after he struggled with Wampler and tried to grab his holstered gun Community activists with the National Action Network an organization founded by civil rights leader Al Sharpton said at a news conference they wanted Wampler who the police commission on Tuesday found most at fault in the shooting to be criminally charged We do have confidence somewhat in the system said KW Tulloss western regional director of the National Action Network Now were asking the district attorney to do the right thing Some protesters have called for a murder charge in the case On Wednesday District Attorney Jackie Lacey in a statement said the case is under review The focus of the review is to determine whether the filing of criminal charges is warranted not whether LAPD internal policies were violated Lacey said The District Attorneys Office has not charged any Los Angeles police officer over a shooting in more than a decade The police commission on Tuesday found Wampler violated department policy in drawing his gun and using lethal force and that Villegas mostly adhered to policy but should not have drawn his gun early in the altercation That finding contrasted with Police Chief Charlie Becks review finding both Wampler and Villegas followed department policy The commissions ruling sends the matter back to Beck who must decide what discipline if any the officers deserve Minnie HadleyHempstead president of the Los Angeles chapter of the NAACP civil rights group said in a phone interview she believed the district attorney could have reason to bring charges but she stressed that first Beck should discipline the two officers I think they immediately should be removed from the department she said The Los Angeles Police Protective League the
