company I have seen many discussions about how Ford cannot afford to make share repurchases That the company is too heavily laden with debt and it would put the company at financial risk The first point concerning this is that I believe the CFO may understand the financial position of the company he works for so I dont feel that he would make a comment like this one if it were not a possibility discuss a share repurchase program a little I have seen many comments concerning share repurchases and some of them seem to me to be misleading Lots of comments state that repurchasing shares is just financial manipulation and doesnt provide benefit to the company and its growth The first thing I want to mention was that as an investor you purchase a share of stock You own that share of stock You are willing to pay a price for that share according to the earnings that are attached to it times a multiple this multiple has many other factors that weigh into it such as growth industry etc If a company has earnings of and it has two shareholders then each share in essence has attached to it If we have the same scenario only now there are shareholders instead of two there is in essence attached to each share As an investor the share that has attached to it is much more valuable than the share that only has and you as a logical investor would be willing to pay much more for it So when a company chooses to repurchase shares it is directly making the shares you own more valuable With fewer shares claiming the earnings of the company your share now has more earnings attached to it It is not just financial manipulation but it does directly increase the value of your share Now it is true that the share repurchase does not boost the profitability of the company and its operations Ford is not going to sell more cars because it issued a share repurchase program However it does boost the profitability of the share that you own That is the question that you need to ask as investor What is the value of a share After all that is what you are buying When a company issues a share repurchase program it is taking cash away that it could have used to invest back into the business It is just a matter of how much the company can get in return if it invests back into the business and how much cash it has available If it has plenty of cash to continue to fund the growing business there is no point in just funneling money into itself if it is not getting a good return on that invested capital Different companies have different policies and different growth rates If a company is growing its business by a large percentage year over year and has lots of room to keep growing then I as an investor would want it to continue to use its cash to grow its business not pay a dividend or repurchase shares This is why it is common for example to see young companies not pay a dividend or repurchase shares as they are continually using that capital to grow the business operations Companies that are more established and have slower growth are more likely to pay a dividend and return profits to investors There is also an example like Apple OTCAPPL where despite the fact that the company has continued to grow at a fast rate it has enough excess cash that it continues to pay a dividend and repurchase shares Apple could choose not to pay a dividend and not repurchase shares and instead use the cash to invest in the growth of the business but the company already has sufficient cash to fund its continued growth and rather than throwing the extra cash at projects that will not give a high return it returns the cash to shareholders through a dividend or a repurchase When we look at Ford we see the same thing The company could continue to spend all of its extra cash on upgrades to plants RD on new features for cars etc but is that going to provide the best return for the shareholder That is the question a company has to ask and figure out What is the best use of the capital that it has and what will provide the best return to shareholders A share repurchase program and a dividend are doing the same thing Both are ways for the business to take capital and return it to shareholders The dividend as well as the share repurchase program take capital out of the business that it could have used to invest in the growth of the companys operations and give it back to shareholders So if you dislike the idea of a share repurchase program you might equally dislike the idea of a dividend There are of course distinct advantages that both a dividend and a share repurchase program have and depending on the stock and its valuation one might be a much better option than the other but they are in essence performing the same function just in a different way So when I read a comment that says a company should increase its dividend but that a share repurchase program is just financial manipulation and a waste of capital I tend to disregard that comment If you think repurchasing shares is a waste of capital then more likely than not you should agree that paying the dividend is a waste of capital as well Analysis of a Share Repurchase
