Company insiders usually executives board members or influential stockholders often have superior information about the companys financial condition sales prospects etc so insider transactions like the one mentioned here are watched closely because investors like to see how insiders evaluate their businesses In theory and practice insider ownership is a very good thing for shareholders Having skin in the game aligns managements interests with the interests of shareholders and properly incentivizes management to do the best job it possibly can to create value for the owners of the company Logically selling shares as a company insider is often construed as an unwelcome and unfortunate event However this shouldnt be the case here First insider sale transactions take place regularly to some extent because insiders have legitimate financialpersonal reasons to sell stock Second the insider in question here Leitch owns shares of Ford Motor worth approximately million after the latest transaction was executed Third the transaction volume is relatively small when put into relation to Fords market valuation Fourth Fords executives have regularly sold shares in the past so I wouldnt characterize the latest transaction in any form as unusual Bottom line The latest insider sale has at least for me no implication at all with respect to how I see Ford Motor as a value investment Ford Motors chart setup is improving After rebounding and recapturing the mark in June helped by a Goldman Sachs upgrade the stock is trading in the lowmid range to gather strength for another push north In the absence of any shortterm market dislocations or negative earnings surprises with respect to the second fiscal quarter Ford Motor surely has potential to move higher from here Insider transactions can give investors valuable clues as to how executives evaluate their company But things must be put into context The latest insider transaction is not unusual and the low transaction volume should not make anyone anxious Now if the CEO dumped all of his stock suddenly at week highs that would be another story But the reported transaction should not be construed as a negative vote
