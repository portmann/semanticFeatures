(Surprise) Due for an entry to service date sometime in 2019 , the A321LR has been hailed by Airbus as having the longest range of any narrow-body jet . (Uncertainty) From an aerodynamic point of view , for a given cabin width , the aircraft would be subjected to less parasitic drag if an oval cross-section fuselage is chosen . (Surprise & Uncertainty) In light of this enhanced cabin width , it is expected that the aircraft would be a twin-aisle offering , possibly seating passengers in a 2-3-2 configuration in economy and 1-2-1 or 2-2-2 in premium cabins . (Uncertainty) It is speculated that the MOM aircraft would have an aluminium body with composite wings , similar to the 777X . (Uncertainty) The MOM programme is however expected to be not as technologically ambitious as the 787 Dreamliner , on which entry to service suffered delays of more than 3 years due to glitches plaguing its lineup of cutting-edge technologies .
