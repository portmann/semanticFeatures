Having the CSeries finally validated with a major order by an airline like Delta should put an end to , or at least reduce , the sort of insane discounts airlines like United received - something that should be good for Boeing shareholders going forward . Focusing on replacing the 757 aircraft and making the 737 more technologically competitive against the CSeries and the C919 seem like better things for Boeing to do.Playing the price game will not lead the company down a very positive path, especially when it is doing so against the C919 in the future, as COMAC could be willing to absorb large losses to get a foothold in the market. (Uncertainty) I therefore believe that any sell-off in Boeing shares as a result of a loss to Bombardier would be a good buying opportunity.
