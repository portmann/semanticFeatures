US Supreme Court agrees to hear Microsoft appeal in Xbox case Reuters Microsoft Xbox gaming console units are shown for sale at a Microsoft retail store in San Diego January WASHINGTON The US Supreme Court on Friday agreed to consider Microsoft Corps MSFTO bid to fend off class action claims by Xbox owners who contend that the videogame console has a design defect that causes game discs to be gouged The court will review a March ruling by the th US Circuit Court of Appeals on the question of whether a lower court decision denying class certification to the plaintiffs could be challenged Class action lawsuits can lead to larger damages or broader remedies than individual lawsuits that can be costly to pursue Microsoft has sold tens of millions of Xbox consoles since their launch But owners have claimed that the consoles optical disc drive cannot withstand even small vibrations They said this causes game discs to spin out of control and become scratched even under normal playing conditions rendering them unplayable Microsoft said class certification was improper because just percent of Xbox owners reported disc scratches and that misuse was the cause In US District Judge Ricardo Martinez in Seattle dismissed the class action claims He relied on a ruling in a similar case in which another judge said the dearth of complaints ruled out class certification Whats Next
