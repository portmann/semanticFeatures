investors could benefit from the evolving business Breakdown to Virtual Reality and Augmented Reality For a long while the virtual and augmented reality concepts were assigned together into one market with similar characteristics and dynamics Both tech ideas were put together because they are naturally adjacent to each other and the same software and hardware vendors have developed solutions for both technologies So it was easier to assign them together However now that we have much more information about the market the dynamics between the different players and the solutions that are expected to launch we can break down the VRAR into two separate markets with different solutions challenges and catalysts In the virtual reality market there is a large number of vendors developing VR headsets that are expected to be available next year The most prominent ones are the Samsung OTCSSNLF Gear VR the Sony NYSE SNE Morpheus the HTC OTCHTCXF Vive and the Facebook NASDAQ FB Oculus Rift which also powers Microsofts NASDAQ MSFT Windows XBOX VR Headset For now virtual reality is mainly targeted at the gaming community as most companies revealed a long list of games that will be compatible with their devices In the augmented reality market or as it is sometimes called smart glasses there are currently only two prominent players Google NASDAQ GOOG NASDAQ GOOGL with the next generation Glass and Microsoft Hololense Both solutions offer users enhanced onthego features such as pairing their devices with a smartphone to receive calls text messages emails etc directly on their headset Also it will provide augmented search capabilities healthcare and fitness features realtime detailed GPSbased information on items within sight and a headmounted camera Currently it seems that Google and Microsoft will struggle to win over the VR market with their AndroidWindows OS that will allow them to expand their ecosystem into the gamingfocused VR market By offering a dedicated Android VR OS Google could generate revenues from VR games purchases on the Play Store while Microsoft could strengthen its Xbox and Windows ecosystem by allowing Xboxs users to stream VR games from their console to their Oculus Rift headset Microsofts announcement of the Oculus partnership might come as a surprise to some however as Microsoft is mainly focused on its AR solution it makes sense to partner with Oculus It provides an excellent solution that will attract gamers and allow Oculus to focus on its bigger challenge the AR headset The company is starting to look like the undisputed market leader of VR as it provides the VR solution for Microsoft and Samsung in addition to its own solution which is expected to start shipping in On top of the three companies mentioned above one cannot describe the VR market without mentioning Sony Morpheus which is a VR headset that provides VR capabilities to Sony PlayStation which is similar to the OculusXbox experience Oculus and Morpheus are battling for console dominance in the VR world As mentioned above there are currently two main players in the augmented reality market that could control the majority of the market in the future Google and Microsoft However they offer slightly different solutions Google Glass offers a nextgeneration smart device that will pair with users smartphones and enable them to catch messages onthego and enjoy the benefits of augmented reality content Microsoft offers a Windows based solution that provides users with the same smartphone interaction features as Google Glass however Holoense is expected to provide advanced holographic features on top of the augmented reality content Microsoft Hololense will allow consumers to stream content from Windows devices including Xbox which will allow dedicated games to use the holographic feature in Hololense Currently there are small vendors like GoldenI EyeTap and others who do not seem able to compete with Google and Microsoft
