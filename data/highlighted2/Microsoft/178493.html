take a page from Microsofts book and return a greater percentage of its free cash flow even if it has to issue more debt Both Apple NASDAQ AAPL and Microsoft NASDAQ MSFT have excellent balance sheets stuffed with cash have strong fundamentals and reward their shareholders with rising dividends and share buybacks But in one key area Apple falls short of Microsoft Microsoft is much more aggressive than Apple in returning cash as a percentage of its free cash flow Microsoft provides a much higher dividend yield and also much higher dividend growth than Apple For an income investor who might be choosing between the two heres which one may be the better stock to buy for dividends Both Apple and Microsoft include a mix of dividends and share repurchases in their capital allocation programs The difference between them is that Apple is more conservative when it comes to returning cash to shareholders The company prefers to retain more free cash flow and keep a greater portion of its cash on hand presumably to invest back into the business in new products whereas Microsoft returns more of its free cash flow to shareholders Over the first three quarters of its current fiscal year Apple five years Microsoft has increased its dividend by compounded annually including its recent dividend raise Since Apple instituted its current dividend program in late it has increased its dividend by compounded annually That is a respectable number but again falls short of Microsoft Microsoft isnt afraid of raising debt to return a greater portion of cash to shareholders and nor should it be While some investors shudder at the prospect of a company taking on debt its a financially sound strategy right now Thats because interest rates remain extremely low and Microsoft can take advantage of this by issuing cheap debt and can create value in excess of the cost For example Microsoft trades for times its adjusted earnings per share in fiscal That represents a earnings yield In addition the companys dividend stands in excess of By taking out debt and using the proceeds to repurchase shares and increase dividends the returns are greater than the cost of the debt being issued None of this has jeopardized Microsofts balance sheet In fact Microsoft is one of only three US companies to hold the AAA credit rating from Standard Poors It makes sense to leverage its excellent balance sheet strength to create additional value Microsoft has been able to retain its topnotch credit rating even though it returns more to shareholders than it generates in free cash flow and utilizes debt to make up the difference It stands to reason that Apple would not endanger its own credit rating by pursuing a more aggressive capital return program And its worth noting that investors have not punished Microsoft for its more aggressive capital returns Far from it if anything Microsofts stock has benefited from its significantly higher dividend yield and stock buybacks as a percentage of
