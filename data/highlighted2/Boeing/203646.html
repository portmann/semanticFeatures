Boeing
Boeing says it now expects China will need 6,330 aircraft over the next two decades which will be worth an estimated $ 950 billion . Boeing made its prediction based on its expectation for a tripling of China 's aircraft fleet between now and 2034 . (Boeing: Uncertainty) One of the big drivers for the new demand is an expected boom in budget airlines.China's first such carrier, Spring Airlines, has done quite well in the market and held a successful IPO last year that looks like a good long-term bet.Several other companies also have recently launched, including 9 Air, and others are in the process of being formed.Next there 's Smithfield , whose CFO is saying the company 's exports to China soared 45 percent in the first half of the year , even as growing signs emerged of a broader economic slowdown ( English article ) . (Surprise) This particular story is one of consolidation since Smithfield 's gains followed the departure of many smaller pork farmers from the market due to stiff competition that has undercut their ability to operate profitably . (Surprise) This particular story also is related to Smithfield's purchase two years ago by WH Group, formerly called Shuanghui.That acquisition carried a price tag of $4.7 billion, making it the largest such purchase of a US company by a Chinese buyer.Part of the logic of the deal was for WH Group to use its own local connections to boost the export of Smithfield's higher-quality products to China, which is what's happening now.At the end of the day , the Boeing and Smithfield stories both bode well for China 's broader private sector - especially more mature companies that are profitable and do n't depend on big state-run banks for funding .
