After Farnborough Seeking Alpha To be honest there was a lot of action Judging by the trade press the recent Farnborough air show was quiet and unexciting As an attendee I can assure you that despite torrential rain that shut the event down on Monday afternoon and then the rain Tuesday and Wednesday there was a lot going on The highly visible deals aircraft orders were indeed less prominent this year compared to Paris last year or the previous Farnborough show But the news that emerged was very important Air Asia ordered Aneos This was described as necessary because of growing airport congestion in Asia where the smaller A was not able to move enough people per flight One might overlook an order for aircraft But at the message is being shouted Airport congestion is real and growing This news links to the decision by Airbus OTCPKEADSF to slow its A production due to slow orders If airport congestion is a thing then the need for larger aircraft like the A is real Despite slow sales now Airbus needs to keep the A line going just like Boeing NYSE BA has kept the line ticking over The market demand will catch up and the A is far from dead The news that Boeing has modified its MAX is important By keeping the MAX wing and making the MAX a bit smaller to be called the MAX is very clever Boeing is focused on standardizing its production This drives down costs by pushing economies of scale The is a best seller and the MAX its successor also is selling better than any other MAX model By focusing on the core MAX and tweaking it Boeing essentially will get three models out of one MAX MAX and MAX This provides Boeing with an ability to blanket the Airbus Aneo offering It should allow Boeing to thrash Airbus on pricing as it will probably have lower production costs what was happening in the halls at the show Not on the runway In the halls were a host of companies that were showing off clever ways to help drive down production costs and speed production Additive
