Price is not the only challenge facing Boeing 's MOM . As Aspire Aviation repeatedly pointed out ( '' Boeing NSA/MOM launch timing hinges on 737 MAX progress , '' 19 Mar , 15 ) , the technology out there to bring single-aisle economics to a small twin simply does not exist today . (Boeing: Uncertainty) A 2-class small twin-aisle aircraft only has a 5.4 % lower block fuel burn on a 1,500 nm sector and yields a 9.43 % reduction in COC per seat against 2 A320ceos , with each A320neo offering a 15 % lower block fuel burn and a 14 % reduction in COC per seat . (Surprise) Should out-of-autoclave (OoA) carbon fibre reinforce polymer (CFRP), rather than aluminium lithium, be adopted in an attempt to overcome this insurmountable technological challenge, the production cost of the MOM will inevitably rise, further compounding the aforementioned price premium problem.In conclusion, 2015 is shaping up to be a momentous year for the commercial aerospace sector, with the A320neo entering into service in December after its 2 PW1100G-JM-powered test aircraft resumed flying, A380 and 787 breaking even, and Airbus's upcoming decision to launch the slightly stretched A380neo.For Boeing , trimming 787 costs is proving to be more complicated than simply reducing the use of titanium , whereas its middle-of-market ( MOM ) void will have to be addressed sooner , rather than later , amid mounting pressure exerted by the A320neo family , and the A321neo in particular , with the launch timing continuing to move to the left ( '' Boeing eyes 2018 launch for New Small Airplane '' , 26th Nov , 14 ) .
