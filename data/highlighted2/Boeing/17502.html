Typically a high return on equity value is pretty nice to have, but not all returns on equity are equal.Although it is a straight ratio as suggested , it is a bit more complex than that . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Not many investors know this, but return on equity can actually be decomposed into three parts, made popular by DuPont back in the 1920s.The decomposition of return on equity tells us three things: Operating efficiency, which is measured by profit margin Asset use efficiency, which is measured by total asset turnover Financial leverage, which is measured by the equity multiplier Prepare yourself for some algebra; I promise it will be pretty easy.We already know that profit margins are dictated by the equation of profit/sales.Total asset turnover is dictated by the equation of sales/assets and the equity multiplier is dictated by the equation of assets/equity.So when we multiply them all together we get the following proof: I for one am looking at return on equity in a much different manner now after learning this method.The part of the proof above that got me to investigate ROE more intently is the equity multiplier portion . (Surprise) Depending on how a company chooses to finance its assets ( by debt or by equity ) , the equity multiplier can be really huge , causing the overall return on equity to be high . (Uncertainty) Personally I don't like a whole lot of debt on the balance sheet.But for capital structuring reasons debt can be used as a tax shield as taxes are calculated after interest payments.I've chosen to evaluate Disney (NYSE: DIS ) today because since I've been writing about the company the return on equity has been increasing with the following profile.As mentioned earlier , operating efficiency is the profit divided by the sales . (Surprise) This in essence tells us how efficiently the company is operating from an operations, financial, and tax perspective.From the table below we see that the profit margin increased by 2.29% for Disney from the fourth quarter to the first quarter.We see that net income has increased and sales increased over the period but at a lower clip . (Surprise) Nonetheless, profit margins increased which means the increase in ROE was partially due to the increasing profit margin.From the equation I showed above, the total asset turnover ratio is defined by sales divided by assets.Total asset turnover gives us the big picture of how well the company is transforming all of its assets into sales.From the table below we see that the total asset turnover actually increased from the fourth quarter to the first quarter.Total sales increased slightly while average assets increased at a lower clip . (Surprise) This portion of the equation tells us that the ROE increase was not really related to Disney 's ability to make more money on their assets because the increase was negligible . (Surprise) The equity multiplier is the part of the ROE equation that I don't like.It 's with this portion of the equation that a company can choose to get risky or play it safe . (Uncertainty) It completely depends on how the company manages its debt , equity , and on how well the cash flows are coming in to pay those debts .
