Boeing
For Q1 , Boeing spent $ 3.5 billion on share buybacks as the stock collapsed . The fact the airplane manufacturer spent so aggressively during a weak period is a positive sign that management is in tune with the true value of the stock based on the prospects.The net payout yield (dividend yield plus net stock buyback yield) has soared to 12.2%.Note this number is very difficult to squeeze above 10% without the stock being very cheap though the number is never full proof.Sure the order level is hitting a trough in 2016 , but even the orders this year are approximately keeping up with deliveries . (Uncertainty) The reality is that the numbers aren't indicating whether orders are low due to extended delivery date options or any sign of a lull in future demand.Boeing remains a solid buy in the low $ 130s and offers shareholders a solid 3.3 % dividend yield to offset the volatile stock price . (Boeing: Uncertainty) At this point , the risks appear very over stated .
