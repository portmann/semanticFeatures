US Navy official eyes more Boeing FA fighter jet orders Reuters WASHINGTON Nov The US Navy on Tuesday underscored its desire to buy more Boeing Co FAEF Super Hornets in coming years to deal with higherthanexpected operational demands and past delays in the Lockheed Martin Corp F fighter jet program Rear Admiral Michael Manazir director of air warfare for the US chief of naval operations told lawmakers that the Navy was working to speed up maintenance of oldermodel FAs but would also need to buy more new FAEF jets to avert a shortfall in strike fighters for its aircraft carriers Manazir testifying before the readiness subcommittee of the House Armed Services Committee echoed a call earlier this year by former Chief of Naval Operations Admiral Jonathan Greenert who said the Navy would need two to three more squadrons of Super Hornets or to more aircraft to meet its needs US lawmakers are poised to approve the purchase of FAEFs in fiscal which began Oct Manazir said problems could be avoided if the Navy bought more Super Hornets in both fiscal and and was able to start using an initial squadron of F fighter jets as now planned in August F delays forced the Navy several years ago to extend the service life of its older FAC Hornets from hours to hours a project that turned out to be more intensive and take much longer than expected Manazirs comments spell good news for
