Boeing
A reduction in the rate of production of the current generation 777 would have a significant effect on the company 's earnings and cash flow as the relatively high margin 777 program plays an outsized role in cash generation for Boeing . Poponak believes Boeing will be forced to cut the rate of production of the current 777 aircraft from 6.8 to 5 per month . (Boeing: Uncertainty) Boeing is a giant in the aerospace industry and is well situated strategically as demand for commercial aircraft will allow the company to continue to ramp up production of its 787 Dreamliner and 737 program in the near and medium term and its 777X in the longer term . (Boeing: Surprise) The company 's military business , long a source of concern for investors due to U.S. Defense spending cuts , is awaiting a decision on the next generation of strategic bomber from the U.S. Airforce while continuing to deliver a variety of other products to the U.S. Military . (Uncertainty) The company also has a number of contracts with foreign governments to deliver many of its proven defense products.Despite these positives, the company's current share price is likely fairly valued at 19 times 2015 earnings.Boeing will likely face continued Dreamliner cost deferments in the near term , a production slow down in the current generation 777 program in 2016 , and possible design , engineering , or production delays in its 737 Max and 777X programs based on the nature and complexity of the programs and the company 's recent history . (Boeing: Uncertainty) All of these possible drags to its cash flow will be partially offset by rising rates of production for its commercial products , but the risks they pose are fairly priced in the stock at current levels and investors should wait for a pull back in share price before buying this strategically well positioned company .
