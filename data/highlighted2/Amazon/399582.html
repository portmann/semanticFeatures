Hulu has begun to launch original content , as Netflix and Amazon already have . It is working on more exclusive content deals , which should bring more people to the site to find shows they ca n't watch anywhere else . (Surprise) Hulu should feel comfortable spending money on these shows because if they are put on a strong platform , they will yield quick results and allow it to tap the same virtuous cycle Netflix has : more content brings more eyeballs , brings more money , brings more content . (Surprise & Uncertainty) The only problem with Hulu for investors is that they can't invest.Hulu is a joint venture of Comcast, Disney, and Fox, with no publicly traded shares.Of the three , all are so heavily invested in linear-TV that whatever benefits Hulu brings to their bottom line may well be cancelled out by the damage done to their other businesses . (Uncertainty) Disney has ESPN , Comcast is America 's largest cable company , and Fox has no fewer than 15 RSNs , the channels loaded with expensive sports contracts little less than ESPN . (Surprise) However, there are periodic reports of interest in Hulu from other parties, either joining the Big Three in the existing configuration or in some combination of a sale/IPO.TimeWarner (NYSE: TWX ) was rumored to be bidding not too long ago, although so far nothing has come of it.The price was said to value Hulu at $5 billion.My recommendation is to hold off on the three incumbents , but to buy any new partners who enter the business with less deadweight . (Surprise) CBS ( NYSE : CBS ) would profit the most from Hulu , since with such strong content they have less to fear than most from the demise of the old system . (Surprise & Uncertainty) They could milk Hulu 's profits without fear of cannibalizing themselves . (Uncertainty) For Hulu it would round out their broadcast TV lineup nicely.Discovery Communications ( DISC ) or Univision ( Pending : UVN ) would also be good candidates to go long if they could get their foot in the door . (Uncertainty) Best of all of course would be an IPO , but that seems less likely right now . (Surprise & Uncertainty) To me, the fact that Hulu has gone from 6 to 12 million in two years with as little exclusive content as it has says a lot about its potential, not its shortcomings.To be clear, Hulu has a long way to go.Too much of its library is still non-exclusive , and it probably still charges too much considering Netflix ( $ 10 ) and Amazon ( $ 9 ) prices , especially considering that they offer two streams at a time instead of one . (Amazon: Uncertainty) But after years of wallowing in the shadows it has begun to move.Netflix today sports a market cap of almost $ 50 billion .
