Even With Higher Margins , Amazon Would n't Be Cheap - Amazon.com , Inc. ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) | Seeking Alpha Alhambra Investment Partners , Alhambra Partners ( 1 click ) Raising profit margins at Amazon is n't that easy . (Amazon: Surprise) Amazon 's rate of capital spending is n't that extraordinary . I was watching an interview with Stanley Druckenmiller the other day when the subject of Amazon ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) arose . Druckenmiller owns the stock and raved about the company saying that unlike a lot of others Amazon was `` investing in their future . '' He was particularly excited about their cloud business and said that Amazon could ramp up margins and profits whenever they want . (Amazon: Uncertainty) That 's a sentiment I 've heard from a lot of people over the years when it comes to Amazon 's serial lack of profits . Since we are primarily value investors , Amazon rarely comes up for discussion and has never made it through any of our stock screens to be included in any of our model portfolios . The stock is just too expensive and the return on invested capital too low for us to consider.But this sentiment that Amazon can ramp up profits whenever they want intrigues me . Is it possible that Amazon stock is actually cheap enough to consider ? (Amazon: Uncertainty) Could they really just ramp up margins from the current fraction of a percent to something more normal for a retailer ? (Surprise & Uncertainty) Certainly Amazon has , in a relatively short period of time , become a major player in US retail so they must be doing something right . As an avid reader I've been a fan and customer for a long time.And when I buy anything these days , like most people , I check prices on Amazon before I buy anywhere else because chances are they 're selling it cheaper . (Amazon: Surprise) Amazon has now become part of our culture , the go to place for just about anything one needs and plenty one does n't . But the stock price?That's one thing they haven't figured out how to discount.The stock has always traded for a premium multiple and that is certainly true today at over 100 times next year's earnings estimate.But if margins could really be expanded at will , then maybe the price can be justified . (Uncertainty) Let 's forget for a moment that doing that probably is n't as easy as Amazon fans imagine and just make some assumptions to see if there is any way to justify the current price . (Amazon: Uncertainty) WMT Profit Margin ( TTM ) data by YCharts The range for retailers is roughly 3 to 5 % . (Uncertainty) For the purposes of our Amazon investigation , let 's assume they achieve the upper level and raise margins to 5 % . (Amazon: Uncertainty) That 's up from the current 0.33 % by the way , a heroic leap but apparently one that 's achievable for exceptional retailers . (Uncertainty) For the last 12 months Amazon generated $ 100.588 billion in revenue . At a 5% profit margin that would generate over $5 billion in earnings or about $10.52 per share of earnings (using the latest diluted share count).If we assume that the current growth rate of about 25 % persists for the next couple of years earnings would grow to $ 16.44 per share . (Uncertainty) At the current price that means an investor is paying almost 40 times earnings two years hence for a company growing at 25 % . (Uncertainty) That still seems a bit rich but not nearly as onerous as the multiple based on the current margins . (Uncertainty) Of course , it is n't Amazon 's retail business that really has people excited . That honor belongs to Amazon Web Services , the cloud business . That business is growing rapidly, on pace for about $7 billion in sales this year at a margin of about 25%.Deutsche Bank recently estimated that the business would grow to $16 billion by 2017 and put a 10X multiple on that revenue and arrived at a value of $160 billion.I would n't buy it at that price but maybe someone would . (Uncertainty) So , if we assume that AWS grows to $ 16 billion by 2017 and Amazon 's current overall growth rate of 25 % continues , then AWS will represent roughly 10 % of Amazon 's revenue by that time . (Amazon: Uncertainty) Even if AWS maintains those margins , the impact on Amazon 's bottom line is not that great . If you assume a 25 % margin for the AWS business and a 5 % margin for everything else the total profit margin only rises to 7 % . (Uncertainty) Making all those heroic assumptions gets the two year forward P/E down to ... 28 times . (Uncertainty) All of this assumes too that Amazon can indeed ramp up profits at will , something I 've heard a lot of very smart people say . (Amazon: Uncertainty) There seems to be several schools of thought on this . (Uncertainty) One is that the cloud business will grow so rapidly that it will raise the overall profit margin.But as I just showed above , while that could certainly raise margins , it is n't enough to make the stock cheap . (Uncertainty) I suppose if one looks out over a much longer time period it might be true . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Jeff Bezos himself has said that the cloud business will eventually surpass the retail business , something I find hard to believe but he 's Jeff Bezos and I 'm not . (Uncertainty) Another idea is that Amazon can just raise prices to achieve those margins . That was the predominant theme for the bulls before the cloud business came along to provide another justification for the stock price.I 'm not sure if the people who believe this think raising prices wo n't affect growth - the laws of supply and demand do n't apply to Amazon - or if they believe that Amazon has monopoly pricing power . (Amazon: Uncertainty) If it is the former, I have to disagree.If Amazon raises prices , it will certainly affect their growth rate and therefore the multiple one can put on the stock . If it's the latter, I also have to disagree.If Amazon really has monopoly power someone in Washington , DC , is going to notice and do something about it . Lastly , I 've heard a number of people say that all Amazon has to do is cut back on their capital spending to ramp up earnings . There are a number of problems with this line of thinking.First is that capex only flows through the income statement via depreciation - capex flows through the cash flow statement.Reducing capex will not have an immediate and significant impact on the bottom line.Second , if Amazon can just turn this capex spending off at will does n't that imply that they 've been spending on things they do n't need ? If that's true shouldn't shareholders be upset about that right now?If it isn't true wouldn't reducing capex be starving the business of necessary investment?And lastly , Amazon 's rate of investment really is n't that extraordinary , even for a retailer : AMZN CAPEX To Revenue ( TTM ) data by YCharts I am a big fan of Jeff Bezos and Amazon . I admire Bezos' willingness to invest for the long term and ignore the quarterly earnings game on Wall Street.I admire his willingness to take risks that other , more timid managers wo n't . (Surprise & Uncertainty) He isn't always right - he's had his share of product flops - but at least he's trying to innovate and compete.But there is a big difference between a good company and a good stock.At current prices Amazon stock does n't appear all that attractive even when one makes some pretty generous assumptions about their profit margins . (Amazon: Uncertainty) In that last chart above I included Microsoft (NASDAQ: MSFT ) for a reason.They also have a fast growing cloud business that sports pretty good margins.In fact, their profit margins are pretty good for the entire business, not just the cloud part.It certainly does n't qualify for deep value status but it 's a lot cheaper than Amazon and you do n't have to make heroic assumptions about the future to justify holding it . (Amazon: Surprise & Uncertainty) They also happen to be investing in their future and at a higher percentage of revenue than Amazon .
