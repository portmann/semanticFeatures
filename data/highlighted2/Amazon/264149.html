Netflix Is Positioned For A Major Stumble In 2016 - Netflix , Inc. ( NASDAQ : NFLX ) | Seeking Alpha Netflix stock is up 143 % this year - We believe it 's overvalued by at least 30 % -35 % and has a one-year target price of $ 80 - $ 85 per share . (Uncertainty) Netflix 's U.S. leadership position will be challenged by Amazon Prime and other competitors . Further price increases will drastically slow US membership growth.In our view , Netflix 's significant investment in original programming will be an ineffective strategy in the long-term as streaming services become more commoditized . (Surprise) Significant downside risk for investors at current valuation . (Uncertainty) Several warning signs are emerging at Netflix and these cautionary indicators suggest that the stock has far outpaced the fair value of the business and a correction is likely to occur in 2016 . (Uncertainty) We are particularly concerned that long-term investors who are holding shares in Netflix ( NASDAQGS : NFLX ) with a 5-10 year investment horizon and expect considerable capital appreciation from the current level may be exposed to considerable downside risk . (Uncertainty) The stock is up 143% this year and currently has an enterprise value of $52 billion.With a ttm EBITDA of $492 million, the company is trading at 105x ttm EV/EBITDA and at a P/E of 317.At the current valuation the bull case remains tenuous, at best.Many of the key assumptions driving the frothy valuation appear to be misguided and not supported by fact . (Uncertainty) Based on our outlook , we believe Netflix is overvalued by at least 30 % -35 % and there are several catalysts in place that may spark a sell-off in 2016 . (Uncertainty) In the U.S. streaming market, Netflix's subscriber growth momentum has slowed dramatically.The company reported a decline in net adds in the US in Q3 2015 (880,000) compared to Q3 2014 (980,000).We believe the proximate cause for the loss of momentum is the increasing appeal of competing services such as Hulu and Amazon Prime . (Amazon: Uncertainty) Also , the recent price increase may have played a larger role in the slowdown than management will admit . (Surprise & Uncertainty) In our view , it 's very unlikely that the past growth of nearly 6 million net subscribers in each of the past four years will be replicated by Netflix for several reasons . (Uncertainty) Netflix currently has captured 65.1% of U.S. TV watchers which is down slightly from 65.6% in 2014, according to PricewaterhouseCooper's recent December 2015 report on the SVOD market.The recent trend suggests that with such a large share and several new competitors with lower prices in the market , Netflix will face challenges in 2016 to expand this number meaningfully . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Secondly , in our view , the U.S. SVOD market is already over-saturated and subscriber growth will likely slow to single-digits by 2018-2020 for Netflix given their already large membership base and increased competition , particularly from Amazon ( NASDAQGS : AMZN ) . In our view , even though a secular shift from linear TV to on-demand internet TV will benefit Netflix over the next several years , we believe Amazon Prime will continue to gain on Netflix . (Amazon: Uncertainty) In our view , Amazon represents a legitimate direct threat to take a leadership role in the U.S. market . Amazon Prime , which is priced at $ 8.25 per month , gives consumers access to `` Amazon Prime Video , Prime Music , Prime Photos , the Kindle Lending Library , free two-day shipping on certain items , and discounted next-day shipping on certain items . '' With over 44 million Amazon Prime members , the company is heavily focused on improving the Amazon Prime Video offering with aggressive initiatives to build original programming and by launching a new feature that gives members access to premium TV networks including Showtime and Starz . In contrast , we believe Netflix 's exceptionally large $ 5 billion bet on producing exclusive and innovative original programming will not protect it from the increased competition over the long term . (Uncertainty) Netflix has a recent track record of success, including developing the hit series Orange is the New Black as well as Narcos.However we do not believe this a strategy which will give Netflix a sustainable advantage over the competition . (Uncertainty) Each streaming service is committed to developing original content , but Netflix 's large investment does not guarantee the company will continue to produce more successful projects than unsuccessful ones . (Surprise) Moreover , a December 2015 survey by PricewaterhouseCoopers confirms our view on this strategy and suggests that original programming is not as important as many Netflix bulls would seem to believe . (Uncertainty) The number of OTT subscribers who view original programming as very important is down in 2015 to 27.2% from 33.3% in 2014.The number also is down for those who view original programming as somewhat important in 2015 to 36.6 % from 40.7 % in 2014 . (Uncertainty) Overall 36.2% of OTT subscribers view original programming as not very important or not important at all.We see the SVOD market as becoming more commoditized over time in a similar fashion as cable television . (Surprise) As such , Netflix 's programming budget will add risk to the overall business and may only serve as a point of differentiation amid a trend toward the increasing commoditization of streaming services . (Uncertainty) We therefore believe that total U.S. memberships growth will continue to lose momentum and will result in a top-line miss in 2016 . (Uncertainty) We are forecasting total revenue of $8.59 billion in 2016.It remains to be seen whether management can offset this stagnant domestic growth with remarkable international growth.In our view , there will likely be a drastic share price correction to reflect the lower projected growth . (Surprise) Bull Case Overestimates Netflix 's ARPU Pricing Power We believe the Netflix bulls are overestimating the degree to which Netflix can increase prices without a loss in subscribers . (Uncertainty) Many Netflix bulls argue that the two recent price increases , one in May 2014 and another in October 2015 , did not result in slower membership growth and indicates that Netflix has the ability to implement further increases in the future . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Furthermore , the bulls argue , as more exclusive and original programming is introduced by Netflix , consumers will be compelled to the pay upwards of $ 12 - $ 15 per month or beyond . (Surprise) We disagree with this conclusion.In fact , we believe the recent price actions are the reason behind the loss of momentum in U.S. membership growth and puts Netflix at a competitive disadvantage . (Uncertainty) Ultimately , in our view , a significant number of consumers will move into other options such as Amazon Prime Video ( $ 8.25 per month ) and Hulu ( $ 8 per month ) that offer tremendous value . Our projections for U.S. membership growth are 5.3 million net adds in 2016 and 4.7 million in 2017 but we expect another price increase in early-2017 which will contribute to the slower membership growth beyond that point . (Surprise) We believe the bulls are underestimating how cost-conscious customers of streaming services are and how willing customers are to switch to one of the many other providers including HBO Go , YouTube Red or another new entrant . (Uncertainty) Our five-year projection reflects the aforementioned market dynamics and the increasing competition in the crowded SVOD market.We expect a top-line miss and earnings miss in 2016 and project $8.59 billion in revenue and EPS of $0.22 for the year.We believe this will serve as a catalyst for a correction in the stock price and bring the price within our one-year target range of $ 80 - $ 85 per share . (Uncertainty) In the following years we are still projecting strong growth in revenue of 15.5% per year.This base case scenario illustrates how overvalued, in our view, Netflix is.Even with these assumptions of only slight weakening of fundamentals , the fair value of the shares drops to $ 80 - $ 85 per share . (Uncertainty) We are still expecting strong EPS growth of 40.7% per year over the five-year period and EPS in 2020 of $1.19.There is a significant possibility that even if Netflix exceeds our projection of EPS of $ 1.19 five years from now by as much as 50 % to $ 1.79 that the multiple on these earnings could be markedly lower in 2020 than the current multiple . (Surprise & Uncertainty) For example, a P/E ratio of 47 in 2020 on earnings that exceed our reasonable estimate by 50% would still put the stock price in our one-year target of $80-$85 per share.In our view , investors in Netflix should proceed with caution in 2016 and prepare for the possibility of a substantial correction in the stock price given the extreme optimism the bull case is based upon and the implications of even a modest shortfall to these expectations .
