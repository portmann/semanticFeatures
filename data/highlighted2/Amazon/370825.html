Keep in mind that most of the early movers to the cloud are from the private space.As we covered in the above-linked Microsoft article , this company 's huge presence in the public sector makes it a heavy favorite to win the lion 's share as more state , county and federal agencies move to the cloud . (Surprise) To combat Amazon 's `` GovCloud , '' which is specifically for government agencies , Microsoft 's Office 365 is speeding things up as well with recent expansion of business meetings and voice services . (Surprise) Microsoft held meetings with the Indian IT minister a few weeks ago, and the seeds have been laid to foray into this $2 trillion opportunity.Yes, $2 trillion, according to the CEO.While AWS is still the leader by market share, Azure has many advantages that will help it gain market share: When it comes to programming and testing tools, support and their integration, nobody beats Microsoft.The fact that many of its programming languages and tools (think of Microsoft .Net, MS SQL Server database and the like) are used widely adds to Azure's appeal with native compatibility.Irrespective of programming languages and tools, most private and public entities operate and host on the Windows platform.This again falls right into Azure's lap with native compatibility.Dedicated instances in general appear costlier with AWS compared to Azure .
