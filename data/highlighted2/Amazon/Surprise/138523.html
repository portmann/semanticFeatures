Is Netflix Overvalued?- Netflix, Inc. (NASDAQ:NFLX) | Seeking Alpha The prevailing rhetoric for not buying Netflix is driven by fears over its valuation. (UNCERTAINTY) Netflix is not overvalued; if anything it's reasonably valued when based on current fundamentals.I think that if the valuation were to stay constant, the stock would in fact trade at a higher price assuming the PEG were to remain constant. (UNCERTAINTY) This implies that Netflix is a poor candidate for a short, because investors would not only lose their shirts, but also their jeans and shoes.Here's what he stated in his most recent article: First, today's market is trading at multiples much higher than it has in at least 10 years.Were fundamentals to remain constant, but multiples contract to historical averages, the market would undergo a substantial downdraft.All investors should be cautious as a result. (UNCERTAINTY) Second, given that multiple expansions haven't impacted all sectors equally, my plan going forward is to focus my valuation shorts in the 3 sectors whose multiples are highest today: biotech; software & services; and consumer services.Everyone wants to predict the next market crash, the problem? (UNCERTAINTY) I haven't seen anyone do this consistently.Sure, we could argue that Netflix is a short if the market tanks, but so is everything else. (UNCERTAINTY) I don't think the broader market has topped out.It's going to be a while before valuations become over extended.The cyclically adjusted P/E multiple for the broader S&P 500 is at the midpoint of the cycle.In the past market valuation expansionary cycle, we saw valuation tick higher for nearly 20 years straight. (UNCERTAINTY) The market hit bottom about 6 years ago.So any early speculation on a massive market correction that involves a massive regression in valuation could be premature. (UNCERTAINTY) Remember, markets can remain irrational longer than you can remain solvent.Netflix trades at a more generous valuation, because there's a pathway to sustained revenue growth over a multi-year period.There's no other realistic alternative to capturing the Internet TV space aside from Amazon (NASDAQ: AMZN ).However, Amazon is not a pure play on Internet TV, and to Amazon, public cloud is a much bigger priority than Instant Video viewing hours.Therefore, investors who want exposure into web TV don't have a long list of candidate to choose from. (UNCERTAINTY) Netflix secured huge wins in the early innings of the Internet TV space.The company signed a deal with Disney (NYSE: DIS ), and launched various in-house productions.This keeps the process of content bidding a bit more honest as Netflix can always revert to more in-house content to avoid price gouging.Netflix expanded internationally, so it will also have a global base of subscribers to market content to, which creates trouble for regionally locked content studios.The ability to source higher quality content becomes easier when you operate on a 30% growth rate for revenues.MVPDs (multichannel video programming distributors) aren't growing at the same rate as Netflix, so eventually; the competing economics of content deals for online streaming becomes better whereas deals with MVPDs become less desirable.When assuming a 30% top line growth rate, Netflix's revenue will reach $20 billion by 2020. (UNCERTAINTY) The growth will have to accelerate by 5 points, but I think that's feasible given the adoption rate of 4K TVs, and the robust growth in international subscribers (68.5% year-over-year growth in Q2 2015).The movement of domestic subscribers to higher pricing tiers will eventually materialize by 2017.I also think international subscriber growth will be driven by China, as a wave of deregulation will make it easier for Netflix to launch its streaming service in the mainland.My long-run net income forecast is $3 billion on a 15% profit margin assumption. (UNCERTAINTY) The company may produce higher margins, but given the rapid investment into content and in-house productions, I've tempered my expectations on margins. (UNCERTAINTY) If Netflix were to trade at perhaps 60 times earnings, the stock would be worth $180 billion. (UNCERTAINTY) Even at 45 times earnings the stock would be worth $135 billion.The valuation will contract from 240 times earnings, but anticipating a more rapid reduction in valuation would be ridiculous.
