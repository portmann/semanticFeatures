Intel's Saving Grace: The Data Center Group - Intel Corporation (NASDAQ:INTC) | Seeking Alpha Additional details on the DCG strategy were shared by Intel via a conference call yesterday.The DCG may operate on Tick Tock, whereas PCCG will run on Tick Tock Tock. (UNCERTAINTY) Strategically this makes sense, as it optimizes node efficiency on a higher-margin product. (UNCERTAINTY) The integration of FPGAs will boost compute performance for large-scale cloud vendors, which will result in a more virtuous cycle for Intel.Demand seems robust as demanding use cases have cropped up over the years. (UNCERTAINTY) The market has expanded in response to more powerful parts, which is counter intuitive.Intel's (NASDAQ: INTC ) Data Center Group seems to be on the right track. (UNCERTAINTY) In fact, it's the only division that seems to be getting it right these days as the various other segments have under performed due to competitive issues or market dependent issues. (UNCERTAINTY) The stock has already responded to the weakness in PC fundamentals, and should eventually find some support on the back of reassuring comments from management on DCG and ongoing developments in the data storage space.I reviewed the Q&A session of Jason Waxman (DCG Group Vice President), which can be accessed on Intel's Investor Relations website.The conference got through a lot of pertinent information, which helped me to further understand the durability of earnings going forward.I can't cover everything, but I'm going to cover the three most pertinent points that surfaced throughout the conference call.Intel's DCG may run on a tick tock cycle whereas PCCG will run on tick tock tock. (UNCERTAINTY) This makes sense, because server chips have higher margins, and the initial ramp of a new process node tends to compress gross margins due to less-than-optimal yields.So, pushing the server chips into a more advanced node will sustain Intel's competitive advantage without impacting margins as significantly.This gives Intel time to optimize for 10nm on the server side, and develop more advanced architecture on the consumer side. (UNCERTAINTY) By the time consumer chips reach 10 nm Intel will be able to maximize wafer production, and conversion of 14nm capacity to 10 nm will be less cost intensive.The acquisition of Altera will give Intel the ability to create a truly comprehensive platform for co-processors and GPGPUs.For algorithms that are repetitive in nature, i.e. file compressions, crypto currency mining and various other compute heavy tasks that are heavily repetitive, FPGAs fill in the hole.Furthermore, the energy efficiency, and the ability to scale the FPGAs is a huge selling point for top cloud vendors.The companies looking to implement FPGAs are at the cutting edge, so think in terms of Google ( GOOG , GOOGL ), Microsoft (NASDAQ: MSFT ), IBM (NYSE: IBM ), Hewlett-Packard (NYSE: HPQ ), Facebook (NASDAQ: FB ) and Amazon (NASDAQ: AMZN ).The performance advantage of a fully integrated platform has left alternative server ecosystems dead in the water i.e. ARM based servers.Intel doesn't see meaningful deceleration in chip volumes even as reports indicate that the top cloud vendors are expected to reduce capex growth pertaining to data centers.Perhaps, demand is coming from alternative sources? (UNCERTAINTY) The growth rate in DCG seems to have held up pretty well, which speaks volumes about the demanding computing needs that are being offloaded onto servers rather than being run on PCs. (UNCERTAINTY) Ideally Intel should have had a robust PCCG and DCG business, but at least mobile growth comes accompanied with more demanding data center usage on the back-end.To conclude, Intel is in a position of strength in the data center space, and I don't anticipate competitors to encroach upon those higher power envelopes. (UNCERTAINTY) Intel has optimized its server CPU architecture over the years. (UNCERTAINTY) The simplicity of a cloud build out using Xeon CPUs and the better total cost of ownership has mitigated much of the hype presented by alternative platforms.Therefore, the high growth nature of the business and the mix-shift to higher-margin SKUs across server class-CPUs keeps me optimistic.
