investing because Insider Monkey points out that hedge funds' long positions have shown a history of outperformance ( see the details here ).After this recent drop, DIS now trades at 19x forward earnings, which is in-line with the greater market.DIS, however, is growing faster than the overall economy with growth rates in revenue in the 7% to 8% range and growth rates in net income ranging from 14% to 16% in the last couple of years.Similarly, cash flow from operations has grown every year from at least 2010 when it was about $6.6 billion to about $10.7 billion today.What's not to like?The company still has tremendous latent value in its stable of assets housed under Marvel, Star Wars, ESPN, Frozen, and its older brands as well.The new Star Wars film is virtually guaranteed to generate $1 billion in sales worldwide and kick-off renewed interest in the franchise, leading to even more revenue from merchandising and other derivative products including shows, amusement park rides, and clothing.ESPN is keeping cable alive and still dominates the sports broadcasting business.It will make the transition to becoming more mobile and on demand as millennial customers want, but it still generates the highest fees from cable subscribers - so it is not paramount that DIS move ESPN completely to a stand-alone platform right-away.A gradual approach to making ESPN more stand-alone, while still reaping the income from cable fees should be followed and I believe is being followed by DIS.
