(Amazon: Surprise) The other big move came from Google (NASDAQ: GOOG ) (NASDAQ: GOOGL ) following its strong quarter and upbeat commentary, and when one looks at the numbers it is quite staggering.The changes in market cap are bigger than many companies and in some cases are equal to market caps of the largest publicly traded companies in certain industries ! (Surprise) The news from Amazon and how it aligns with what we heard from others in the tech industry leads us to continue to believe tech is one of the two main pillars investors want to drive returns for their portfolios through the end of the year . (Amazon: Uncertainty) Expect to see the Nasdaq make another move towards the 5,200 point level today on the back of strong tech earnings.We have economic news today, and it is as follows: Seattle saw two of its largest corporate citizens report solid quarterly results and full-year guidance after yesterday's closing bell.While Amazon.com stole the headlines with their unexpected blowaway earnings , Starbucks ( NASDAQ : SBUX ) also had good news to report and talked about the success of their mobile payments strategy and the product mix . (Amazon: Uncertainty) Amazon 's strong EPS were fueled by the company 's revenues coming in higher than expected , which was due to strength in Amazon Web Services and Marketplaces . (Amazon: Surprise) Interestingly enough , it appears that Amazon is not only pushing top line growth with AWS , but is also seeing margin expansion for the cloud unit ; something which is an anomaly of sorts when discussing Amazon and its historical business model . (Amazon: Uncertainty) With this being a potential breakout quarter and data indicating that Prime Day was a success for the company, analysts are out with bullish comments and have raised their price targets once again.This is partly in response to the quarter , but probably has a lot more to do with the fact that the stock shot up more than 18 % in after hours trading . (Surprise & Uncertainty) The move pushed Amazon 's market cap above that of Wal-Mart ( NYSE : WMT ) , which was something unthinkable only a few years ago , and increased Jeff Bezos ' net worth by roughly $ 7 billion . (Amazon: Uncertainty) Investors can expect Amazon to have a tailwind propelling them higher so long as AWS and Prime membership continue to grow because the margin expansion that has occurred at AWS is a real benefit to the bottom line and new Prime members help drive revenues as they typically spend 2-4 times more than non-Prime members . (Amazon: Surprise) While Starbucks ' 4.3 % + move after hours paled in comparison to Amazon 's move , it was nothing to turn one 's nose up to . The move to $59/share is a new all-time high for the company's shares and continues the winning streak that Starbucks has been on over the last few years.While technology is most certainly benefiting the company, the stores continue to be the strength with sales at stores open at least 13 months increasing by 7% on a global basis and individual customer transactions increasing by some 23 million over the previous year's quarter.Same store sales in the Americas, which includes the US, rose 8% but the company stressed that analysts should continue to use mid-single digit same store sales growth.The board of directors also voted to increase the company's share repurchase program by authorizing another 50 million shares to be added to the current program.Separately, the company also announced that it had entered into an agreement to expand its distribution agreement with PepsiCo (NYSE: PEP ) to enter 10 Latin American countries as the two partners seek growth from new markets.While AT&T ( NYSE : T ) may have missed revenue projections for the quarter , the company did report EPS of $ 0.69 which handily beat analysts ' expectations of $ 0.63 / share . (Uncertainty) While the bottom line results are something that investors should feel good about , we think that the wireless net additions figure is more important . (Surprise) AT&T reported 2.1 million net additional wireless customers, which blew away the 1.32 million estimate and shows that T-Mobile US (NYSE: TMUS ) is not the only carrier which can steal away customers right now.Regarding the company's purchase of DirecTV (NASDAQ: DTV ), Bloomberg is reporting that the FCC has voted to approve the deal.While an official announcement has not been made , this is most certainly good news for investors as it ensures AT&T 's ability to cover its dividend in the future and will enable both companies to better compete against the cable companies .
