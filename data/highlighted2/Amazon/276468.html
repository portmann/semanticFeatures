This is a ten-year chart of Wal-Mart.If one had bought in 2007 or perhaps even at the lows of 2010 , they would have been tracking along at around a 12 % total return , depending on exactly when they bought , up until 2015 . (Amazon: Surprise & Uncertainty) Of course, not all investors care about total return or as stock's performance relative to some benchmark like the S&P 500.For some dividend growth is what matters, and for others, they are only looking for a 4, 5, or 6% return.The key is to have a goal.Depending on what the thesis is for the initial medium-to-long-term stock purchase , I usually start measuring performance no earlier than a year , more typically around two years , after purchasing stocks in this category . (Surprise & Uncertainty) It's important to keep in mind that these goals are built-in to every medium-to-long-term thesis I make.This means that I do n't purchase , or recommend purchasing , medium-to-long-term stocks unless I think there is a good probability of the annual total return exceeding 12 % . (Uncertainty) If the thesis plays out over , say , a two-year period and the annual return is only 8 % , then I would admit that I made a mistake in judging how the market would value the company after the thesis played out , and I would sell . (Uncertainty) For example , if I thought Gilead ( NASDAQ : GILD ) would sell far more hepatitis drugs over the next two years than the market anticipated , and that it would develop another profitable drug during that time as well , and that those factors would cause the price of the stock to rise more than my required 12 % per year , and then both of my predictions happened , but two years later the price had only risen 8 % per year , I would sell . (Surprise & Uncertainty) My thesis would have been correct, but the price wouldn't have met my goals.This would happen only if I purchased Gilead as a medium-to-long-term stock.In my Roth account, all my purchases are strictly long-term purchases (3) that I hold for at least five years.Essentially what this does is ties my thesis to the performance goals, so that the thesis goes unstated.In my next article, I'll discuss my investing strategy for long-term purchases and when to sell the successful ones.I am always trying to improve my process and love to hear others' thoughts.Feel free to leave them in the comment section , and if you would like Seeking Alpha to notify you when future articles of mine are published , consider ` following ' me .
