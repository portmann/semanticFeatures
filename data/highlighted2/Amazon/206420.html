WalMart NYSE WMT told investors it would pull an Amazoncom NASDAQ AMZN for the next few years foregoing profit in order to invest in technology the stock tanked and the onlookers laughed But what most people dont know is that WMT has always been a technology company Its rise in the s and s was fueled by bar code technology which could track shipments directly from Chinese manufacturers through stores and efficiently manage inventory to reduce the need for warehousing Competitors have caught up with all that Amazon in particular has gone well beyond that its Amazon Web Services cloud disrupting the heart of the technology market Thats where WalMart is now going to focus Its move this week to open source its cloud hopper technology which allows companies to manage their workloads on disparate clouds is aimed squarely at the heart of Amazons cloud business As WalMart said in its announcement the whole idea behind OneOps is to eliminate cloud lockin to allow enterprises to continuously choose among cloud vendors in order to get the best possible deal As WalmartLabs head Jeremy King wrote Its great news for companies who have considered switching cloud providers but experience the Hotel California Effect where they can check out but never leave their cloud provider because theyre locked into the providers proprietary APIs architecture and tools The key words there are the providers proprietary APIs Amazons proprietary API is considered the secret sauce of AWS an industry standard coders have to write to The reason Amazoncom is now worth times its annual revenue while WalMart is worth only of its comes down entirely to Amazon Web Services which has been growing year over year with very fat margins The WalMart move wont entirely change that but it could give customers the ability to reduce their dependence on AWS and cause the kinds of small cuts that Amazons success is causing to WMTs stores Another piece of WalMarts technology puzzle is Radio Frequency Identification or RFID The cost of passive RFID tags has come down to cents each still too high to put them on individual items but low enough that along with portal readers at warehouse and store entrances you can track goods with far more precision than before The key software challenge is to get the cost of picking orders from a store closer to what Amazoncom pays in its warehouses close enough so that the time and distance advantage a network of stores has over a warehouse becomes meaningful That also means automating consumers shopping lists Amazoncom is trying to do that with its widelylampooned Dash buttons This is the current focus of WalmartLabs and it has quietly made acquisitions aimed at that in the last two years as part of that effort The holy grail is that consumers lists of what they need and use are kept in an electronic form that can be turned into shopping lists with precision and then fulfilled automatically Instead of you going to the store what you need would come to you and shopping will be transformed from something you do into a service you can buy This is a software challenge It is also a system integration challenge which is why only scaled companies need apply for it Amazon is well along in this effort Other stores like Kroger NYSE KR are making efforts in that direction Now WalMart has decided to focus on this key technology
