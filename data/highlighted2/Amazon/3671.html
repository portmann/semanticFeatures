Facebook Powers Up Its E-Commerce Ambition - Facebook (NASDAQ:FB) | Seeking Alpha I like Facebook's recent acquisition of TheFind, a leading online shopping search engine.TheFind’s website will get shut down but its technology will be integrated with Facebook’s advertising services.Facebook users can look forward to receiving product advertisements that are no longer redundant.I am happy with Facebook's (NASDAQ: FB ) acquisition of personalized online shopping search engine, TheFind .Facebook can now use TheFind's search engine algorithm to improve the accuracy of its targeted ads.TheFind was established in 2006 , and was one of the first sites that help people find the best product deals.Facebook bought a real winner because TheFind's search engine can seek out and compare products of online ecommerce sites and local brick & mortar retailers.Users also get to refine their product search results to include those with sales promotions, have discount coupons, or great shipping deals.Facebook can now exploit TheFind's access to 500 million products sold by 500,000 online and local stores.A check on SimilarWeb reveals that TheFind's website has a higher web traffic ranking than most of its competitors.As of February 1, 2015, TheFind's site received 6.7 million monthly unique visitors.Facebook will shut down TheFind's website in the next few weeks.TheFind's mobile apps will also likely be killed because Facebook will be integrating TheFind's search engine technology in its core advertising algorithm.Anything that improves the advertising cash cow of Facebook is one more reason to go long on FB.The first benefit of owning TheFind's search engine will help Facebook deliver more relevant and updated advertisements to its 1.4 billion users.Targeted people will no longer receive annoying advertisements for a product they already bought.Facebook wants to use TheFind's technology to track users when they browse and/or buy products online.Facebook will then use the gathered data as a basis of what ads those users will see when they log back in to their Facebook accounts.If the user already saw and bought a product from a Facebook ad, he or she will not see the same product advertisement again.Making sure that users do not get too annoyed by redundant ads insures Facebook's advertisements remain welcome to those users.Annoying ads sometimes prompt people to use adblock software to keep their desktop and mobile browsers ad-free . (Uncertainty) The second benefit from TheFind is that it helps improve Facebook's Buy button campaign on mobile.The extensive search engine capability of TheFind will inspire more local retailers to use Facebook's Buy button platform .Location-based search engine positioning plus an impulse Buy button is a great way for local retailers to advertise their products to nearby customers.I previously discussed that Facebook is already a massive users-initiated e-commerce ecosystem.Facebook Groups and Pages are already being used to buy and swap products without any fees being paid to Facebook.The Buy button is therefore Facebook's attempt to reap some benefits from this crowd-initiated online shopping in its social network.TheFind's technology will help Facebook deliver localized sponsored ads of products based on the location of users.If a user clicks and buys the product, TheFind's search engine algorithm will make sure that the user will not get annoyed by the same product ad.Facebook 's huge presence in mobile could help TheFind maximize the power of its search engine . (Uncertainty) A majority of the 15 million users of TheFind are likely desktop PC users.The Android app of TheFind only had less than 100k download .Not that it is a bad thing, online shopping is still dominated by people who use desktop computers.As per eMarketer's chart below, desktop/laptop computers accounted for 57% of web traffic and 75.1% of total sales in U.S. retail ecommerce during the Q4 2014 period.TheFind 's web search engine technology might also work well on mobile . (Uncertainty) Facebook needs something to encourage more of its mobile users to buy products - by clicking the ads or the Facebook Buy button.Marketers and advertisers definitely want to improve the 1.1% conversion rate of smartphone online shoppers.Facebook , with its TheFind technology and Buy button , might just have found the right combination to convert more smartphone users from being mere ad clickers to product buyers . (Uncertainty) FB is a Buy .Facebook is expanding beyond its ad-driven business model into becoming a facilitator of online shopping.Due to the huge amount of people using them, social network websites and messaging apps are great enablers of ecommerce.Facebook , as the biggest social networking platform and owner of top messaging/sharing apps , could become a potential rival to eBay ( NASDAQ : EBAY ) and Amazon ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) .
