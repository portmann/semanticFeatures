(Surprise) For example, last week, Netflix revealed it had signed a deal for Julie's Greenroom, a new series starring film legend Julie Andrews and produced by the iconic Jim Henson Company.The show follows Andrews as she guides her "greenies" (aka puppet kids) through the process of creating a show.Each episode looks at a different form of theater, while featuring an original song and a guest spot by a fellow top-tier celeb.In addition to it being a clever concept, Greenroom also has aspects that appeals to both kids and their parents.This is taking that need for kids programming and thinking outside the box.Again, it's all about the overall added value but it doesn't have to just come from in-house ideas.For some strange reason, that Paramount (NYSE: VIA ) still hasn't really explained, the studio decided not to release its animated update of the classic The Little Prince.What makes it odd was that the decision was made just weeks before it was scheduled to hit theaters.It is rare (even unheard of) for that to happen so late in the game.Although that freed up Netflix to then swoop in and rescue the film, which will start to stream in August.While this is not the first time Netflix has acquired content , it may be the first time Netflix has done so under these circumstances . (Surprise) Fuller House and the Ashton Kutcher/Danny Masterson sitcom The Ranch represent the first multi-camera series for the network.For those unfamiliar , multi-camera series are taped in front of a studio audience and usually come with a built-in laugh track . (Uncertainty) Think Seinfeld, Friends, The Big Bang Theory, etc.To date Netflix had been focused on, and had great success with, single-camera projects such as Kimmy Schmidt, Grace and Frankie and (to some extent) Orange Is The New Black, but the network recognized it needed to expand out.For Netflix to remain competitive it needed series of all types, just like ABC, CBS, NBC, Fox, etc.Now the network has proven it can succeed in both ventures, with Fuller House and The Ranch both getting second season pickups.These renewals also come despite some amazingly bad reviews, but Netflix series are essentially critic-proof.Speaking of critics the other area that Netflix has ventured into is the world of talk shows ; for better or worse .
