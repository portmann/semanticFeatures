(Surprise & Uncertainty) State aid is defined as "an advantage in any form whatsoever conferred on a selective basis to undertakings by national public authorities."Of note is that the European Commission has the power to "recover incompatible state aid ."The EC seeks to control "state aid" under the auspice that "a company which receives government support gains an advantage over its competitors.Therefore, the Treaty generally prohibits State aid unless it is justified by reasons of general economic development."The Vice President of the European Commission responsible for Competition Policy , Joaquin Almunia , had this to say at the International Bar Association 's 18th Annual Competition Conference : Under State aid rules , national authorities can not take selective measures that allow certain companies to pay less in taxes than they should if the tax rules of the country were applied in a fair and non-discriminatory way . (Uncertainty) As the EC appears on a mission against anticompetitive state aid in the form of tax relief to large corporations , as well as increasing transparency in the tax rulings and arrangements of European Union member states , this effect becomes more and more of a reality . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Though I found no precedent for the levy of a substantial, multi-billion dollar fine regarding receiving state aid in the form of favorable tax arrangements, focus on this is shortsighted, as there exists a legitimate concern that at some time in the near future countries like Ireland, the Netherlands and Luxembourg, the trio of advantageous corporate tax regimes, will be forced to change to Apple's detriment.To what degree and the shape of this change at this point in time is uncertain , but it should loom as a consideration as a long-term threat for investors .
