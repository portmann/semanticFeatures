(Surprise) Essentially, the PC - or various functions of it - now fits on your wrist.And that 's ( wearables ) going to become a faster-growing market than the PC market Microsoft once thrived upon . (Surprise) But for now, the company's shareholders should rejoice that Microsoft's "cloud-first" mentality , ushered in by Nadella, has begun to take shape.For that reason , investors should be piling in on Microsoft stock , which is down roughly 7 % on the year . (Uncertainty) Save for Apple stock , there 's perhaps no cheaper alternative on the market than Microsoft , when combining its 2.86 % annual dividend yield . (Surprise & Uncertainty) With its P/E at 17, Microsoft stock is trading at meaningful discount to the S&P 500, which trades at a P/E of 21.This means if Microsoft traded on par with the rest of the market , the stock would trade today at around $ 55 ( 27 % higher ) , not $ 43 . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Sure, Microsoft's growth is still tied to the PC.That its bulk of revenue and profit comes from Windows and Office may suggest slow growth , thus the discounted P/E . (Uncertainty) But sales in its commercial division grew 5% to $12.8 billion in the fiscal third quarter.And that commercial cloud revenue grew 106% - the seventh consecutive quarter of triple-digit growth - shows how quickly Nadella is expanding Microsoft's cloud capabilities.This means Microsoft's commercial cloud segment, which comes from its enterprise Office 365 product, Dynamic CRM Online as well as its Azure cloud business, is now its fastest-growing segment .While the company doesn't reveal exact quarterly or annual revenue figures for the cloud business, Microsoft does say that the segment is running at an annualized rate of $6.3 billion.Amazon ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) on Thursday finally revealed its dominant AWS cloud segment generates $ 5 billion annually . Wall Street responded , sending Amazon shares up 13 % in after-hours .
