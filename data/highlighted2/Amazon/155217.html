By retransmission revenue , here I 'm referring to content sold to companies like Netflix and Amazon . As the number of streaming niche content channels or individual series grow, there will be spending from a large number of viewers on them.Under a recession environment, that means choices will have to be made as to what content to continue to pay for and which to abandon.What that suggests is a recession will hasten the disruptive process , which will then reveal the depth of the content disruption and how consumers view the niche channels or series against general content providers . (Uncertainty) Content subscription options will continue to grow even as the economy slows.How I see that playing out is further fragmentation of the video industry , even if some of the larger , emerging content producers are acquired by traditional media , as some already have been , such as Maker Studios and AwesomenessTV . (Surprise) The performance of ESPN is definitely a bellwether of the traditional broadcast industry, and I find no problem with investors getting concerned in light of it losing a "moderate" number of subscribers.It has even brought a response from Disney that it is open to considering offering it as a standalone service.It has mentioned that in the past , but in a more casual way that suggested it would be a long way off . (Surprise & Uncertainty) I don't think the pace of the disruption will allow Disney to be lethargic with ESPN.While that points to cord cutting , the metric to watch is ad spend ; the two are of course directly correlated , because if there are less eyeballs watching , obviously brands will pay less for advertising on TV . (Surprise & Uncertainty) They already are.The other metric is how much a particular company is exposed to advertising.For example , Time Warner ( NYSE : TWX ) is in a very strong position there , with approximately 16 percent of its revenue coming from ads . (Uncertainty) Consequently , it 'll be much less susceptible to performance surprises than those media companies with much more ad exposure . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Also look to see what management is saying about lowering ad exposure . (Uncertainty) Finally , two forces heading for a collision are the demand for a la carte and the industries conviction it may not survive if it does n't maintain a content bundle in some form , even if it is n't what is being offered now . (Uncertainty) That resistance from the industry comes from what happened when music albums were unbundled.Streaming content and subscription options are going to continue to soar.Now we 'll have to wait and watch to see if traditional media companies , and even new players like giant Netflix will be able to hold their models together as the number of individual stars and series explode in number on the Internet .
