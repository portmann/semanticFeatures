(Uncertainty) Let's focus on increasing DCG revenues.I'll divide this discussion into two parts: 1.)Increases due to industry growth; and 2.)Increases due to better products.First, some basics.Intel's DCG sales in 2014 were worth $14.4 billion.We don't know much more than that from Intel data that's publicly available.There's no financial breakdown among parts of the data center (e.g., processor, SSD, communications).We also don't know how much is sold to OEMs and how much is sold direct to companies that make their own data center servers.We know squat because Intel's ability to compete would be damaged if the competition knew how much Intel sold of what to whom for how much.We do know some information on pricing and global industry-wide sales.Intel provides details of the prices for the various products that it sells, but we don't know how many of each it sells.IDC and Gartner provide information on how many servers are sold, and a split between non-x86 and x86 units.If I guess at an average price of $1,500 (likely higher) per processor, and using IDC/Gartner data indicating 5.6 million Intel servers are sold each year, I arrive at $8.4 billion of $14.4 billion overall DCG sales are from processor sales.Seems low . (Uncertainty) How about $2,000 per server and $11.2 billion is from processor sales?The rest, between $3 billion and $6 billion, is then from other products sold into data centers.Given the gross level of real data, it looks like we are stuck with overall adjustments to DCG earnings.If connected devices are growing from 6 billion to 50 billion in six years , the annual growth rate for data centers is approximately 50 % . (Uncertainty) That's huge.This 50% per year growth is being reduced by data center infrastructure efficiency/utilization improvements.If the company's programs increase the efficiency/utilization of data centers by 30% per year, then the net growth in data center expansion is 20% per year.Assuming that Intel 's market share stays the same , then its DCG sales should grow 20 % per year . (Uncertainty) This is pretty close to the company's guidance of 15% per year.If Intel, by increasing data center efficiency/utilization by 30% per year, is able to increase average selling prices by 10% per year, then the company's DCG profits increase 30% per year.This is pretty close to what happened over the past year, with sales increasing 18% and profits increasing 31%.In addition to data center growth (18%/year) and increased profits (30%/year), we need to add Intel's increasing share of sales to data centers... this is not an increase in processor sales, but an increase in sales of other components of the data center.With a larger share of data center sales, the company's profits get even larger.Intel has been quietly buying companies that increase Intel's ability to provide all the products needed to build a complete data center.With this approach , Intel 's $ 14.4 billion in DCG market share could increase to be a larger portion of the $ 50 billion IDC identifies as being spent annually on servers , and a larger percentage of the $ 120 billion to be spent on data center infrastructure over the next several years .
