One of Intel 's strategies is to accelerate disruption of the way we do business by enabling digital services that allow companies like Netflix ( NASDAQ : NFLX ) , Airbnb , Uber , Amazon ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) and Facebook ( NASDAQ : FB ) to exist and thrive . These companies are only possible because of innovations led by Intel 's low-cost computing technology . (Uncertainty) The cloud allowed these services to exist , and in turn , the services these companies provide are increasing the demand for a larger cloud infrastructure . (Surprise) This is the essence of Javon's paradox.Today 's IT challenges : 2-3 weeks to provision new servers ( networking , storage , processors ) , 66 % annual growth in mobile data traffic , 40 % annual growth in storage , 90 % of data is unstructured , server utilization less than 50 % . (Surprise) The current data centers are insufficient - not just size, but structure needs to change dramatically.Intel provides a top-to-bottom solution stack : Workload optimized silicon , breaking out of the box with new ways to manage data center throughput and storage , optimize delivery of data center services , unlock the data vault . (Uncertainty) The company innovates functions and then integrates them into the silicon processor.It provides a full range of processors for the data center: ATOM, XEON, XEON PHI.All of these have a place in the data center to meet specific customer needs.Another focus of Intel is on Rack Scale Architecture (RSA).The key to RSA is an orchestration layer that allows the application (the app) to define the virtual network, virtual processor and virtual memory that are needed for that application.Intel is working with partners to allow the data center to automatically, in seconds, spin up or down or sideways to meet the demands of millions of applications, save power and increase data center utilization.Resources are pooled, and each application gets the resources it needs at that instant from all resources in the data center.Whether a data center builds or buys servers , the keys include performance , security and workload optimization . (Uncertainty) New data center infrastructure powered by Intel cloud technology can increase performance and optimization by about 30 % from systems from just a few years ago . (Uncertainty) The cloud is now a whole new platform for computing.First, the platform was mainframes, then desktop computers, then networks with desktops, and then the networks were expanded with laptops, smartphones and tablets.Now, the cloud is the latest platform for computing across all devices.In 2014, Intel invested over $800 million in companies that accelerate Big Data analytics.Intel sees that it not only needs to develop silicon, but enable an architecture that's robust, modular, scalable and efficient to handle cloud growth.Then , the company optimizes those services to ensure data centers can easily build a cloud , deploy the cloud and monetize Big Data services . (Uncertainty) Intel sees that its work to accelerate development of improved cloud services is best for its server OEMs, direct data center clients, the clients served by data centers... everyone.Remember that over $120 billion will be spent on cloud infrastructure over the next several years.Diane Bryant, general manager of Intel's Data Center Group, makes it clear that software-defined infrastructure is a key element of Intel's strategy in the data center world.In essence, software-defined infrastructure allows one application to run across thousands of servers.This approach makes custom processor solutions a very effective way to have one application run efficiently on numerous processors.A big step in this direction is Field Programmable Gate Array (FPGA) married to a Xeon processor package.This package can double the performance of a processor related to a specific application.An FPGA is flexible and reprogrammable on the fly as application requirements change.An FPGA is an on-ramp to custom processor solutions.Once shown to be effective, an FPGA/Xeon can be duplicated in hundreds or thousands of custom processors.An FPGA also can be deployed broadly in a data center to allow reprogramming of an FPGA to accelerate high-demand functions as the demand for functions change.More on FPGAs can be found here . (Surprise) Utilization of compute resources in a data center today is often 50 % or less . (Surprise) This utilization can be increased to 80% by rack scale solutions and software-defined infrastructure.Both public cloud and telco service providers can avail themselves of this increase in utilization by using the latest Intel data center products.Some say that software-defined architecture works against Intel profits, since it reduces the number of processors needed to process the same throughput.Intel 's perspective is the same as with Moore 's Law : process more data for lower cost , and thereby increase sales and margins . (Surprise) Intel's approach to competition includes the idea that, "Only the paranoid survive."This, then, is carried out in the following approach: Whatever it is, it needs to run best on Intel.Yes, data center people are looking for a second source of data center processors - customers will evaluate new sources.The competitive advantage of Intel is its constant development of improved, lower-cost technology, and the fact that in a data center, a homogeneous environment (everything running on Intel architecture) always results in the best overall performance and reliability.Amazon 's Data Center is as an example is sticking with Intel , because competitors `` are just not moving fast enough , '' the cost savings are `` not quite there , '' and `` Intel is more receptive '' to meeting Amazon 's custom needs .
