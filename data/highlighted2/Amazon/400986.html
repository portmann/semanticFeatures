Ford Delivers A Strong April - Ford Motor Company (NYSE:F) | Seeking Alpha Ford posted a 4% increase in U.S. auto sales during April 2016 compared to April 2015, which came in slightly below estimates.After a slow month in January, the Mustang, F-Series and other SUVs have each posted impressive results the past few months - boosting sales numbers as well as average transaction prices.The company certainly faces challenges in 2016 , but given the strength of the automotive industry and the weak valuation , I 'm optimistic on Ford 's 2016 . (Uncertainty) Ford ( NYSE : F ) announced April 2016 U.S. vehicle sales of nearly 231K , up 4 % from the same month in 2015 . (Uncertainty) This low-single-digit increase came in slightly below the 6.5% estimate from Kelley Blue Book.The increase comes a month after Ford posted an 8% increase and two months after posting the leading increase of 20% among all of the automakers in February.After a mediocre January, Ford has been steadily posting positive if not record-breaking monthly U.S. sales results.Equally as important , the company increased its average transaction price at a steady 2.1 % to $ 36,541 due to the continued success of its larger vehicle offerings . (Surprise) Looking at the industry-wide results , the seasonally-adjusted annualized rate is between 17.32 million and 17.42 million vehicles after falling to 16.57 million vehicles after March.Competitors such as Honda (NYSE: HMC ) (up 14.4%), Nissan ( OTCPK:NSANY ) (up 12.8%), Fiat Chrysler (NYSE: FCAU ) (up 6%) and Toyota (NYSE: TM ) (up 3.8%) posted solid increases while General Motors (NYSE: GM ) (down 3.5%) saw the only decrease.Furthermore, in the critical pickup truck market, Ford's F-Series posted a 13% increase for the month after sales totaled 186K in the first quarter, which represented the best first-quarter start in a decade without any signs of slowing.The model is coming off a busy 2015 where it underwent a drastic change to an aluminum body starting this year, and has seen tight inventory levels so far as the company has worked to configure its manufacturing plants.Recently, Ford announced that it will idle an F-150 plant in Kansas City for a week to address a couple of issues that have prevented the facility from running at full production capacity.While it's never good to see a company facing manufacturing issues, especially after the issues the company had last summer, it is good to see management addressing the issue.It also explains why the days of inventory supply is at 105.As I've said before, the success of the F-150 is critical to Ford's turnaround.This slow start to 2016 , in addition to other possible threats such as the overall health of the US economy , further interest rate hikes and emergence of new competitors in the auto industry , are weighing heavily on investor sentiment .
