Wal-Mart's Crisis Creates Opportunity For Contrarians - Wal-Mart Stores, Inc. (NYSE:WMT) | Seeking Alpha There's blood in the aisles at Wal-Mart.Shares have cratered 35% since the beginning of the year, making the retailer a textbook example of a deep value play.A trifecta of catalysts have the potential to boost profits at Wal-Mart : smaller Neighborhood Market outlets , e-commerce initiatives and a rapidly approaching holiday season . (Surprise) Wal-Mart shares offer 30% upside and a 3% dividend yield.I have a confession to make.I've never been too keen on retail sector investments.In fact, I've mostly avoided them.Something about sourcing widgets for 4 cents and selling them for a nickel has never appealed to me as an investor.The title of my least favorite retailer in my least favorite sector has long belonged to Wal-Mart (NYSE: WMT ).The stores have always had a scorched Earth vibe to me.Like big-box capitalism run amok.A refuge for squatters and looters fleeing the zombie apocalypse just outside the doors.But I digress... Wal-Mart is changing my mind.I recently visited one of the new smaller-format Neighborhood Markets near my home in Florida and found myself almost . (Uncertainty) .(gulp) ..impressed.Someone turned on the color!The floors were clean!!And it didn't smell like a truck stop!!!Whether or not my reaction was a function of low expectations remains to be seen.I know this though: win, lose or draw, this is a company clearly determined to invest in its operations and identity.By all means necessary.The new concept is not going to do anything for margins at Wal-Mart.In order to pull it off, prices must remain just as low as they are at Supercenters.This long game here is market share, and my experience is a perfect example, since the shopping center I visited also has a Publix, a Target (NYSE: TGT ), and a Walgreens (NASDAQ: WBA ).Publix is a hugely popular grocer in Florida, but the prices are outrageous; the Target is too big to dip into just to pick up dinner; and Walgreens doesn't have food.Guess which store just became my go-to bounce for food and toilet paper!Moody 's expects Neighborhood Markets to eventually outnumber Supercenters , and with more than five Supercenters currently in operation to every one Neighborhood Market locations , this upscaling of the down market is clearly in its infancy . (Surprise) While it 's impossible to under-estimate the retail threat from Amazon ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) , until I can beam a salad down from the cloud , the online juggernaut does n't pose a threat to the new concept . The Supercenters are a different story.They are , in fact , endangered by Amazon , and this is one of the main reasons Wal-Mart 's share price has been crushed this year . I 'm actually surprised it did n't happen sooner as the writing 's been on the wall for years . (Surprise) But Amazon has its proverbial irons in a lot of fires and Amazon Fresh notwithstanding , the company has no designs on being your neighborhood market . Nor should it with so many higher margin opportunities in the works . (Surprise) Still, that doesn't mean Wal-Mart is prepared to cede its legacy business to Bezos and company.If it looks familiar that 's because Wal-Mart has shamelessly embraced the best that Amazon has to offer to enhance its own web presence . Amazon has all but perfected the web user experience and if it ai n't broke , why fix it ? The new interface is simple , clean and optimized across input mechanisms ( ie . (Uncertainty) PC, phone, tablet), and developers are working to increase the relevancy and frequency of personalized item recommendations throughout the site.The real key , however , to Wal-Mart differentiating itself from Amazon is to embrace the omnichannel ( buzzword alert ! ) consumer by leveraging its 5200 Supercenters to offer instant gratification via in-store pickup.After all , right now is faster than 2-day shipping with Amazon Prime , as long as you do n't mind the drive . (Amazon: Surprise) Early results are encouraging as Wal-Mart claims a 300 % increase in store pickups during a July 15 sale in response to Amazon Prime Day , and it may have even come out the winner overall among Prime Day shoppers who appreciated the deals available . (Amazon: Uncertainty) Still, it's early innings and success is far from guaranteed.Wal-Mart has to find a way to bring its shipping charges down to Earth, while maintaining the super low prices its known for.They also have to keep the behind-the-scenes infrastructure humming and make it all feel effortless.Even something as simple as the loading speed of pages can cut into revenues and see consumers lose confidence . (Uncertainty) The next real test for Wal-Mart is rapidly approaching - the 2015 holiday shopping season - and the retailer is already capitalizing on a potentially huge profit driver in the release of Disney's new "Star Wars" film.It's impossible to miss the excitement felt within and among generations as December 18 beckons.My 5-year-old nephew doesn't know Jabba the Hut from Boba Fett and still went out as Darth Vader for Halloween; his mother just got him a ridiculously cute R2D2 backpack; Games Radar calls the Stars Wars videogame Battlefront "a relentless, giddy, air-punch generator;" and as for the movie itself, let's just say the hype is about to hit lightspeed.Target and Wal-Mart both participated in September 4th's Force Friday retail event.Wal-Mart went all out, rolling out new Star Wars merchandise at 2900 of its 4000 U.S. stores, while Target only hosted events at 207 of its 1799 U.S. stores.Wal-Mart understands the resurgence of toys , which are increasingly the domain of mainline retailers not specialty shops.Videogame maker Electronic Arts (NASDAQ: EA ) just upped its sales expectations for Battlefront from 10 million to 13 million units, and 3000 Wal-Mart locations are hosting an "Epic Weekend" November 13 - 15 during which time shoppers will be able to play the game before it's released.A dozen other Star Wars items will be available exclusively at Wal-Mart, as well as the Disney Infinity 3.0 Star Wars bundle for PlayStation 4.I don't mean to paint a rosy picture of the future for bricks-and-mortar retailers.The arrival of Amazon is akin to the asteroid that struck down the dinosaurs 65 million years ago . The herd is destined to thin in coming years.The amount of retail floor space will be a fraction of its former self.There's simply too much of it in a world where so many needs are met online.But they're not all going away.There 's room for more than one and the largest retailer in the world certainly has a shot at being one of the survivors .
