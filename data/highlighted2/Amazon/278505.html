Netflix : 2016 Outlook - Slight Chance Of Shower - Netflix , Inc. ( NASDAQ : NFLX ) | Seeking Alpha Dec. 31 , 2015 7:03 PM ET | About : Netflix , Inc. ( NFLX ) , Includes : AMZN , DIS , DISH , GOOG , GOOGL , LGF , SNE , TWX by : WestEnd511 Competitive outlook the key risk for NFLX in 2016 . (Uncertainty) Expect domestic subs growth to be subdued while international subs to be steady.Netflix (NASDAQ: NFLX ) certainly had a good year in 2015 with the stock up +139% vs. the NASDAQ's +7%.At 250x forward earnings , the current valuation is priced for perfection as we head into what I believe will be a rocky 2016 for the company as growing competitive risk will weigh in on domestic subs growth and slower-than-expected international penetration could impact the company 's overseas growth plan . (Uncertainty) Although OTT companies are positioned to benefit from the cord cutting/shaving trend we saw among the US cable companies in 2015, it is important for investors to distinguish that NFLX is only one of many OTT products available in a maturing US market.That said , I would be cautious on NFLX in 2016 and prefer to stick with the content owners , namely Disney ( NYSE : DIS ) , Lions Gate ( NYSE : LGF ) and Time Warner ( NYSE : TWX ) rather than the distribution pipes . (Uncertainty) First , NFLX 's Q3 domestic subs miss could be the start of a structural trend due to the growing competitive environment in the company 's domestic market from rivals including Hulu , Amazon ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) , HBO and web TV alternatives including Sony ( NYSE : SNE ) Vue and Sling TV ( NASDAQ : DISH ) . (Amazon: Uncertainty) The US is perhaps the most mature and OTT saturated market in the world driven by the shift in viewer habits that resulted in diminishing viewership of traditional media . (Uncertainty) NFLX was certainly the leader with this trend and is one of few companies that successfully migrated its business model to digital.Others such as Blockbuster were not so lucky.Although NFLX had the first mover advantage, the US market is becoming increasingly saturated and I see NFLX gradually losing its competitive advantage and appeal as viewers migrate to alternative platforms.With an estimated 2.5% of US TV household cutting the cord and migrating to OTT services, US viewers have more OTT options than before and the availability of competing services does not bode well for NFLX which was widely considered the OTT platform before the cord-cutting trend materializes.Second, not only the number of competitors has multiplied, but also the quality of competition has increased with YouTube (NASDAQ: GOOG ) (NASDAQ: GOOGL ), Hulu and HBO being the three likely candidates to grab NFLX's subs share in the near term while AMZN has the shot of taking shares in the long term.YouTube is perhaps the biggest threat to NFLX with over a billion monthly active users on the platform , which has the monetization capability of either ad based and/or subscription based . (Uncertainty) The billion plus user along allows YouTube to negotiate favourable content deals similar to how NFLX was able to leverage its ~30m or so subs to negotiate key contents such as EPIX a few years back.Besides YouTube , HBO is perhaps the second biggest threat to NFLX with roughly 138m cable subscribers globally and an estimated 1-1 .5 m HBO GO subs . (Uncertainty) At $14.99/month, HBO GO is priced above NFLX, but many would argue that its original programming is one of, if not, the best out there.With HBO finally becoming a standalone platform, those NFLX subs who prefer HBO can no churn off Netflix and move onto HBO while still maintaining a reasonable cable bill.Hulu is another interesting platform that is positioned to replace NFLX among the mainstream audience.Content from Disney ( DIS ) , Fox ( NASDAQ : FOXA ) , Comcast ( NASDAQ : CMCSA ) and Time Warner already makes it a competitive platform , and I believe that it is matter of time before DIS decides to stream ESPN on Hulu given the eroding ESPN subs base over the past two years . (Uncertainty) In my view, a decision by DIS to distribute ESPN content on Hulu such as live streaming would be the biggest negative for NFLX.Conclusion , competitive risk is certainly looming on NFLX , and as an investor , I would be cautious on all of the content distributors given their lack of technological edge .
