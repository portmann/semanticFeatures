Potential return , in the site 's terminology , is a high-end estimate of how a security will perform over the next several months , and the site calculated a potential return of 10 % for Amazon , so we 'll use that as its cap . As of Friday 's close , this was the optimal collar to hedge 400 shares of AMZN against a greater-than-15 % drop by mid-July , while not capping an investor 's upside at less than 10 % . (Surprise & Uncertainty) As you can see in the first part of the optimal collar above , the cost of the put leg was $ 7,640 , or 3.44 % , as a percentage of position value . (Uncertainty) But if you look at the second part of the collar below , you 'll see the income generated by selling the call leg was slightly more , $ 10,380 , or 4.67 % of position value . (Surprise) So the net cost of this optimal collar was negative , meaning an investor would have collected $ 2,740 more from selling the call leg than he paid for the puts , an amount equal to 1.23 % of his position value . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Two notes on this collar hedge : To be conservative , the cost of the optimal collar was calculated using the ask price of the puts and the bid price of the calls ; in practice , an investor can often buy puts for less and sell calls for more ( again , at some price between the bid and the ask ) . (Surprise & Uncertainty) So , in reality , an investor would likely have collected more than $ 2,740 when opening this collar . (Surprise) Although the threshold on this collar is 15%, and thresholds are calculated to take into account positive hedging costs, they don't take into account negative hedging costs.So , the worst-case scenario in this case , when taking into account the negative hedging cost , would be a decline of less than 15 % : assuming , conservatively , a negative cost of 1.23 % , the maximum drawdown here would be 15 % - 1.23 % = 13.77 % .
