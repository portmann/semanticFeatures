They are aware ( though are loath to admit ) that Amazon has eaten into their consumer sales , and that online shopping is the future trend . Their Wal-Mart Labs division has done much to enhance the retailer's online operations with apps such as Shopycat, and are continuing to develop other methods of enhancing Wal-Mart's e-commerce.Investment in e-commerce is one part of Wal-Mart's effort to improve its operations going forward.Investment in their staff is the other key aspect.For years, Wal-Mart has been criticized as paying its staff poorly.Founder Sam Walton was quoted as saying : "I pay low wages.I can take advantage of that.We're going to be successful, but the basis is a very low-wage, low-benefit model of employment."The increase in employee wages to $ 10 per hour and provision of better training is an effort to break with the negative public image that the above quote typifies , boost staff morale and reduce staff turnover . (Surprise) Workers will receive better paid time-off and more advanced notice of their shifts . (Surprise) The cost of this investment will amount to $1.5 billion this year.In consequence, earnings per share will be reduced and quarterly reports will reflect this.Short-term investors reacted badly to the initial consequences of Wal-Mart 's long-term strategy in October , and there is every likelihood that they will do so again and again so long as it takes Wal-Mart 's strategy to pay off . (Uncertainty) Long-term investors who missed the opportunity to initiate a position with the company during the sell-off will be able to do so in the near future.After all , the stock price may fluctuate , but that only reflects the mood of the market , not the long-term health of the company . (Uncertainty) And whatever happens with Wal-Mart the stock, Wal-Mart the company remains the largest retailer in the world.It generates more in sales than Amazon ( $ 101 billion ) , Costco ( NASDAQ : COST ) ( $ 117 billion ) and Target ( NYSE : TGT ) ( $ 74 billion ) combined . (Amazon: Surprise) It can still pressure suppliers to lower costs on goods and make up the difference on bulk sales, which benefits customers and ensures repeat custom, reinforcing that pressure.The likely recession that many anticipate also makes Wal-Mart an ideal investment candidate - its reputation as a low-cost retailer means that customers are more likely to shop there when times are difficult than would otherwise be the case . (Surprise & Uncertainty) A recession , then , will not adversely affect Wal-Mart in the same manner it would affect more ` upmarket ' retailers . (Surprise) Wariness of the stock due to short-term issues is , therefore , entirely unwarranted .
