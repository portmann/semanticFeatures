Why Isn't Anybody Shorting Apple?- Apple Inc. (NASDAQ:AAPL) | Seeking Alpha Short interest dropped another 9.8 million shares.Apple now one of least shorted large-cap tech names.A couple of weeks ago , I detailed how the sentiment around shares of Apple ( NASDAQ : AAPL ) was not what you might think . (Uncertainty) Despite all of the bearishness regarding iPhone sales and falling fiscal Q2 revenues, short interest was at a multi-year low.Recently, the end of February update on short interest came in, and the trend of short sellers covering has continued in a large way.In fact, Apple is now one of the least shorted tech giants in the market today.In my previous article, I mentioned how short interest for Apple was near its lowest point that I've seen since I started tracking this item in April 2012.Well, another 9.8 million shares were covered by the end of February.With short interest now under 50 million shares, I had to adjust the y-axis of the short interest chart, seen below.The latest short interest number marks a new low , the least amount of shares short in roughly 4 years ( split-adjusted ) . (Uncertainty) Source: NASDAQ Apple short interest page The number of shares short is a single data point, so there are many ways to look at it.One primary way I do is in terms of the stock's float, the number of shares actually available for trading.According to Yahoo! Finance , Apple 's float is 5.54 billion shares , meaning about 0.86 % of Apple 's float is short , less than 1 in every 100 shares . (Surprise) In the overall market, this number is relatively low.When you look at some high-growth areas , the 3D printing sector or stocks such as GoPro ( NASDAQ : GPRO ) and Tesla Motors ( NASDAQ : TSLA ) , you can see times when 20 % , 30 % or more of the float is short . (Surprise) But to do a realistic comparison for Apple, we really should look at some other major technology names.In the chart below , I did just that , comparing Apple against Alphabet ( NASDAQ : GOOG ) ( NASDAQ : GOOGL ) , Microsoft ( NASDAQ : MSFT ) , IBM ( NYSE : IBM ) , Intel ( NASDAQ : INTC ) , Qualcomm ( NASDAQ : QCOM ) , Cisco Systems ( NASDAQ : CSCO ) , Amazon ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) , Facebook ( NASDAQ : FB ) and Oracle ( NYSE : ORCL ) . ( Note : Settlement date for short interest of names above is either 2/29 or 3/1 , depending on NASDAQ reporting date . ) (Uncertainty) For the ten stocks above, the straight average comes to 1.38% of float shorted.If we take out Apple which is well below that, the nine-stock peer group average comes to 1.44%.This means Apple is about 40 % less shorted than average , which is a considerable amount . (Surprise) Only Microsoft is less shorted than Apple , and those are the only two stocks below 1.00 % . (Surprise) Of the group above, Apple is one of four stocks estimated to show both revenue and earnings declines in its current fiscal period .Oracle is in a similar revenue situation to Apple, although Apple isn't expected to see as much of an EPS decline.IBM and Qualcomm are forecast to see even greater top and bottom line declines . (Surprise) Of these four, Apple has the best buyback program at the moment, while IBM and Qualcomm have the highest dividend yields.Microsoft , the only stock less shorted than Apple , is expected to see a revenue decline in its current fiscal year , but EPS are forecast to jump . (Surprise) In the second half of February, there was a bit of news regarding Apple other than the FBI phone saga.Apple launched another bond offering , probably for helping the buyback plan , although the coupon rates were a bit higher than normally hoped . (Surprise & Uncertainty) On the negative side , recent Apple bull Carl Icahn trimmed his position in the stock , selling nearly seven million shares . (Uncertainty) Additionally, SMBC Nikko cut its iPhone demand forecast by 30 million units after another reported round of production cutbacks .Given mostly negative news , you might have expected short interest to increase for Apple , but it went the other way . (Uncertainty) Apple shares traded higher as the month ended , and have regained the $ 100 mark here in March . (Surprise) So, I ask the key question again in regards to Apple: where are all of the short sellers?I continue to hear iPhone sales are struggling, revenues and earnings are going to decline in the period, etc.However , short interest in the stock is actually declining , showing that investors do n't believe the stock is headed lower . (Surprise & Uncertainty) The number of shares short is at its lowest point in the nearly four years I 've tracked for Apple .
