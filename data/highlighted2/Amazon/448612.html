Will Amazon Stock Buckle Under The Weight Of Its Price To Book Ratio ? - Amazon.com , Inc. ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) | Seeking Alpha Amazon shares are currently trading near their highest price to book ratio during the past five years . Amazon shares typically drop in the months following a relatively high P/B ratio . Amazon ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) shares are currently trading near their all-time highs of $ 728.24 per share ( recorded on 6/2/2016 ) , currently at about $ 725 per share , as of 11:27 AM , July 1 , 2016 . With a resulting market capitalization of about $ 342 billion , and total shareholder equity of about $ 14.76 billion at the end of Q1 2016 , Amazon 's current stock price translates to a price to book ratio ( P/B ) of about 23.2 . Amazon stock price from 2000 to 2016 - Source : Yahoo Finance During the past five years , the highest P/B ratio reached for Amazon was about 26.17 on 12/29/2015 . Following that , Amazon shares dropped from about $ 693.97 to $ 482.07 on 2/9/2016 , a drop of about 30.5 % . Prior to that , Amazon P/B ratio had reached a local high of 20.37 on 12/26/2013 , when Amazon stock was trading at about $ 404.29 . During the following several months , Amazon shares dropped to $ 292.24 by 5/5/2014 , a drop of about 27.8 % . Click to enlargeAmazon price to book ratio ( P/B ) - Source : Y-Charts We believe that downside risk for Amazon shares is quite elevated at this stage not only from a P/B ratio perspective but also from a P/E perspective . (Amazon: Uncertainty) With analysts ' earnings estimates of $ 5.31 and $ 9.91 respectively for the years ending in Dec. 2016 and Dec. 2017 , Amazon is currently trading at a forward P/E ratio of 136.5 and 73.16 , respectively . Meanwhile , Amazon is trading at a 12-month trailing P/E ratio of 298.8 . Many admirers of Amazon continuously point to the fact that such numbers are less important than Amazon 's revenue growth , whereby Amazon is expected to grow its revenues by 20.9 % from $ 134.1 billion in 2016 to $ 162.1 billion in 2017 . (Amazon: Surprise) In its latest Q1 2016 quarter , Amazon international sales of about $ 9.6 billion accounted for about 32.9 % of Amazon 's total sales of $ 29.1 billion . Such international sales grew by 23.5% from the prior year.Given current macro headwinds relating to Brexit , stronger dollar and upcoming presidential U.S. elections , Amazon 's international revenues growth is likely to face challenges during the next several months . (Amazon: Surprise) Meanwhile , any spillover into US financial markets will likely reduce consumption by US consumers as well , hence presenting an additional hurdle to Amazon sales growth . Finally , although falling fuel prices have provided a boost to consumers ' disposable income during the past four quarters , oil prices have stabilized during the past few months and are expected to increase , hence possibly reducing future consumer disposable income . (Uncertainty) As per the below graph, average US gas prices had dropped from as high as $2.81/gal.in June 2015 to about $1.69/gal.in February 2016, but have increased to about $2.25 during the past four months.Average retail gas price in U.S.A. - Source : Gasbuddy.com Given Amazon 's elevated valuation both from the perspective of price to book ratio , as well as price to earnings ratio , in addition to macro economic headwinds related to Brexit , U.S. elections a stronger dollar , in addition to the negative effect on consumers ' disposable income by the recent increase in gas prices , we believe Amazon shares can drop noticeably from current levels during the next several months .
