Why Amazon Books ? Beyond The Obvious - Amazon.com , Inc. ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) | Seeking Alpha Amazon finally introduces its first Amazon Books store . Initial response has been positive , and there are sure to be more opened in the years ahead . (Surprise) What needs to be seen is how much the costs of these concept stores will be offset by in-store sales.Amazon.com ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) has come full circle with its book business , having recently done the unthinkable : opening its first physical book store . The immediate questions investors have on their minds is this: what is this all about and what does it mean?That is important because Amazon , while not getting into specifics , has said it has plans to build more Amazon Books stores . (Amazon: Surprise) As for the store itself , it 's interesting in that Amazon did get creative with the book section of the store by placing the books with their covers out , rather than the spines being out , as most book stores have the majority of them displayed . Source: Rob Salkowitz via Publisher Weekly It also includes a technology section with products like Kindles, Fire TV, Fire Tablets and Echo.My initial belief was that was the primary reason for the store, with books being props to fill up the store with merchandise.I still believe that 's a part of the plan , but a smaller part than I originally thought . (Surprise & Uncertainty) What 's interesting to me is Amazon has tied the physical merchandise in the store to the moving prices online . Customers can scan the bar codes to get a current price, which means there is a fluidity in that regard.It mimics the online buying experience.It also includes details of online customer reviews and sales information on placards near the books.Located in Seattle 's University Village , I expect that will be the model used by Amazon in the future - placing these physical stores near universities . With regard to the books stocked in the store , only those with a rating of four stars or higher , good sales and significant pre-orders are stocked there . (Surprise) Those with a smartphone Amazon app can scan the bar codes right there to get an up-to-date price . Again , what the price is on Amazon.com is what the price of the book and other products in the store will be . Credits available on a regular Amazon account also can be used in the store for purchases . It 's obvious Amazon wants to make it as friction-free as possible to make a transaction . (Amazon: Uncertainty) One thing that suggests this is going to be projected with very targeted locations is Amazon , at least not at this time , is n't going to allow this to be a fulfillment center where you can order online and then pick up the products at the store . (Amazon: Uncertainty) If it was rolling out something like that in the form of this book store, it wouldn't have included those restrictions.That is something different that will be expanded under a different format.With books as the bulk of the products in the store and a centrally located tech display , it 's obvious to me this is about the experience associated with Amazon and not about making money . It's also different than Apple's (NASDAQ: AAPL ) physical stores, although I think some of the same reasons for introducing them are the same.Neither are looking for it to be a major source of revenue , but more a public relations and marketing strategy . (Surprise) When Steve Jobs decided to introduce concept stores for Apple , he did it primarily because he believed retail outlets were n't marketing the Mac in the way it should have been . (Uncertainty) Interestingly, Apple Stores now make a lot of money, but I don't think that was the primary motivation of Jobs and Apple.It 's also different for Amazon Books because it 's selling items with far less margin and earnings potential than Apple does at its stores . (Amazon: Surprise) For that reason I see this as a loss leader for Amazon where sales will help offset some of the expenses but are unlikely to be profitable . Looking at the interior of the store and product balance , it 's apparent to me Amazon built the store as a reflection of its roots , while at the same time offering the tech gadgets in an attempt to expand that market . (Amazon: Uncertainty) While not an Apple knockoff, it does have a feel of that within that particular part of the store.Being able to interact with tech products like Kindles , the Echo , the Fire TV and Fire Tablets is a great marketing tool and when eventually leveraged across strategically located stores , it could be a good catalyst to expand those markets . (Uncertainty) It already does well with Kindles, but some of the other tech products have been slow to be embraced by consumers.Since Amazon sells just about every type of product available to the retail market , it 's instructive to see it include tech products within the book store and not other products . Book competitors like Barnes & Noble (NYSE: BKS ) already do this, and it provides a concept already familiar to consumers.If this part of the business helps to increase sales for its slow-moving tech products , it could significantly help to offset costs in its physical retail store strategy . (Uncertainty) I believe this is almost entirely a new way of marketing for Amazon as it looks for ways to provide a more public face to the company . (Amazon: Surprise & Uncertainty) There 's a reason Amazon went with a book store and not a tech store like Apple : it 's all about the perceived experience . At one time Amazon was watched with awe and fascination as it disrupted the giant book and retail industry , providing a better experience and better prices for consumers . (Amazon: Surprise) Over time , as the company grew , it gravitated to being considered another giant player in the retail market , which tends to give consumers and the public in general , a more negative view of the company . (Surprise) A physical book store, being the choice of its public face to consumers and potential customers, is the best choice because of the atmosphere associated with a book store, which readers enjoy basking in.Tech stores can be a lot of fun, but they present a different atmosphere than a book store does.The book store concept is more conducive to what Amazon needs at this time . (Amazon: Surprise) The point is Amazon Books should be considered a marketing and branding tool by investors . Anything more that may come out of it would only be icing on the cake . (Surprise & Uncertainty) I would be happy to see sales offset expenses.It doesn't matter to me if it were to add to the top and bottom lines.So far I like everything associated with Amazon Books . The obvious interest by the media has produced a lot of buzz , primarily because of the way Amazon disrupted the physical book industry , and is now going that route itself . That has already paid major marketing dividends for Amazon , and should continue to do so . I do n't see this becoming some major expansion by Amazon , based upon my belief it 's a concept store for the purpose of branding and marketing . While it could generate some impulse sales , most consumers making purchases would probably have made those purchases online as well . (Uncertainty) It's only changing how the sales are made, not the number of sales.Once the data come in concerning how consumers are using the store and tweaks are made , I do believe there will be new stores introduced in areas where they should do very well . (Uncertainty) Since that will almost certainly be near to universities , that will help maintain the interest of and attract a younger demographic that did n't grow up during the time Amazon was battling it out with the giant retailers for market share . (Amazon: Surprise & Uncertainty) What I want to see is the costs that this project will eventually incur as more stores are rolled out , and how much of those expenses will be offset by store sales . (Surprise) Other than that , it 's hard to measure the value this will eventually have for Amazon , assuming they become popular in the areas they do business in . (Amazon: Uncertainty) Once there are enough stores operating, we can at that time at least measure the increase of sales to give an idea of the marketing tool is working.That will take years.For now, this has already garnered a lot of attention, and it's simple and easy to understand.In that regard it's already a success, and most responses to the physical store have been very positive.We only use your contact details to reply to your request for more information .
