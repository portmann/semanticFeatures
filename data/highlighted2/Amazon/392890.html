Amazon
Amazon over the next year intends to shift the scope of TrueBlue services from serving U.S. fulfillment centers to serving Canadian fulfillment centers and providing for U.S. delivery stations , which are the smaller package sorting centers closer to the final delivery location . The development is significant on many counts.First , this $ 175 million is almost half of the revenue that the company was expecting from Amazon . (Amazon: Uncertainty) Secondly , considering it may take a few quarters to ramp down and a few quarters to ramp up at other sites , expecting any growth comeback during this year may be ambitious . (Uncertainty) Third, the development highlights the weakness in the business model itself, i.e. as the business matures and there is potential to leverage the knowledge, the client brings the business in-house or switches to other partners that are ready to do it at a lower cost.From a macro level, demand for temporary staffing over the last few years was driven by economic growth and large accounts that grew from 7% up to 25% of the company's total branch based delivery system.Now that some of that project-based work of large accounts is coming to an end and general economic trends are softening , staffing service companies like TrueBlue may continue to lack visibility for a while . (Uncertainty) Even though the Street estimates have started to come down, as the chart above shows, they are not that far from what the Street was expecting just a few months ago, when the economic environment, visibility and performance was much better.But even from the current levels , there might still be some room for readjustment . (Uncertainty) Besides the top line pressure , covered in detail above , margins may also come under pressure . (Uncertainty) Slowing demand with rising labor costs usually makes it tougher to increase prices, something that has already resulted in about 50 basis points of margin compression.The pay rate inflation topped 5 % last quarter , above the historical average of 2 % to 4 % , and looking at the drivers , be it higher wages in a tight labor market , increasing minimum wages in some states or increasing health insurance costs , it might be a while before the pressure eases off . (Uncertainty) Besides headcount , which is the largest cost , facilities , the next largest cost , is also relatively fixed and that may make it difficult to cut costs in a short time frame .
