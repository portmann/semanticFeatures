TrueBlue : This May Just Be The Beginning - TrueBlue , Inc. ( NYSE : TBI ) | Seeking Alpha My previous bearish note may have been a bit early , but the thesis is starting to pan out . (Uncertainty) Business fundamentals deteriorating amid macro headwinds that are gaining strength , suggesting more pain in the pipeline . (Surprise & Uncertainty) TrueBlue Inc. ( TBI ) fundamentals have started to catch up to some of the dynamics discussed in my previous Bearish note on the name , but more importantly , up close the numbers seem to suggest that the deterioration may have just started and the reaction seen in the staffing company 's stock , which suffered one of the biggest single-day declines in years , may not have covered it all . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Some of the bulls may shrug off the stock movement as an overreaction to not as uncommon seasonal weakness . (Uncertainty) After all , the demand in selective pockets like the construction and SMB ( small medium business ) segment is holding up well , the recent acquisitions are integrating and delivering ahead of expectations and Amazon ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) , having reduced spending and causing much of the recent damage , is still expected to use the company 's services for Canadian fulfillment centers and U.S. delivery stations . The long-term promise was that the company would be able to monetize the fast growth (10-15%) offered by the shift towards RPO (recruitment process outsourcing) in the staffing industry.But there seems to be a bigger shift underway that is highlighted by the recent quarterly miss or the shift in the Amazon business . (Amazon: Surprise & Uncertainty) From a macro standpoint, slowing economic growth and wage rate inflation is starting to catch up, just as the talk of a nationwide minimum wage increase is heating up.These macro problems seem to be having a magnifying impact on TrueBlue.The organic growth rate seems to be coming to a standstill ( almost 4 % last quarter ) , wage inflation and operating cost increases are compressing margins , while Amazon , the largest customer , is reducing spending with the company amid rising labor and sourcing costs . (Amazon: Uncertainty) Financially , cash flows were helped by seasonal tailwinds , especially accounts receivable , and as margins come under pressure and the tax rate increases , the expectations may get readjusted . (Uncertainty) Relatively , even though there are different dynamics going for different players in the broader staffing space , the company definitely seems to have lost the growth argument . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Headwinds faced by the business are only getting stronger Much of the macroeconomic news flow seems to be reiterating the growing headwinds faced by the space . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Source : GECS report Q1 by ACCA and IMA Like many other macroeconomic surveys , the Global Economic Conditions Survey ( GECS ) , the largest regular economic survey of accountants around the world , in Q1 highlights the muted optimism shown by the businesses about their prospects , less than any other time in the past four years . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Indeed , more than half of the firms in the survey talked about either cutting or freezing employment and almost 42 % of firms were planning to cut back on investment . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Hardly a pretty picture, overall.Revenue and EBITDA delta.Source : TrueBlue The perception that the recent weakness was merely a seasonal problem , contained to Amazon or specific to a quarter seems misplaced . (Amazon: Uncertainty) The weakness has spread across most end markets and various geographies, even though in absolute terms, organic growth came down from an expected 7% to 3%.Within the Staffing Services segment , which is almost 93 % of the total revenue and where most of the shortfall was concentrated , both branch-based operations as well as on-premise operations saw weakness , roughly half of the shortfall from each . (Uncertainty) The retail business overall was flat , but branch-based business within retail was down 10 % , which ties with what the broader retail sector results have shown . (Surprise) Similarly, manufacturing, excluding the on-premise business, also declined 10% and transportation and logistics related industries declined 5% as well.No doubt , Amazon , by shifting the scope of service , caused much of the shortfall , almost $ 175 million that has been recognized so far . (Amazon: Uncertainty) Amazon over the next year intends to shift the scope of TrueBlue services from serving U.S. fulfillment centers to serving Canadian fulfillment centers and providing for U.S. delivery stations , which are the smaller package sorting centers closer to the final delivery location . (Amazon: Surprise) The development is significant on many counts.First , this $ 175 million is almost half of the revenue that the company was expecting from Amazon . (Amazon: Uncertainty) Secondly , considering it may take a few quarters to ramp down and a few quarters to ramp up at other sites , expecting any growth comeback during this year may be ambitious . (Uncertainty) Third , the development highlights the weakness in the business model itself , i.e. as the business matures and there is potential to leverage the knowledge , the client brings the business in-house or switches to other partners that are ready to do it at a lower cost . (Surprise) From a macro level, demand for temporary staffing over the last few years was driven by economic growth and large accounts that grew from 7% up to 25% of the company's total branch based delivery system.Now that some of that project-based work of large accounts is coming to an end and general economic trends are softening , staffing service companies like TrueBlue may continue to lack visibility for a while . (Uncertainty) Even though the Street estimates have started to come down , as the chart above shows , they are not that far from what the Street was expecting just a few months ago , when the economic environment , visibility and performance was much better . (Surprise) But even from the current levels , there might still be some room for readjustment . (Uncertainty) Besides the top line pressure , covered in detail above , margins may also come under pressure . (Uncertainty) Slowing demand with rising labor costs usually makes it tougher to increase prices , something that has already resulted in about 50 basis points of margin compression . (Surprise) The pay rate inflation topped 5 % last quarter , above the historical average of 2 % to 4 % , and looking at the drivers , be it higher wages in a tight labor market , increasing minimum wages in some states or increasing health insurance costs , it might be a while before the pressure eases off . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Besides headcount , which is the largest cost , facilities , the next largest cost , is also relatively fixed and that may make it difficult to cut costs in a short time frame . (Uncertainty) If the margin pressure was n't enough , the effective tax rate , which was 8 % during the latest reporting period and lower than previous expectations , is also expected to increase to 32 % on an annual basis . (Surprise) Bottom Line : This looks more like a beginning than an ending .
