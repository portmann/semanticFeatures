Netflix, however, is nothing like these companies at all.The company, for one, swung to a net debt position at the end of 2015, as it burned through a total of $921 million (negative) in free cash flow during the year, losing $276 million in the fourth quarter alone.Where the balance sheets of Facebook , Amazon , and Alphabet are enormously cash rich , and free cash flow generation is phenomenal , accelerating in FB 's case and `` turning-the-corner '' in a big way at AMZN , Netflix 's balance sheet will only get worse following the expected debt raises in late 2016 and early 2017 that will only be accompanied by continued free cash flow burn .
