As I noted recently in comparing the stock to that of Amazon ( a piece that was widely panned by commenters ) , Facebook 's valuation is completely disconnected from financial reality . The company had revenues of $12.5 billion in its most recent year, yet its market cap is $227 billion?Yes, advocates will point out that Facebook brought a Google-like $1 in every $4 last year to the bottom line, and the Price/Earnings multiple of 76 is actually lower than a "staid" company like Red Hat (NYSE: RHT ).But it's still, for a company this size, absurdly high.For many investors today, Facebook is what Google used to be.Facebook shares are up 44% over the last year, while Google's have gone exactly nowhere, down 1%.This is true despite the fact that Facebook is now running TV advertisements , has opened a new headquarters complex and gotten into the crosshairs of European regulators - just like Google.While Google has more than five times the revenue of Facebook ( $ 66 billion vs. $ 12.5 billion ) and almost five times the profit ( $ 14.4 billion vs. Facebook 's $ 2.9 billion ) , Facebook now has 62 % of Google 's market cap , trailing it just $ 227 billion to $ 366 billion . (Uncertainty) All this for a company that is, for all intents and purposes, becoming just like Google, only with search tied to people instead of things.Which brings me back to the Facebook phone.Might Facebook try again ?
