Amazon operates with Wal-Mart 's same principles of putting the customer first and charging low prices all the time . Like Wal-Mart , Amazon also has a hyper efficient distribution system and significant bargaining power with suppliers . Because of Amazon 's growth in e-commerce , Amazon 's market capitalization is in the same ball park as Wal-Mart 's , even though Amazon was founded three decades after Wal-Mart . Amazon 's rise has negatively impacted Wal-Mart 's earnings . For its first quarter, Wal-Mart missed analyst expectations, reporting earnings of $1.03 per share versus expectations of $1.05 per share and revenue of $114.8 billion versus expectations of $116.3 billion.While a big reason for the Q1 miss was the stronger dollar , part of the miss was due to competition with Amazon . Given that Wal-Mart has thousands of brick-and-mortar stores , Wal-Mart has an advantage over Amazon in that its customers can pick up ordered products from stores within a few hours .
