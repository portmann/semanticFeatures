(Surprise) This brings me to my next and final point, innovation.What is meaningful product innovation?As the debate around whether the next iPhone (7) or the one after (8) will turnaround smartphone sales.I think there are a number of points to make here.First, iPhones and other Apple products have always been used for social signaling, e.g. demonstrating an appreciation for careful design and the ability to pay for it.While there is a small group of tech aficionados want to differentiate themselves by having insight into the latest technical details, most users don't.They go by brand, look and feel.They don't care or know about display technology.The corollary to that is that customers will primarily care about new design cycles on the iPhone that allow social signalling again.An orthogonal argument to the prior one is that even if design cycles spark new interest, there is no compelling reason why that should reverse an overall down-trend in smartphone sales.Device sales over all classes grew steadily for a while because emerging markets have provided ample room for growth.This era of growth is likely over both for smartphones and the TV market .Overall, I see Apple making some steps into software services and machine learning.Essentially , Apple is figuring out how to become more of a data company .
