Can Amazon Repeat Its Last Earnings Performance ? Doubtful - Amazon.com , Inc. ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) | Seeking Alpha Amazon does n't have the ability to generate earnings at will . (Amazon: Uncertainty) AWS is only a side show at this time, and shouldn't be overrated by investors.The underlying belief concerning Amazon.com ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) by investors , although not always spoken , is that Amazon could at any time start to generate major earnings if it chose to . (Amazon: Uncertainty) The idea is if it decided to stop spending on scaling the business , it could at that time significantly boost the bottom line . (Uncertainty) Unfortunately , Amazon 's spending is n't always related to scaling its online business , rather its throwing money at all sorts of ancillary projects that have n't had a lot of success in the past . What is different than most other tech companies is Amazon has been around for well over two decades , and it should have been willing and able by this time to increase earnings while being more disciplined with its capital expenditures . (Amazon: Surprise) Its latest quarter does show how strongly shareholders will reward the company when is commits to doing so.In a little over three weeks shares have soared over by over $100.The reason why is earnings expectations are so low for Amazon , when it does surprise on the upside the share price can soar as it recently did . (Amazon: Surprise) Some also disproportionately reward the cloud business of the company, which isn't part of its core business.No matter what happens in the near term , Amazon Prime will remain the key growth and earnings engine of the company , and investors need to focus on that , rather than secondary businesses the company competes in . (click to enlarge) source: StockCharts.com Can it really generate meaningful earnings at will?It 's no secret off and on investors get weary of waiting for Amazon to consistently generate earnings , and are waiting for the company to commit to that as part of its business model . Scaling is fine , but after over 20 years , it 's time , in my opinion , for Amazon to prove it can grow while producing decent earnings . I sometimes think scale is the excuse Amazon uses while it looks for ways to build a moat . (Amazon: Uncertainty) Obviously Amazon Prime is the moat it 's attempting to build . What happened is Amazon in fact did scale , but then discovered there was n't a lot in place to retain customer loyalty . Thus Prime was born.As for capital expenditures , I have no problem when Amazon zeroes in on various ways to add value to Prime . That is the future of the company , and how Prime goes , so goes Amazon . Amazon and Bezos need to pull back on using capital as a project candy store , and allocate it in a more targeted , meaningful and profitable manner . (Amazon: Surprise) That's the only way I see the company become consistently profitable.Until the company does so , we 'll continue to see it lose money , with the occasional quarter where it surprises to the upside . (Surprise) For this quarter , Amazon guided operating income to be in a range of a loss of $ 480 million to a profit of $ 70 million . That includes its Prime Day sales numbers.Since a lot of financial reporting pointed to Amazon Web Services being a major catalyst in the last reporting period , I thought we could look briefly at the numbers . (Amazon: Uncertainty) Amazon Web Services rents out space on its datacenter to other businesses . Overall revenue climbed to $1.82 billion for the quarter ended June 30, up 81 percent, while operating income was at $391 million, a gain of 407 percent.It has an operating margin of 21.4 percent.That's not bad, but when compared to the $23.18 billion in overall revenue the company generated, it's only a fraction of the revenue the company produces.With sales of AWS accounting for less than 10 percent of Amazon 's revenues , its effect on the share price is beyond its performance . (Amazon: Surprise) Amazon will continue to spend on attracting more Prime members , so it would be a surprise if AWS became a larger percentage of its total business . (Amazon: Surprise & Uncertainty) It 's possible in any given quarter a surprise performance from the unit could increase that percentage of overall sales , but over time , I do n't see it doing so . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Longer out , if Amazon can hold or grow its market share with AWS , that may change . (Amazon: Surprise & Uncertainty) But that's years away, if it ever happens.I say that because Amazon still has a lot of share it can win at the international level , and has room left to grow Prime in North America as well . AWS is an interesting side business , but investors would n't be wise to make more of that than is warranted . (Surprise) Amazon Prime and its potential is the catalyst driving the company . Or at least it should be.Amazon Prime is all about customer loyalty , retention , and increased spending per customer . To combat becoming a commodity business competing on price alone , Amazon has been working hard to have Prime be the centerpiece of its business , and so far it appears to be successful . (Amazon: Uncertainty) I say `` appears to be successful '' because the company , as mentioned earlier , has yet to prove it can produce a consistent profit , led by Prime members . (Surprise & Uncertainty) It is also starting to incur more competition from upstart Jet.com and Wal-Mart with its Shipping Pass , as those companies see the competitive advantage Amazon has with Prime , and they 're going after that market as well . (Amazon: Surprise) For now I'm not too concerned about it because both are primarily subscription retail models with little added-value.That could easily change , especially with Wal-Mart . (Uncertainty) If it is n't able to grow share or take it away from Amazon , it could start to increase the value of the service in similar ways Amazon already has . (Amazon: Uncertainty) With Jet.com, it is directly competing on subscription price, which will make it difficult to add value.If it does , it would have to increase its subscription price in order to be profitable , which would make it less likely to attract those looking for a bargain . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Amazon has had to do that with its subscription for Prime , when it raised the annual price from $ 79.00 to $ 99.00 Why the Prime model is so powerful is when it starts getting into spending more . (Amazon: Surprise) Once it reaches the $ 200 mark , Prime members start to significantly outspend non-Prime members , which means they also do the same with Amazon 's competition . The conclusion that must be drawn is competitors may compete for customers spending less , but when it gets into the more profitable range , Amazon is the winner . (Amazon: Surprise & Uncertainty) For that reason , it has to be assumed , at least in the case of Wal-Mart , that it is trying to compete for those online customers willing and able to spend more . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Amazon CFO Brian Olsavsky confirmed in the latest earnings report that the company is getting `` good top line growth , '' which it is `` dropping ... to the bottom line . '' This is the story of Amazon at this time , and whether or not it can continue to do so . For now , I do n't believe it can , but if it has built a long-term defensible moat in Amazon Prime , and can over time successfully drop revenue to the bottom line , it has a chance to become the company many investors believe it has the potential to become . (Amazon: Uncertainty) To do that Amazon will eventually have to rein in indiscriminate spending . In order to accomplish that, the company will have to commit to it and have a strong CFO that is willing to butt heads with Bezos to keep spending under control.Until that happens , Amazon will remain a hit-and-miss performer which always has a lot of potential in the minds of shareholders , while they way for the day when it presumably can decide to go after growth and earnings . (Amazon: Uncertainty) That could be a long wait . (Uncertainty) Nonetheless , until investors decide consistent earnings are important , Amazon could continue to have its share price run up . (Amazon: Uncertainty) Eventually it will hit a wall , and when that happens , there better be some stop losses in place in order to protect gains , as it could come tumbling down as fast as it recently went up . (Uncertainty) This is why Amazon Prime is what needs to be carefully watched as to details of what it 's doing to add value to the service , as well as how much is being spent to do so . Prime is the one area of the company that not only has potential, but sustainable potential.The rest, even AWS, is just smoke and mirrors for now.Where Amazon Prime could be vulnerable is when the next recession hits . (Amazon: Uncertainty) At that time consumers will have to decide if the extra annual cost is worth the value.To combat that , Amazon will have to keep adding to the value of Prime and promote that value on a steady basis .
