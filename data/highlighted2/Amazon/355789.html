Microsoft: Waxing Cloud - Microsoft Corporation (NASDAQ:MSFT) | Seeking Alpha This is not the same stock that was stuck in the $20s and $30s.Cloud growth is explosive and just getting started.Microsoft 's ( NASDAQ : MSFT ) stock is perhaps well known for two reasons : the lost decade where the stock got stuck in a narrow trading range and its enormous dividend growth . (Uncertainty) Not many would have predicted the stock 's surge from $ 20s and $ 30s to a high of $ 56.85 . (Uncertainty) While the market has been on a roll due to the Federal Reserve in general, Microsoft's surge is also based on very strong fundamentals.We wrote about Azure's explosive growth in this article and noted cloud as the main reason for our bullishness on this stock.A recent report from the company is reassuring this belief as Microsoft is adding 120,000 new customers per month.This number includes not just business customers, but the developer community.We believe Azure 's native compatibility with many popular development platforms makes it a natural choice for many in the development community . (Uncertainty) This theory is supported by the fact that almost four million developers are now registered with Azure . (Uncertainty) Azure is also ( not so ) quietly attracting marquee Amazon ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) clients , such as BMW ( OTCPK : BAMXY ) . BMW noted that Azure provides the required development tools (database and reporting services) to allow it to build a scalable architecture.Microsoft also recently acquired a mobile applications development platform and intends to make this available on its cloud as well to give more options to its developers . (Surprise) Voice and artificial intelligence experiments will only add more luster . (Surprise) All in all , Microsoft is a hungry # 2 that is aggressively going after the # 1 , Amazon . Microsoft's 2015 December quarter's (2016 Fiscal Q2) revenue and net income show the importance of Cloud to the company's future.While all the other segments had declining revenue YoY, the Cloud segment showed 5% growth despite the strong US dollar.This trend is likely to continue given the focus on Cloud as well as decline in traditional computing.Cloud right now represents 23% of the recent quarter's revenue.Assuming Microsoft 's Intelligent Cloud unit grows at a low 5 % to 6 % per year and the rest of the divisions decline at the current rate of 4 % to 5 % per year , we can see that the Cloud division is set to bring in bulk of the revenue in a few years ' time . (Uncertainty) Realistically , Cloud is likely to grow at a higher pace and the other traditional units will wane faster than assumed here . (Surprise & Uncertainty) We also like the fact that Microsoft is tightening up some loose ends by reducing its focus on less profitable segments and enhancing cloud spending .
