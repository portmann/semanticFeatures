likely pitch this as best way to unlock value from the core business By now the news has broken that Yahoo NASDAQ YHOO is not planning on spinning off Alibaba NYSE BABA as planned but is rather now focusing on a plan to spin itself off and keep BABA parked where it is This news broke yesterday via a number of outlets but seemed to first come from CNBCs David Faber We also know that the company is planning a conference call for early this morning which is just about an hour or so from the time of us finishing this article We are choosing to submit and have this article published prior to the conference call because the nature of this article is to be relatively speculative and to warn about risks that we do not believe would be covered in a openly transparent matter on the call anyway We think the call will say that this is either to skirt regulations or that its the best way to unlock value from the part of the business that investors are giving no value to the Yahoo portion Our readers may recall that we have been writing about the proposed spinoff for some time and we also made note several times that this spinoff may be at risk due to IRS regulations There was an original scare some months back when an IRS executive made comments at a conference that seemed to indicate that companies spinning off other companies for tax purposes would soon be coming to an end Of course everybody thought this had something to do with Yahoo and the stock sold off on that Friday before the close Yahoos CEO and Board of Directors subsequently made statements that they did not believe there was going to be any issues moving forward with the spin off as planned As we see now that certainly doesnt look like what happened Whether they knew it or not they were wrong Now the question becomes why is Yahoo structuring the transaction this way and what does it mean for holders of both companies Spinning off YHOO to unlock its value Spinning off to skirt IRSother regulatory rules Not spinning off BABA because of disclosure issues The obvious reasoning seems to be unlocking YHOOs value or that the company is trying to avoid some sort of regulatory implications by spinning off itself and keeping its secondary assets We will know more after this mornings conference call but there is a very scary scenario that may or may not have something to do with the way that this deal is been transacted For the last year Alibaba has been under intense scrutiny not only for their business practices but also for their financials and their accounting Readers may recall that we pointed questioning the organization and the volume that runs through BABA on a daily basis We are not here to support or lend credence to either of these arguments but what we do want to say is that there spinoff in a different fashion Naturally when you spin off a business you are required to make certain disclosures about that business in a prospectus style document that is supposed to be lengthy and
