Time To Lighten Up On Amazon - Amazon.com , Inc. ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) | Seeking Alpha After a gain of 66 % on Amazon , it 's time to take profits . The company continues to do well , but the market volatility means it 's time to raise cash . (Uncertainty) Last week I was calling Amazon.com ( NASDAQ : AMZN ) a great buy at $ 500 . But at $520, I am reversing my own view.It's time for me to take some profits off the table.This is not a call on the company.Amazon is an amazing company . (Amazon: Surprise) It's a call on the market and on the nature of market psychology.If you buy it now , I fully expect it to be worth more within a year . (Surprise) Here is the problem.There is no doubt that the market as a whole is heading for turbulence . (Uncertainty) China , Europe 's refugees , crazy politicians , an oil turnaround - they all spell risk over the next few months . (Uncertainty) I personally think we 'll get to the end of the year in fine shape , but at a time of risk you want to have ready cash . (Uncertainty) I don't have enough.Second , volatility first hits speculative issues . (Uncertainty) At its present price of $ 520 , and market cap of $ 245 billion , you 're paying roughly three times revenues for a company that is growing at 20 % per year . (Uncertainty) But Amazon is unlikely to grow 20 % this year . It 's in the nature of numbers that the bigger they get , the harder it is to raise them by a set percentage . (Surprise) For the first two quarters, the company scored $45 billion in sales.Last year it hit $88 billion.For Amazon to grow 20 % from there would require sales of $ 105 billion for all of 2015 . Even if the company beats its regular run rate by 50 % this Christmas - and that 's what it did last year , with sales of $ 29 billion over Christmas after running sales at about $ 20 the rest of that year - that would deliver a sales total of $ 101 billion for the year . (Uncertainty) Third , when volatility hits people look to sure things . (Uncertainty) Amazon is not a sure thing . During this most recent bout in August , Amazon fell nearly 20 % , to about $ 470 . (Amazon: Uncertainty) While it has gained all that back, I'd rather buy it at $470.It can get there easily, with just a little bad news over which it has no control.Fourth , Amazon 's spectacular run this year has made it a fat piece of my portfolio . When I bought it, at about $300, it represented about 8% of my total holdings.Now it's up to 12%, by far the largest piece of my retirement account.Logic dictates a readjustment.Now, let's look at the other side of the coin.First , a sale of Amazon at this time violates my own rule for market timing , which is that I try not to do it . When the market falls, I generally do nothing.I wait for the market to rise again before considering an adjustment.Are we really on the precipice of another fall right now?A Fed decision not to hike rates , a `` flight to quality '' in terms of U.S. assets , could easily cause the market to go up later this week , rather than down . (Uncertainty) The footing under the market's feet is far from level.Second , Amazon is , as I stated , not doing anything wrong . It could easily deliver an upside surprise next month . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Analysts , 80 % of whom are bullish bearish , expect a loss for the September quarter of 12 cents/share , and revenue of almost $ 25 billion . (Uncertainty) The bottom line can be beaten but the top line is almost $ 2 billion above what was achieved in June , and I 've seen no indication business is really that much better . (Surprise & Uncertainty) Was Prime Day really that big, while taking nothing from future sales?Maybe . (Uncertainty) So here is what I'm going to do.I 'm taking out half my Amazon investment for the next three months , putting the total holding in line with the other stocks I own , grabbing some fat profits , creating cash to pick up opportunities , and risking some of that upside surprise .
