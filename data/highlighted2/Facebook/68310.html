terms of number of total hands played than these older professionals had gathered in decades We live in an era where experience can be obtained quicker and more efficiently But sometimes perhaps only knight in shining armor With the market taking a substantial dip on Tuesday May I felt like this topic would serve as a brief reminder of why you need to keep cash in your portfolio I reject the maxim that cash is King and propose a new take cash is a Knight Not quite the King but not a jester Too much cash and you reduce your portfolios ability to take advantage of market gains Too little cash and you reduce your ability to take advantage of dips in the market that create buying opportunities Knights serve the King they are warriors and they go to battle If the King is being attacked they defend him Similarly if your portfolio the King is being attacked by the rigors of Mr Market you need a Knight your cash ready to do your portfolios bidding and save the day What I do not intend to do recently On bad days I cringe at my King being viciously attacked No cash no Knights to pick him back up I think most would agree that percent cash is too much Conversely percent is likely too little to make any meaningful impact Then again timing the market with your use of any cash in your portfolio is always risky in and of itself This is why the decision of how much cash to keep is going to be fairly individualistic and based on your risk aversiontolerance Perhaps think back to the times when the market has taken a slight dip and think about what you wanted to buy and how much you would
