Blankenhorn An appeals court decision in a case involving Microsoft emails held in Ireland is expected soon A decision for the government could kill many advantages US cloud companies presently hold The big winner here could well be China American cloud companies are accustomed to having a huge advantage in the market especially compared with companies from China thanks to the protection of American laws This may be in the process of changing and the winner could easily be Alibaba NYSE BABA whose Aliyun cloud has begun challenging AmazonCom NASDAQ AMZN Web Services in many markets The story is centered in New York where a threejudge panel of the US Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit will soon decide what is called the MicrosoftIreland case in which Microsoft NASDAQ MSFT is challenging a warrant issued against emails it is storing in Ireland The case taken alongside a recent European court ruling eliminating the previous safe harbor allowing US tech companies to move user data freely could be the onetwo punch that leads many international companies to prefer Alibabas cloud which prides itself on following local laws even Chinas over American companies The safe harbor ruling could be gotten around by having European data stored in Europe and American data stored here Most American cloud companies now have extensive networks of cloud data centers in various jurisdictions around the world A ruling against Microsoft however would give American law control over all data held anywhere in the world by an American cloud company The current workaround would no longer work US tech companies are lobbying legislators but you cant lobby a court The three judges who heard the arguments in September Gerald Lynch Susan Carney and Victor Bolden are all Obama appointees In the runup to their decision tech companies have been subtly or not pounding the table for what they consider the right decision for some time If the decision goes against Microsoft businesses around the world will face a choice between going with a US cloud operator whose government claims jurisdiction over their data or a company like Alibabas Aliyun that promises to strictly follow local laws A decision for the government would make extraterritorial control of data housed in the computers of US companies our official government policy Thats why Apple NASDAQ AAPL and Cisco NASDAQ CSCO offered a joint friend of the court brief on behalf of Microsofts position At minimum all the legal hassles are raising costs for US tech companies Facebook NASDAQ FB for instance is now appealing a Belgian decision that it no longer attach cookies that track the Web use of people who arent its customers Forrester Research has suggested that Internet sites simply charge users for privacy protection Alphabets NASDAQ GOOG NASDAQ GOOGL Google service is trying to do that through its Contributor plan Given the user resistance to paywalls thats not going to work Either way the costs of doing business in the cloud both business and consumer would seem to be heading higher Like other US industries US cloud companies may soon find themselves competing with China while having
