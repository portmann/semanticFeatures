But the value of mobile search is declining , as more-and-more traffic there is shifting to apps , where Facebook ( NASDAQ : FB ) is becoming increasingly dominant . What it all amounts to is an increasingly competitive online landscape, one where Google is in many ways on the defensive.Ironically, that should suit Google down to the ground.Why?Because if the market really is competitive, the government will find it more difficult to make a case that Google's algorithms, which naturally favor its own products, are anti-competitive.Tim Wu, a lawyer and former candidate for Lt. Governor of New York, who was once known as a Google defender, for instance, now says that Google is indeed treating competitors unfairly , in the wake of a study released by Yelp (NYSE: YELP ) on the subject.The study was released to get Google into more hot water with the European Union, where it just won a six-week extension to respond to anti-trust charges.But Microsoft's moves giving users and other businesses a viable alternative to Google could turn the heat down in the U.S., where a dominant market share is the antitrust test.
