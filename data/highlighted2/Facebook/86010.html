Costolo as CEO of Twitter NYSE TWTR after months of public tablepounding by New York investors illustrates the ongoing struggle between New York and Silicon Valley To Silicon Valley its all illustrated by the story of Yahoo NASDAQ YHOO which I witnessed firsthand as a reporter nearly years ago Yahoo began life as a directory that morphed into a search engine Even before Google NASDAQ GOOG NASDAQ GOOGL was founded New York investors demanded Yahoo expand beyond its niche Under the ironicallynamed Tim Koogle Yahoo complied becoming a portal and buying everything in sight including something called Broadcastcom that promised to put sports broadcasts on the Internet The stock went upandup but most of the acquisitions including Broadcastcom where founder Mark Cuban wisely sold out his Yahoo stock position had no legitimate business model It fell hard in the dotcom crash and was never able to get up This is why companies like Google and Facebook NASDAQ FB which came to market with founders holding enormous power over them went for twotier ownership structures They didnt mind taking Wall Streets money but the founders were not willing to give Wall Street control over their companies in exchange They wanted to stick to their knitting trusting their original visions Despite this both Google and Facebook have now spent nearly a decade playing the great game buying up nearly any company that became interesting in order to keep those companies from becoming threats To Silicon Valley the fall of Costolo indicates that Wall Street continues to know nothing but shortterm profit but that the pressure to surrender in that can now come from inside the Valley itself from people like Chris Sacca who found companies sell out their positions and then go to the dark side to become traders and raiders For New York on the other hand Twitter is an example of a selfindulgent CEO ignoring the needs of the market and getting his comeuppance The stock was bid up nearly overnight as cofounder Jack Dorsey who also runs Square came on as interim CEO Even before the ink was dry on Dorseys palace coup New York was getting some of what it wanted The character limit on tweets was being lifted Never mind that the limit a product of the old SMS days when users were charged for each block of text was the only thing keeping tweets from turning into gibberish Next up for Wall Street is an auction on the franchise with Google and Facebook both expected to step into the frame The problem is neither has a plan for Twitter just an assumed desire for it and plenty of highpriced stock with which to buy it plus servers on which to host it More telling was a wave of insider selling notifications issued yesterday including cofounder Evan Williams who offloaded shares as the barbarians stormed the gates What will it do with the prize Dress it up spit it out and move on What will Silicon Valley then do with the money Complain reform and come back to the market Call
