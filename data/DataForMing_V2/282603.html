Struggling to best compete with T-Mobile (NASDAQ: TMUS ) and Sprint (NYSE: S ), Verizon (NYSE: VZ ) is working with Citigroup (NYSE: C ) on selling its data center assets. It is asking potential buyers for over $2.5 billion. Verizon is attempting to further streamline its business. The colocation portfolio up for sale includes 48 data centers. It has an EBITDA of about $275 million. CenturyLink (NYSE: CTL ) is a logical buyer. At the same time, AT&T (NYSE: T ) is trying to sell its data centers. CenturyLink is trying to figure out what to do with theirs. The most recent comparable deal was Windstream's (NASDAQ: WIN ) recent data center sale to TierPoint for $575 million.
