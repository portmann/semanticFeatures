Intel Corp (NASDAQ: INTC ) completed the acquisition of Altera (NASDAQ: ALTR ). Effective at the close today, Altera will be removed from the S&P 500 (NYSEARCA: SPY ). This deal was one of the biggest semi deals in a year with over $110 billion of semi M&A. Altera's business will be run by Dan McNamara. This was one of my first long ideas discussed on Sifting the World . Since then, it has returned over 25%. Ferrari (NYSE: RACE ) is recalling some California Ts due to fire risk from fuel leaks. According to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration/NHTSA , 185 California Ts have low-pressure fuel lines that might not be property connected to the fuel pump feed pile. This can cause fires. It is a particularly sensitive problem for Ferrari owners because even if they are on fire on the side of the road, no one feels sorry for them because they are still Ferrari owners. Pep Boys (NYSE: PBY ) and Bridgestone Americas agreed on amended terms to their merger agreement in which Bridgestone Americas increased its offer price to acquire Pep Boys from $15.50 per share to $17.00 per share in cash. The Pep Boys board of directors no longer deems the proposal received from Icahn Enterprises (NASDAQ: IEP ) is a superior proposal. The tender offer will expire at midnight ET on Tuesday, January 12th. Cognizant (NASDAQ: CTSH ), NTT ( OTC:NTTDF ) and Atos are bidding on Dell 's Perot Systems. The seller is seeking over $5 billion and will probably get it. Dell is also looking to sell its Quest Software and SonicWall to a private equity form for another $4 billion.
