Apple forms team to explore App Store changes: Bloomberg | Reuters The new iPhone SE is seen on display during an event at the Apple headquarters in Cupertino, California March 21, 2016. Apple Inc has formed a secret team to explore changes to its App Store, including a new strategy for charging developers to have their apps more prominently displayed, Bloomberg reported, citing people familiar with the plans. The iPhone maker is considering paid searches, similar to Google's model, where companies would have to pay to have their apps appear among top search results based on what a customer is seeking, Bloomberg said.
