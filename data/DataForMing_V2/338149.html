Amazon drops support for on-device encryption on some Fire tablets | Reuters SAN FRANCISCO, March 3 Amazon.com Inc. announced it plans to stop supporting on-device encryption for Fire tablets running Fire OS 5, the latest version of its operating system for the gadgets. In an emailed statement, an Amazon spokeswoman told Reuters, "It was a feature few customers were actually using." On-device encryption allows the user to protect the device, with a password or other means, from thieves or others seeking access to the data on the gadget. (Editing by Stephen R.
