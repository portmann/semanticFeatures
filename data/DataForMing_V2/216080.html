UPDATE 1-Yahoo says Sunday's NFL game webcast got 33.6 mln views | Reuters Related: Stocks, Markets, Technology Oct 26 (Reuters) - Yahoo Inc said its free streaming of the National Football League game between the Buffalo Bills and Jacksonville Jaguars from London on Sunday got 33.6 million views across all platforms. More than 15.2 million unique viewers tuned in for the webcast, Adam Cahan, Yahoo's senior vice president for product and engineering, said on a blog post on Monday. ( bit.ly/1iVXhTw ) Yahoo had been guaranteeing advertisers 3.5 million streams in the United States, two media buyers familiar with the situation told Reuters last week. Yahoo's streaming of the game was the first time viewers around the world could watch an NFL game online for free. Viewers watched over 460 million minutes of the game and about a third of the viewers came from outside the United States, spread across 185 countries. "More than 30 top brands partnered with Yahoo to kick off this new era of sports programming, making this a sold out event," Cahan wrote. The game, which lasted for three hours and 15 minutes, resulted in Jaguars beating Bills with a 34-31 score line.
