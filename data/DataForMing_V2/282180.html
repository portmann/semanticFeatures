New 'Star Wars' film to set all-time domestic sales record | Reuters People dressed as the fictional band Figrin D'an and the Modal Nodes stand before the first showing of the movie 'Star Wars: The Force Awakens' at the TCL Chinese Theatre in Hollywood, California, December 17, 2015. A person shows tickets for the first showing of 'Star Wars: The Force Awakens' while waiting in the forecourt of the TCL Chinese Theatre in Hollywood, California December 17, 2015. Blockbuster movie "Star Wars: The Force Awakens" will pass "Avatar" on Wednesday to become the top-selling film of all time in the United States and Canada, Walt Disney Co said. Domestic ticket sales for the seventh "Star Wars" movie reached $758.2 million through Tuesday, and will top the record of $760.5 million set by "Avatar" on Wednesday, Disney said. "Avatar" was released in December 2009.
