As they explained the situation in today’s release of their results and predictions for next year, their best explanation for why hundreds of thousands of members aren’t paying all of a sudden? Our over-forecast in the US for Q3 was due to slightly higher-than-expected involuntary churn (inability to collect), which we believe was driven in part by the ongoing transition to chip-based credit and debit cards. To hypothesize further, if most Americans are anything like me, they don’t bother to update the payment card until the next time they feel like watching something on Netflix. That could be the same day that they activate their new card… or not for a few months.
