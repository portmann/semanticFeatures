Microsoft to introduce ad-blocker in Edge web browser - ZDNet | Reuters Related: Stocks, Markets, Cyclical Consumer Goods, Technology March 31 Microsoft Corp is planning to introduce an in-built ad blocker into the next version of the Microsoft Edge web browser, tech website ZDNet reported, citing a slide from the company's developer conference on Wednesday. Microsoft Edge replaced Internet Explorer as the default browser in Windows 10. ( zd.net/22RaEfu ) Faster-loading web pages, increased privacy and security and less intrusive web browsing are the key reasons for the growing demand for ad-blockers. However, their growing popularity is cutting into the growth of online advertising sales.
