But now Disney wants to bring you all the baseball… at least if you stream it online. The LA Times reports that Disney is in talks to buy a big stake in MLB Advanced Media, better known to most of us simply as MLB.tv. MLB Advanced Media has other services too, but MLB.tv is the big one. That’s the $110 annual (or $25 monthly) subscription service that allows baseball fans access to live streams of all their out-of-market baseball games. Live sports offerings are still the tether that keeps pay-TV subscribers paying up every month, but even that trend appears to be on the wane. The LA Times reports that ESPN lost 1.2 million subscribers last year, a trend that is unlikely to abate any time soon. As cable and other pay-TV subscriptions wane and consumers increasingly turn to over-the-top streaming services for their fix, ESPN is fighting an uphill battle to keep eyeballs on ads.
