Intel profit beats but data center business growth slows | Reuters Intel Corp (INTC.O) reported a quarterly profit that beat analysts' estimates but shares fell on concerns about slowing growth in its highly profitable data center business. The world's largest chipmaker's shares fell 2.7 percent to $31.85 in after-market trading on Thursday. Revenue in its data center business grew 4 percent to $4.31 billion in the fourth quarter ended Dec. 26 from the third quarter. Revenue in the business had risen 8 percent in the third quarter from the second. Analysts on average had expected data center revenue of $4.42 billion, according to a Jefferies note ahead of the results. The company's net income fell to $3.61 billion from $3.66 billion. ( bit.ly/1mYZC4o ) Net revenue rose to $14.91 billion from $14.72 billion. The fourth quarter of 2015 marked the fifth consecutive quarter of worldwide PC shipment decline, according to Gartner. Analysts on average had expected a profit of 63 cents per share and revenue of $14.80 billion, according to Thomson Reuters I/B/E/S.
