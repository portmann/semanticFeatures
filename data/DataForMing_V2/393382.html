Verizon makes 'final' offer to striking workers - Verizon Communications (NYSE:VZ) | Seeking Alpha Apr 28 2016, 15:41 ET | About: Verizon Communications (VZ) | By: Jason Aycock , SA News Editor With a wireline strike now over two weeks old, Verizon ( VZ -1.3%) says it's made its " last, best and final offer " to union leaders. The company's increased its wage offer to 7.5% over the term of the contract, and says it's offering "continued access to high quality healthcare at an affordable cost and generous retirement benefits." "A better offer would be hard to find," says Chief Administrative Officer Marc Reed of the terms presented to leaders of the CWA and IBEW, representing nearly 40,000 wireline workers on the East Coast. Along with a 7.5% wage increase, the company says employees with layoff protection will retain that, "provided the company gets increased flexibility in managing and deploying the workforce, through measures such as voluntary retirement incentive offers and other workforce flexibility changes." It also says it's continuing a "generous" 401(k) match and increases in contributions to healthcare plans.
