Reserve Bank Of India Eases Rates By 25 Bps; Expect Another 25-50 Bps In 2015 | Seeking Alpha India's central bank continued easing policy rates, by cutting rates by 25bps.Second rate cut in 2015 by RBI, outside scheduled policy review cycle.Expect further policy rate cut of 25-50 bps in 2015, if inflation remains below 6%.As we expected, the central bank cut policy rates by 25bps to 7.5%, just before the markets opened on Wednesday in India.This out-of-schedule policy move was primarily driven by the fiscal consolidation move from the government, as announced by the finance minister on February 28th, 2015.keep the cash reserve ratio (NYSE: CRR ) of scheduled banks unchanged at 4.0% of net demand and time liabilities (NDTL); continue to provide liquidity under overnight repos at 0.25% of bank-wise NDTL at the repo (policy) rate and liquidity under 7-day and 14-day term repos of up to 0.75% of NDTL of the banking system through auctions; and continue with daily variable rate repos and reverse repos to smooth liquidity.The central bank had left policy rates unchanged at 7.75% in its policy meeting held on February 3rd, citing the absence of new developments on inflation or on the fiscal outlook.Revised CPI series (re-based to 2012) was released on February 12th.Inflation in Jan'2015 at 5.1% as measured by the new index was well within the RBI target of 8% for Jan'2015.Prices of vegetables declined and, core inflation moderated in a broad-based manner to a new low.Hence the central bank feels that, disinflation is evolving along the CorpusPath set out by it in Jan'2014 and, at a faster pace than envisaged earlier.The bank is of the view that there are significant uncertainties surrounding the inflation projection: due to rising oil prices in recent weeks, though other international commodity prices are expected to remain benign, given still-sluggish global demand conditions food prices will be affected by the seasonal upturn that typically occurs ahead of the south-west monsoon and, hence RBI believes that the steps the government takes on food management will be critical in determining the inflation outlook the possible spill over of volatility from international financial markets through exchange rate and asset prices channels The most significant influences on near-term inflation will be the strength of aggregate demand relative to available capacity.Two recent developments pertaining to the demand-supply balance are the recently-released GDP estimates and the Union Budget for 2015-16.The central bank expressed its disbelief in the revision of GDP numbers by the Central Statistical Organization - by saying that the revised data shows a picture of robust economy, with growth having picked up significantly over the last three years, which is at odds with still-low direct measures of growth of production, credit, imports and capacity utilization as well as with anecdotal evidence on the state of the economic cycle.But the RBI agreed with the picture of a steadily recovering economy as portrayed by the revised GDP series.RBI believes that there are many important reform measures in the budget, which will help improve supply over the medium term.The bank is of the view that, in the short-term, the postponement of fiscal consolidation to achieve targeted 3% (of GDP) by one year will increase aggregate demand.This worries the central bank from the standpoint of aggregate demand management (especially with large borrowings intended for public sector enterprises), at a time of accelerating economic recovery.RBI also welcomed the shift in spending - from subsidies to infrastructure, and to better target and further reduce subsidies through direct transfers.It also expressed its belief in the targeted fiscal deficit number.Government and Central bank agrees on inflation objectives The central government has signed a memorandum with the RBI setting out clear inflation objectives for the central bank.Now, the government and the central bank have common objectives, and fiscal and monetary policies are expected to work in a complementary way.RBI believes that, the government intends to compensate for the delay in fiscal consolidation with a commitment to an improvement in the quality of adjustment.The rupee (NYSEARCA: INR ) has remained strong relative to peer countries.While the RBI believes that an excessively strong rupee is undesirable, it creates disinflationary impulses.The central bank reiterated that it does not target a level for the exchange rate, nor does it have an overall target for foreign exchange reserves.While RBI continues to intervene (in both directions) on occasion, it is only to reduce avoidable volatility in the exchange rate, and any reserve build-up is a residual consequence of such actions rather than a direct objective.The bank justified its out-of-policy meeting actions by mentioning two reasons: While the next bi-monthly policy statement will be issued on April 7th, the still weak state of certain sectors of the economy as well as the global trend towards easing suggests that any policy action should be anticipatory once sufficient data support the policy stance.With the release of the agreement on the monetary policy framework, it is appropriate for the Reserve Bank to offer guidance on how it will implement the mandate.Going forward, the RBI will seek to bring the inflation rate to the mid-point of the band of 4 +/- 2% provided for in the agreement with the government, i.e., to 4% by the end of a two year period starting fiscal year 2016-17 (Apr-Mar).The current cut in policy rates was as expected.We believe that further rate cuts will be dependent on inflation dynamics, as quoted by the central bank: Further monetary actions will be conditioned by incoming data, especially on the easing of supply constraints, improved availability of key inputs such as power, land, minerals and infrastructure, continuing progress on high-quality fiscal consolidation, the pass through of past rate cuts into lending rates, the monsoon out-turn and developments in the international environment.If disinflationary trends continue, and inflation remains below 6%, we believe that, the central bank will be comfortable in cutting rates by 25-50bps during the current calendar year.Source: Reserve Bank Of India Eases Rates By 25 Bps;
