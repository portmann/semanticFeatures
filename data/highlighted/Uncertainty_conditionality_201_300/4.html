Includes: AYT , CEW , FORX , JEM , PGD , UDN , UDNT , USDU , UUP , UUPT by: Enzio von Pfeil Looming Emerging Markets (EM) debt crisis.In mid-December , we released a think piece on why the stronger dollar is paving the way for an almighty crisis: ill-advised EM corporates have been mismatching currencies.They have borrowed "cheap" dollars, and now are faced with a double-header: a stronger dollar (aka weaker local currency), along with the prospect of higher U.S. borrowing costs , courtesy of the Fed.Why a global crisis?EM corporates are/could get into hot water: saddled with significant dollar debt, their servicing costs in local currencies could create severe cash flow problems.This cash flow sickness could infect local EM banks, who are inter-connected with money-centre banks.So higher debt-servicing costs could cause all sorts of nasty ripple effects, ultimately landing on the doorsteps of money-centre banks.Endangered corporates.According to today's ever-erudite Financial Times (FT), particularly the South African rand, the Polish zloty and the Hungarian forint have tumbled against the dollar.The Indian rupee has not been doing too well, either.This means
