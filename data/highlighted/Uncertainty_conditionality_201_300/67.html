Why The Israeli Shekel Could Strengthen | Seeking Alpha Israeli economic growth improved in the fourth quarter.Meanwhile, the labor market also strengthened as violence in Gaza lessened in severity.As Israel's economy continues to stabilize, the shekel could appreciate against the U.S. dollar in coming months.Although the Bank of Israel continues to cut rates to fend off deflation, strength in the labor market, as well as improved economic activity could lead the shekel to appreciate against the U.S. dollar in coming months.Since August, the shekel has depreciated against the U.S. dollar by over 14%, but has consolidated in 2015, shown below.The Bank of Israel has consistently cut its benchmark rate to fend off deflation.In February, the Bank of Israel cut its lending rate to a record low of 0.1%, down 15 basis points from the previous 0.25%.Over the last year, the lending rate has been cut from close to 1%, to current levels, shown below.As inflation continues to run below its target rate , policymakers have been quick to make policy more accommodating."The Israeli central bank said in statement that it decided to cut the rate due to continuing declines in consumer prices, recent shekel appreciation, and lower interest rates globally.The bank said that with the consumer-price index down 0.9% on the month in January, and with annual inflation running at a negative 0.5%, inflation expectations remain below the government's target range of between 1% and 3% for annual inflation," according to the Wall Street Journal.Meanwhile, as rates have been lowered, economic growth spiked higher.In the fourth quarter, the economic growth figure came in at an annual pace of 7.2%, above the previous quarter's revised reading of 0.6%, as well as exceeding estimates for 0.21%.The most recent reading of economic growth reached its highest level since prior to the financial crisis.The lessening severity of conflict in Gaza, as well as an increase in exports aided in the strong growth figure ."The Gaza war in July and August held back third-quarter growth as factory output slowed, consumers stayed home and tourists canceled in droves.The economy expanded just 0.6%, versus an earlier estimate of 0.4% and an initial reading of a -0.4% contraction.Part of the rebound in the final three months of 2014 also stemmed from a sharp depreciation in the shekel versus the dollar, which boosted industrial exports -- mainly high-tech ones that benefited from a recovery in U.S. growth," according to Reuters.Additionally, the labor market in Israel strengthened in recent months.In January, the unemployment rate came in at 5.6%, below the previous month's reading of 5.7%, as well as bettering estimates for 5.8%.Over the summer months, the Gaza war led to increased unemployment, which is now stabilizing.With lower interest rates, and lessening violence in Gaza, both economic activity and the labor market have improved in Israel.The shekel could begin
