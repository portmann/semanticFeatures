2015.Ruble devaluation drives margin expansions and reduces 90% of CAPEX.USD/RUB should stay in place at least for the next three years.Management plans to return earnings and proceeds from asset in Norilsk, a town built by political detainees like himself in 1930s.Norilsk is the most northernmost city in the world.Located inside the Arctic Circle with an average annual temperature of -9.7 degrees Celsius, it is home to 170,000 people and the world's biggest producer of nickel and palladium, Open Joint Stock Company "Mining and Metallurgical Company Norilsk Nickel" ( OTCPK:NILSY ).(Source: Google Images.Compiled by author) Norilsk Nickel, together with its subsidiaries, is engaged in prospecting, exploration, extraction, refining and metallurgical processing of minerals, and production, marketing, and sale of base and precious metals.It is the world's largest producer of nickel and palladium with strong positions in copper, platinum, cobalt and rhodium, as well as in various by-products, such as cobalt, rhodium, silver, gold, iridium, ruthenium, selenium, tellurium and sulfur (Source: Capital IQ).It has operations in the Russian Federation, Finland, Australia, Botswana and South Africa (continually reduces exposure to this geographic area).The company's sales offices are located in the US, Europe, China, and Russia.(Source: Norilsk Nickel presentation at UBS GEM Conference in New York, December 2014) The company was incorporated in 1997 and is based in Moscow, the Russian Federation.Its origins date back to 1935 when the NKVD (better known as KGB) took control of "Norilsk Combine," built by the Soviet government.In the present time, Norilsk Nickel is an international company with assets across the globe and its trading business serving clients through the world's major ports.Note: Gray bars show companies I covered in my previous articles.The company with a market capitalization in excess of $28B caught my attention immediately after I analyzed its financials and product mix.I have decided to give it a thorough analysis, after which I concluded that the stock is a strong buy.I will attempt to prove the case for Norilsk Nickel.Metals market overview pertaining to the company's product mix Valuation: Comparable companies and DCF analyses ( excerpts from my model ) Metals and mining stocks have long been underperforming the broad market indexes.The decline started in 2012 when metal prices reached a peak and started falling.Many companies of this sector are now trading near the 2008-2009 levels.This situation offers many risk-tolerant investors unprecedented long-term buying opportunities.Norilsk Nickel's share price peaked earlier in mid-2011 in tandem with nickel prices.Since then, the stock traded 25%-35% below its post-crisis maximums in a sideways trend.In mid-2014, the shares tested a two-year maximum only to drop to 2010 price levels within 4-5 months.The sharpest fall took place in December 2014 when a massive Ruble devaluation took place amid record capital flights.However, compared to the world's biggest metals and mining companies, Norilsk Nickel has been a top producer of investment returns in its sector.The stock currently has a beta of 1.08 and a cost of equity of circa 17% (country risk accounted for).Most importantly, Norilsk Nickel is not on the sanctions list and has access to international capital markets.Cheap debt plays an important role in the company's capital structure: Net Debt (~$3.4B) brings the weighted-average cost of capital down to 12%, which makes the company's projects economically viable.With the exception of palladium, whose prices have been growing since the crisis of 2008-2009 without major shocks, all other metals, which comprise more than three quarters of Norilsk Nickel's product portfolio, have been in decline over the last three years: World Bank forecast for basic metals is grim: Their real prices are expected to continue IQ .All infographics by author) Over the past year, Norilsk Nickel managed to restart growing revenues after three years of declines.Margins have stabilized and net income is expected to be near $2B for FY 2014 (LTM data shows $1.7B as of June 30, 2014).These events have improved return-on-asset, return-on-capital and return-on-equity ratios.The differences among them are minimal, which can be explained by high-equity concentration in the capital structure and low operating leverage due to high margins.Further reductions in net debt caused debt ratios to continue falling.(Source: Capital IQ.All infographics by author) (Source: Norilsk Nickel presentation at UBS GEM Conference in New York, December 2014) Revenues from sales of nickel comprise over 40% of revenue mix while copper and palladium make up approximately a quarter each, and platinum contributes 9% to the corporate coffers.Other metals, many of which are precious, play an insignificant role in revenue generation.Geographically, Europe contributes to over 50% of Norilsk Nickel's revenues.Asian clients contribute 30% of total sales while North America and Russia equally share 9% in the revenue mix.Overall, sales are USD denominated while costs are primarily paid for in Rubles.CAPEX is 90% RUB denominated.In terms of credit management, Norilsk Nickel demonstrates caution: With 38% of assets financed by debt and even a lower ratio of net debt-to-enterprise value, the company is actually underleveraged compared to peers.Among competitors, Norilsk Nickel holds second place in credit scores: On the other hand, amid current uncertainties on the commodities market, it may be prudent to have a larger cash cushion and extra debt capacity.Norilsk Nickel has both.Dividends Overview and
