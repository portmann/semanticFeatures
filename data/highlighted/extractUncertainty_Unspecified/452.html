How Spain's Consumers Are Affecting The Euro | Seeking Alpha Spain's retail sales continue to print strong readings.Meanwhile, consumer confidence and employment data are also trending higher.Although the euro remains weak due to the ECB stimulus program, underlying economies around the currency bloc, such as Spain, are strengthening.Spain's economy continues to strengthen as consumer spending, sentiment, and the labor market all trend higher.The euro is represented by CurrencyShares Euro Trust (NYSEARCA: FXE ), seen below.Spanish retail sales have broadly increased the last few months.In January, the retail sales figure came in at an annual pace of 4.1%, below the previous month's revised reading of 6.3%, but exceeding estimates for 2.4%.After barely growing during the first half of 2014, retail sales in Spain have accelerated to current levels.The pickup in consumer spending further signals improvement in the country's domestic demand."Spanish retail sales rose 4.1% on a calendar-adjusted basis in January, in a further sign of Spain's recovery in domestic demand, according to data from the National Statistics Institute on Tuesday.Retail sales have grown for six straight months and nine out of the past 10 months after a three-year period when they mostly fell during a long economic downturn," according to the Business Times.Meanwhile, as retail sales improved, so did consumer confidence.In February, the consumer confidence figure came in at 100.81, up from the previous month's reading of 100.71.At current levels, the consumer confidence reading has surpassed pre-recession levels, signaling further economic strength in the future.Lastly, consumer confidence and spending have been aided by a strong improvement in Spain's labor market.In the fourth quarter, the employment figure grew at an annual pace of 1.19%, up from the previous quarter's reading of 0.26%.Since 2009, the employment figure has rebounded from -6% annual contraction, to current levels, seen below.As Spain's labor market improved, consumer confidence and spending have drastically increased.This led to strength in Spain's economic growth figure.With headline inflation still low due to weak energy prices, ECB policymakers have been biased towards easing policy measures.Continued stimulus should weigh on the value of the euro, but underlying economies in the currency
