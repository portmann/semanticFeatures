Priceline Might Be Planning A Big Acquisition, Possibly Yelp!- Priceline Group Inc. (NASDAQ:PCLN) | Seeking Alpha To screen ETFs by asset class, performance, yield and more, check out the ETF Hub.ETF Hub PCLN's offering of debt twice in the last two weeks suggests it is planning a big move.Some have suggested PCLN is raising debt to fund $3 billion acquisition, but with $8 billion in cash, there would be no need.Instead, PCLN might be planning a strategic acquisition to grow its business.Just two weeks after announcing a debt offering, Priceline Group (NASDAQ: PCLN ) is offering yet more Senior Notes (dollar instead of euro).While the proceeds from this debt is unknown, it is quite unusual for a company of any size to enter two public offerings this quickly, especially one with $8 billion in cash & equivalents and that has over $2.75 billion of annual free cash flow.Granted, interest rates are low, but the fact that Priceline is aggressively raising capital suggests it's up to something big, much larger than financing a $3 billion buyback program that can be paid using its cash-on-hand.That's why I think Priceline is preparing to make a rather large acquisition, possibly Yelp (NYSE: YELP ).Reviews have become a cornerstone of the consumer experience; as they tell consumers what to expect when dining, shopping or traveling.That's why Priceline has worked so hard to build its "guest reviews" over the last few years.However, the importance of reviews is not the only reason why Priceline might have interest in a leading review site like Yelp.For quite some time investors have speculated that Priceline is interested in entering new industries such as restaurant, event, and attraction bookings.This theory was further supported when Priceline bought OpenTable last year.Therefore, Priceline already has the technology to book in industries outside of travel, thereby making the travel experience that much more enjoyable for its customers once fully implemented.What Priceline still lacks are thorough reviews for restaurants, events, attractions, etc., which is where Yelp comes into play.With Google (NASDAQ: GOOG ) (NASDAQ: GOOGL ) entering the travel space, and starting to gain momentum in North America, gaining a large collection of reviews across many industries has never been so important.Not only did Google gain this when it acquired Zagat several years back, but Google Maps and its social media assets have made it increasingly convenient for consumers to "check-in" at various places, and also write a review on just about anything in the world that has a physical address.Therefore, if Priceline were to acquire Yelp, it would not only strengthen its ex-travel bookings business, but also compete with the likes of Google and Facebook that seemingly have reviews on just about everything these days.That said, if Priceline were to consider an acquisition of Yelp, there has never been a better time to do so.The stock is trading at 18-month lows with a market capitalization of just $3.4 billion.While that represents a price/sales ratio of 9.0, investors must remember that Yelp is now a profitable business, and despite its stock loss it's still expected to grow revenue 53% this year and 35% in 2016.As a result, Yelp would give Priceline a fast-growing business, a new revenue channel and all of the noted perks at a price that hasn't been seen in quite some time.All things considered, it is possible that my "buy Yelp" chant will never happen, but even if not, I do think that Priceline's activity suggests it is planning something big.In my opinion, reviews make sense, as Priceline already has a leading market presence in international markets, but where it could perform better is in the U.S. Yelp could help the company do just that, and as an investor, I for one would be happy to see it become a
