Alcatel-Lucent Looks To Dominate This $16 Billion Market - Alcatel-Lucent (NYSE:ALU) | Seeking Alpha To screen ETFs by asset class, performance, yield and more, check out the ETF Hub.ETF Hub ALU was celebrated at Mobile World Congress for its innovation in VoLTE.The company has now unveiled a technology that puts it way ahead of the competition in this space, even more so than it already was.With VoLTE expected to become a $16 billion market, ALU has a good opportunity to capitalize, and perhaps grow VoLTE into something larger than anyone expected.At Mobile World Congress 2015, Alcatel-Lucent (NYSE: ALU ) had quite a showing.Not only did Citi upgrade the stock to Buy after attending the conference, but it was given an award for " Best Mobile Network Product for Servicing Customers " in favor of competitors Juniper and Ericsson.As many would expect, this award had to do with internet protocol, but more specifically voice-over-LTE ((VoLTE)).To elaborate, Alcatel-Lucent is taking critical pieces of technology to advance the movement of VoLTE worldwide; a move that could be very lucrative for the company and its investors long-term.VoLTE is just now starting to gain traction here in the U.S., but the concept has been building for much of the last three years.Last year, Verizon (NYSE: VZ ) and T-Mobile (NYSE: TMUS ) became the first wireless service providers to offer voice services over an LTE network.AT&T (NYSE: T ) is expected to follow this year.This move paves the way for older legacy GSM and CDMA voice networks to be retired and for customers to gain the advantages that come with voice services over a far superior network.Verizon's 4G network is advertised to be 10x faster than 3G networks, and 3G networks are known as being far more powerful than CDMA networks.With this increase in power, VoLTE allows for fewer dropped calls, better call quality, faster connectivity, and HD video chatting among other services.It also allows wireless service providers to bill voice as data, thereby increasing data consumption long-term.Therefore, VoLTE is obviously an important market; the future of voice calling.Back in 2013, Infonetics predicted that VoLTE would grow at a 145% compound annual rate from 2012 through 2017 until becoming a $16 billion market.Clearly, wireless service companies will take a large chunk of this market due to the increase in data consumption, but equipment vendors will also gain due to higher CAPEX in this arena, along with the licensing fees that wireless companies pay to use VoLTE equipment like servers, APIs, etc.According to the Global Mobile Awards Ceremony, Alcatel-Lucent is leading the way with technology for VoLTE.The company's virtualized VoLTE platform has allowed for services like high definition voice and video, IP messaging, and content sharing that opens new windows of opportunity for both wireless service providers and consumers.Furthermore, at Mobile World Conference 2015, Alcatel-Lucent became the first company to unveil a single network that uses both WiFi and cellular networks as one.Notably, Alcatel-Lucent stated that its VoLTE technology allows for convergence across fixed, cellular, and WiFi networks.This means that Alcatel-Lucent is ready to provide VoLTE services to wireless service providers that don't have the best of 4G networks (ie Sprint, China Unicom, etc.) by combining WiFi and 4G into one network.In my opinion, this innovation puts Alcatel-Lucent into a world of its own in the fast-growing VoLTE market, and further strengthens its already valuable IP business.In other words, it's a great time to be an Alcatel-Lucent long.Source: Alcatel-Lucent Looks To Dominate This $16 Billion Market Disclosure: The author is long ALU.(More...)The
