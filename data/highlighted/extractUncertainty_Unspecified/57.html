What Direction Will The Thai Baht Go Next?| Seeking Alpha The Bank of Thailand recently cut its benchmark lending rate.Moreover, employment and inflation measures in the country remain weak.With policymakers leaning towards further easing, the baht could fall against the dollar in coming months.The Thai baht may weaken further in coming months as its labor market, alongside headline inflation readings lead to policy easing.Over the last year, the baht has traded sideways against the U.S. dollar, seen below.In March, the Bank of Thailand decided to cut its benchmark lending rate to accommodate lower inflation and a weakening labor market.The benchmark rate was cut to 1.75%, 25 basis-points below the previous reading of 2%, as well as missing estimates for 2% as well.Since 2011, the benchmark lending rate has fallen from 3.5%, to current levels.With weakness in China, a major trading partner, policymakers felt it was right to cut the lending rate to reflect economic uncertainty ."In the fourth quarter of 2014 and January 2015, the Thai economy continued to recover slowly, as the economic momentum from private consumption and investment was softer than expected owing in part to weaker private sector's confidence.In the periods ahead, the economy is projected to recover at a slower pace than formerly assessed.Exports of goods are expected to recover at a rate close to the previous projection, but with higher downside risks from a slowdown in trading partners' economies, notably China.Meanwhile, tourism is projected to recover steadily, partially offsetting the weaker domestic demand," according to Trading Economics.As China slowed over the past year, and political strife lingered, employment in the country took a drastic hit.In January, the employment figure came in at an annual pace of -2.21%, below the previous month's reading of -2.18%.After peaking at close to 1.5% growth in 2012, the employment situation in Thailand deteriorated, seen below.Data provided by the Bank of Thailand Additionally, headline inflation in the country broadly declined the last few months.In February, the inflation figure came in at an annual pace of -0.52%, below the previous month's reading of -0.41%, as well as missing estimates for -0.33%.Since peaking at over 2% in 2014, the inflation measures has fallen drastically lower.Low global energy prices are largely being blamed for the weak inflation reading."In the first two months of 2015, headline inflation declined and turned negative due to low global oil prices.Looking ahead, inflationary pressure is forecasted to remain at a low level, close to the committee's assessment at the last meeting," according to Trading Economics.With weak inflation, and a struggling labor market, interest rates in Thailand will likely remain low.This should allow the U.S. dollar to appreciate against the baht in coming months as the Federal Reserve leans towards tightening policy.Source: What Direction Will The
