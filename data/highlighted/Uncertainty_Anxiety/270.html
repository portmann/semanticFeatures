Utility/REIT ETFs Plunge On Rate Hike Speculation | Seeking Alpha Mar. 11, 2015 6:39 PM ET �|� Includes: FXU , ICF , IDU , IYR , REM , RWR , VNQ , VPU , XLU by: Zacks Funds Despite rocky global markets, the U.S. appeared to be quite well placed since the start of the year, led by a strengthening domestic economy, an improving job picture and positive housing data.In fact, the U.S. jobs market is going from strength to strength, hitting record levels at regular intervals.According to the U.S. Department of Labor, the U.S. economy created 2.95 million new jobs in 2014, its highest tally since 1999.Continuing with last year's momentum, the U.S. economy added 295,000 jobs in February, a substantial improvement over the 257,000 new jobs created in January.The latest jobs data marks the 12th straight month of the economy adding more than 200,000 jobs.This is indeed the best pace of hiring seen in the last two decades.Moreover, the unemployment rate now stands at 5.5%, the lowest level since May 2008.The recent upbeat jobs data has raised expectations that the Federal Reserve may hike interest rates as early as June, rather than in the third quarter of 2015, as previously estimated.This is especially true as the latest upbeat unemployment rate has hit the upper bound of the 5.2-5.5% range, which the Fed believes signals full employment.Also, average hourly wages of $24.78 rose modestly by 3 cents in February, though wage growth still remains an area of concern.The latest talk about an earlier-than-expected rate hike has rattled the U.S. REIT and utility markets.This is because both these sectors are extremely sensitive to changes in interest rates, and the recently published upbeat data has pushed Treasury bond yields higher.Last Friday, the 10-year Treasury note saw the biggest one-day sell-off since November 2013, with its yield surging to 2.239% - the highest closing level since December 26.This huge reversal led utility and REIT ETFs to post negative returns over the past five trading sessions.A rising interest rate environment raises concerns about the performance of borrowed money, which in turn, would impact the dividend yield.This has led investors to book profits in the high dividend-paying avenues of utility and REIT ETFs.Almost all the funds in the utility ETF space have seen losses in the past five trading sessions, and in fact, are trading in the red in the year-to-date frame.The Utilities Select Sector SPDR ETF (NYSEARCA: XLU ), the largest and the most popular ETF in its space, with an asset base of $6.2 billion and average daily trading volume of 16 million shares, has shed roughly 2% in the past one week and is down 7.9% in the year-to-date frame.The fund tracks the Utilities Select Sector Index, holding a basket of 30 stocks, and has a solid dividend yield of 3.31%.Apart from XLU, the Vanguard Utilities ETF (NYSEARCA: VPU ), the iShares U.S. Utilities ETF (NYSEARCA: IDU ) and the First Trust Utilities AlphaDEX ETF (NYSEARCA: FXU ) have also lost roughly 2% in the past five trading sessions.These products are also down more than 5% in the year-to-date frame.Like the utility ETFs, most of the REIT ETFs have also delivered negative returns in the past five trading sessions and are trading flat to negative in the year-to-date frame.Vanguard REIT Index ETF (NYSEARCA: VNQ ) - the most popular product in the REIT space, with an asset base of $27.5 million - is down 3.3% in the past one week.The fund has a dividend yield of 3.63%.Other popular products in the space like the iShares U.S. Real Estate ETF (NYSEARCA: IYR ), the iShares Cohen & Steers REIT ETF (NYSEARCA: ICF ), the SPDR Dow Jones REIT ETF (NYSEARCA: RWR ) and the iShares Mortgage Real Estate Capped ETF (NYSEARCA: REM ) have also lost more than 2% in the past five trading sessions.The worries about the actual timing of the first interest rate hike in the U.S. since 2006 is pushing interest rate-sensitive sectors like utility and REIT downward, leading many of the funds to shed their earlier gains and trade in the red in the year-to-date frame.As such, investors should cautiously trade the above two markets and might consider staying on the sidelines until there is some clarity about the timing of the rate hike.We only use your contact details to reply to your request for more information.We do not sell the personal contact data you submit to anyone else.Thank you for your interest in Seeking Alpha PRO We look forward to contacting you shortly for a conversation.Our PRO subscription service was created for fund managers, and the cost of the product is prohibitive for most individual investors.PRO Alerts is our flagship product for individual investors who want to be faster and smarter about their stocks.To learn more about it, click here.If you are an investment professional with over $1M AUM and received this message in error, click here and you will be contacted shortly.We look forward to contacting you when we have an individual investor product ready!
