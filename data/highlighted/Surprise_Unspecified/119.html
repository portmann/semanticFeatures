Imprivata: High Expectations Should Prop The Share Price Until At Least Q3 - Imprivata (NYSE:IMPR) | Seeking Alpha IMPR management has done a wonderful job at delaying accountability until at least 2H/15.IMPR has somehow convinced the analyst community that a 35%-90% share price rise is on the table.It's because of this misplaced analyst enthusiasm and managements guidance that 2H/15 is when financials should turn for 2015 that I have to move on from the short side.Imprivata (NYSE: IMPR ), the single sign on provider for the healthcare services industry, held serve with its underwhelming Q4 earnings and following CC.IMPR since its IPO has been one of the better short positions available.IMPR shares of course have been driven down to $12.75 as of today from pricing of near $17 at IPO on the reality that it has significant existential concerns.I've detailed these since its IPO for PRO subscribers at each quarterly reporting.I regret to inform my fellow short sellers, however, that I am closing my short deep in the green (inclusive of costs to borrow) based on the fact that I think shares can hold this pricing for at least the next few quarters.Still I must say that even today with its recent record full year and Q4 revenue reporting IMPR has done nothing to change or alter the short thesis in any way.This closing of position is being driven entirely by opportunity cost and not upside risk.IMPR still provides a service at a what I speculate is a more expensive rate than peers, doesn't provide any apparent ancillary services of value outside of this completely replaceable and low barrier to entry service, and it still is providing this service at a great distance to profitability.Again, to reiterate, I have a high conviction in the above listed bear thesis points in that: I believe if IMPR did in fact offer ancillary services of value that it would be able to generate a higher composition of sales to new customers rather than its existing customers I believe if IMPR's services were unique, not completely replaceable, or offered as an at cost retention tool by incumbents offering higher margin ancillary services that IMPR would be able to generate consistently increasing gross margins excluding period to period fluctuations in product mix I believe that IMPR as a result of the above two points will face a huge uphill battle in closing the huge profitability gap that exists despite what management has guided.For clarity, management has guided profitability 2H/16 - a goalpost I believe will continuously be moved further and further out.But, with the analyst community guiding in-line full year revenues with management ($121 million at the mid-point) and having absurdly rosy price target projections I could see shares holding this range until at least Q3/15 which is when management guided that it should see FY15 Adjusted EBITDA loss and total operating expenses slow (in rate of growth compared to the 1H/15): Based on the above factors I am closing my short position and moving on to greener (or redder in the case of short selling) pastures.Source: Imprivata: High Expectations Should Prop The Share Price Until At Least Q3
