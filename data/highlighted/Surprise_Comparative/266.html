HDFC Bank: A Bank One Cannot Avoid - HDFC Bank Limited (NYSE:HDB) | Seeking Alpha HDFC Bank shifted its gear to keep earnings growth steady.Excellent asset quality continues to help the earnings growth while many banks have been struggling with their worst performances due to poor asset quality.While being one of the most successful and well-run company in India, HDFC Bank (NYSE: HDB ) has been delivering stellar earnings growth for many years.HDB recently reported stellar earnings results in the most recent quarter.The Bank earned a net profit of Rs.2,794.5 crores in Q3 FY15, an increase of 20.2% over Q3 FY14.Corporate loans grew by 22% year on year, and retail loan growth was only 11% y-o-y.It looks like increased dependence on corporate loan growth to grow its loan book is increasing the risk of investment in HDFC Bank.In India, corporate loans got the reputation of having high probability to default.Heavy exposure to corporate loans has dragged down the performance of most of the public sector banks in recent quarters , and it continues to do the same.HDB has showed more interest in growing its retail loan growth in the past, and it still has high exposure to retail loans, with 51% of its loan book consisting of retail loans.Therefore, the slow growth in retail loans does imply that retail lending opportunities are very low.That has forced it to focus more on corporate loan book to keep growing.It is hard to maintain balance between appetite for growth and being prudent while performing one's duty, but HDFC Bank's management has been doing a great job at balancing both the responsibilities.While growing consistently, HDFC Bank has been maintaining high-quality assets that are crucial for a bank to keep earnings growth steady.HDB Net Income (Annual) data by YCharts HDFC Bank had the second-lowest net nonperforming assets (lower better) among public and private sector banks at the end of Q1 2015.At the end of 2014, HDFC Bank's 0.26% net advances were net nonperforming assets.Low nonperforming assets decrease the need to allocate higher amount of income to reserve account to cover nonperforming loans.This kind of excellent asset quality is only possible to few disciplined managements.Companies have been urging RBI to lower interest rates for many quarters to spur growth; finally, they are getting what they have craved for.Consumer inflation was at 5.11% for January 2015, slightly increasing from 5% in December 2014 and well below the RBI's medium-term target of 6%.Moderating inflation raises the hope for another interest rate cut in the next monetary policy in April, after surprising interest rate cuts in January and this month .Low interest rates increase consumer spending that improves industrial growth, resulting in more loan production for banks.While it takes some time to reach an interest rate level that will kick start loan production, recent interest rate cuts are an entry point to a gradual decline in interest rates.However, there are risks to more interest rate cuts.The significant decline in inflation that caused interest rate cuts is not due to the efforts of new government eliminating too many parties involved (intermediaries) in the supply chain.Rather, it is due to the sharp decline in oil prices that has led to the decline in inflation.If the oil prices rise, then the hopes of more interest rate cuts will go in vain.How the new government manages the demand and supply of food items ahead of southwest monsoon is another critical factor that can change the inflation outlook in the near future.Disciplined management is the real recipe behind the consistent growth results of HDFC Bank.Management has tactically changed its focus towards corporate loans to keep the earnings growth steady.Moderating inflation strengthens the case for improvement in loan growth rates.I expect much better days ahead for HDFC Bank if interest rates continue to decline.Source: HDFC Bank: A Bank One
