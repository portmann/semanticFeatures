PacWest Bancorp: Looks Promising, But Extra Caution Needed - PacWest Bancorp (NASDAQ:PACW) | Seeking Alpha PacWest is highly focused on expanding its banking presence and earning assets through acquisitions.Leakages to income due to poor asset quality and fat cost structure have dented net income growth in recent years.At present, investing in PACW seems like a good decision, but one has to be extra cautious in any adverse conditions in the future.The acquisition of CapitalSource, Inc. provided the much needed boost to the earnings growth of PacWest Bancorp (NASDAQ: PACW ) in 2014.It is highly focused on expanding its banking presence and earning assets through acquisitions.The company has completed 27 acquisitions since 2000; its rapid movement in acquisitions that help net income growth is not supported by asset quality and cost structure.Leakages to income due to poor asset quality and fat cost structure have dented net income growth of PacWest in recent years.Poor underwriting practices had led to huge amount of charge-offs during and after the Great Recession.PACW had net charge-offs of 5.88% to average non-purchased credit impaired loans and leases in 2010.Some banks (one example: Bank of the Ozarks (NASDAQ: OZRK )) have posted net income growth during the Great Recession due to good underwriting practices.Therefore, those who think that high net charge-offs are common in highly-distressed environments; those kinds of assumptions can be proved wrong by committed managements of companies like Bank of the Ozarks.It is not reasonable to expect the same kind of commitment from every company, so a look at the average net charge-offs of FDIC insured institutions gives a clearer picture.PacWest Bancorp's net charge-offs of 5.88% were significantly higher than the industry average of 2.55% in 2010.To improve its ability to participate in bidding on FDIC-assisted acquisitions, PACW sold $398.5 million non-covered classified loans.The loss from these loans aggravated the loss that the company made in 2010."During 2010, we made strategic decisions to sell $398.5 million of non-covered classified loans to reduce credit risk, thereby strengthening the Bank's balance sheet and enhancing its ability to continue to participate in bidding on FDIC-assisted acquisitions.The loans sold included $128.1 million in nonaccrual loans and $148.8 million in performing restructured loans.All of the loans sold were originated by Pacific Western and none were covered loans acquired in the Los Padres acquisition or Affinity Bank ("Affinity") acquisition (completed on August 28, 2009).These sales were for cash of $254.6 million and were completed on a servicing-released basis and without recourse to Pacific Western.Such sales resulted in immediate reductions of non-covered classified loans and improved credit quality metrics."The standards that managements maintain in underwriting practices can survive the worst conditions successfully if they are highly conservative, if not the opposite will happen.That is what happened in the case of PACW, and its desire to acquire more banks has aggravated the losses it made.Another leakage that has dented earnings growth is high expenditures.Banks that have a lean cost structure tend to have efficiency ratio in the range of 45% to 55%.PACW's efficiency ratio was 72.40% and 76.40% in 2012 and 2013, respectively.PacWest's rapid activity in acquisitions definitely deserves applause, but lack of focus on leakages discussed above and more weightage to acquisitions degraded the net income growth of PACW in the recent past.Though the adverse conditions are a thing of past, any occurrence of such kind of conditions (sure to happen) might change the smooth ride PACW is having at present.Is PACW prepared to face such kind of conditions in the future with better mechanism?At present, PACW is in a good position with good cost structure, asset quality, and overall performance, due to healthy and growing economic environment.2014 is an excellent year for PACW, mostly due to the merger of CapitalSource, Inc.In 2014, total assets grew by 148.5% from 2013 and net earnings grew by 274% in the same period.PACW is all set to complete the acquisition of Square 1 Financial, Inc. (NASDAQ: SQBK ) in the fourth quarter of 2015 with few steps left.Square 1 has $3.1 billion in assets at Dec. 31, 2014.Investing in PACW seems like a good decision, but one has to be extra cautious in adverse conditions in the future.Source: PacWest Bancorp: Looks Promising, But Extra Caution Needed Disclosure: The author has no positions in any stocks mentioned, and no plans to initiate any positions within the next 72 hours.(More...)The author wrote this article themselves, and it expresses their own opinions.The author is not receiving compensation for it (other than from Seeking Alpha).The author has no business relationship with any company whose stock is mentioned in this article.We only use your contact details to reply to your request for more information.We do not sell the personal contact data you submit to anyone else.Thank you for your interest in Seeking Alpha PRO We look forward to contacting you shortly for a conversation.Our PRO subscription service was created for fund managers, and the cost of the product is prohibitive for most individual investors.PRO Alerts is our flagship product for individual investors who want to be faster and smarter about their stocks.To learn more about it, click here.If you are an investment professional with over $1M AUM and received this message in error, click here and you will be contacted shortly.We look forward to contacting you when we have an individual investor product ready!
