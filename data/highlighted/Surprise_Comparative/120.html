Is India Heading Towards A Housing Bubble?| Seeking Alpha The Indian government has proposed to create 100 smart cities across India.Defaults on housing loan could rise."Smart Cities" is the ambitious project of the Indian government to create 100 new smart cities and satellite towns across India.The Indian government has allocated $1.2 billion in the current fiscal year, and is looking for private participations in this project.Initially, smart cities are planned across the 1500 km Delhi - Mumbai Industrial Corridor that runs through six Indian states.Recently, Michael Bloomberg announced a partnership with the Indian government towards this project.Bloomberg Philanthropies will assist the government in selecting smart cities.This project will create new housing for the growing urban class.However, the Indian government needs to look at the health of the Indian housing sector.In 2014, Delhi, Mumbai, National Capital Region (or NCR), and Bangalore accounted for 70% of the new housing supply.Residential sales have declined 30% Y/Y across seven major cities in India.There is a huge pile up of unsold housing inventory.Mumbai and NCR region will take between twelve and fourteen quarters to sell the existing inventory.High prices, sticky interest rates, and cautious buyer sentiment are cited to be the key reasons for poor sales.In spite of this, real estate developers have increased prices.Mumbai saw price hike of 10%, while the NCR witnessed a hike of 3% in 2014.High prices will keep home buyers away.The inventory pileup will put stress on the Indian banking system.The real estate and housing sector account for 13% to 14% of Indian banks' exposure.Defaults on housing sector loans are on the rise.Nonperforming assets or bad loans in the housing segment rose by $161.29 million (INR10 billion) to $1 billion ( INR62 billion) during the first half of 2014.The Indian economy is dependent of foreign capital inflows and is susceptible to global shocks.A slowdown in the economy will affect housing sales, and lead to inventory pile up.The net impact would be more bad loans for Indian banks.The Indian government should learn from China's housing bubble that started deflating from 2011.Housing prices in China started falling as the middle class were unable to afford homes in large cities.China has also given rise to many ghost cities, where many residential apartments are unoccupied.Home prices fell in 64 cities last month, compared to 65 cities in December.The housing sector contributes 15% to China's economy and is primarily responsible for the slowdown in the economy.In 2014, the Chinese economy grew at 7.4%, it slowest pace in 24 years.The Indian government should focus on affordable housing rather than creating smart cities.The smart city project will need more investment both from the government and the private sector.If smart cities remain unoccupied, then it will add to existing inventory, which can affect the
