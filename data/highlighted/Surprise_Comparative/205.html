How Japanese Growth Is Affecting The Yen | Seeking Alpha Japanese economic growth emerged out of recession in the most recent quarter.Meanwhile, economic sentiment and the labor market also trended higher.The Japanese yen strengthened against many of its foreign counterparts as economic growth, sentiment, and the labor market all improved the last few months.The Japanese yen is represented by CurrencyShares Japanese Yen Trust (NYSEARCA: FXY ).After declining earlier in 2014, economic growth in Japan gradually rebounded.In the fourth quarter, the economic growth figure came in at an annual pace of 1.5%, above the previous quarter's reading of -2.3%, while slightly missing estimates for 2.2%.Over the last year, economic growth rebounded from -6.7% annual contraction, to current levels, seen below.Japan's economy was able to escape recession in the final quarter of 2014, but consumption and investment still have room to recover."While October-December GDP grew for the first time in three quarters and gave reassurance that the economy is bottoming out, the results also underscored anew the weakness in the recovery of private consumption and corporate capital expenditure," Junichi Makino, chief economist at SMBC Nikko Securities Inc., told the Japan Times.Meanwhile, as economic growth recovered, economic sentiment has similarly risen.In February, the Eco Watchers survey of current conditions figure came in at 50.1, above the previous month's reading of 45.6, while also exceeding estimates for 43.62.A reading above 50 signals expansion.Over the last few months, the sentiment figure has risen from 41.5 in November, to current levels.The culmination of an improving economy and strengthening labor market helped propel sentiment readings higher.As was stated above, Japan's labor market drastically improved the last few years.In January, the employment to population figure grew at an annual pace of 0.84%, above the previous month's reading of 0.82%.Since bottoming in early 2010 at -1.57% annual contraction, employment has trended higher, rebounding to pre-recession growth levels.With economic activity and the labor market both improving over the last few months, economic sentiment in the region increased.While the Bank of Japan remains committed to policy easing, the yen could move higher against other countries also embarking on easing due to the
