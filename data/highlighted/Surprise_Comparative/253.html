MaxPoint Interactive An IPO For An Attractive Acquisition Target | Seeking Alpha MaxPoint Interactive An IPO For An Attractive Acquisition Target MaxPoint Interactive offers large companies unique cloud-based analytical services focus on local marketing efforts.MaxPoint runs a financially strong business, which has seen exponential growth over the past few years.MaxPoint Interactive's (Pending: MXPT ) initial public offering marks the third IPO this year for a cloud-based services and software company.Inovalon (NASDAQ: INOV ) and Box Inc (NYSE: BOX ) both issued IPOs in February and January, respectively.Both of those IPOs were met with initial success, however Box's stock has cooled a little following the sell-off post-hype.MaxPoint, an advertising technology firm, also follows two big IPOs from ad tech companies in 2014 - Rubicon (NYSE: RUBI ) and TubeMogul (NASDAQ: TUBE ) - both have seen stock market success following their debuts.Investors should not be excited just because the industry is hot.Investors should be excited by MaxPoint as it offers excellent competitive advantages with a great value proposition which positions them nicely as a future acquisition target.MaxPoint basically sells marketing-focused business intelligence software that allows companies to focus on very local advertising, positioning, and placement.The software sells large-multinational corporations the ability to focus on very specific consumer trends within a micro-environment.This very specific focus on local consumers is similar to the way an owner of a single boutique knows and leverages the local market.Behind this intimate knowledge offering is the power of an analytical instrument that has proven to be very effective in learning and understanding the local consumer.The basic premise of the software is that it separates a large market into minute and manageable segments, which MaxPoint has termed "Digital Zips."From there, the software allows users to focus on individual stores and the hyper-local market around them to foster marketing efforts.MaxPoint creates value for clients and ultimately shareholders by focusing efforts on a high-growth market.Reports by BIA/Kelsey indicate that spending on digital, local marketing will almost double over the next five years.Currently the market is highly fragmented with most competitors being locally based advertising agencies, and currently little competition is offered on specialized analytical services.The likes of Gannett Co. (NYSE: GCI ) and McClatchy Co (NYSE: MNI ) are some of the only large companies that have some form of operations in this current arena.A review of MaxPoint IPO filing reveals that the competitive advantage is clearly creating value for customers.On a year-over-year basis, revenue from MaxPoint's 25 largest customer's doubled.This same-customer growth was compounded by a steady increase in the overall number of customers.In 2013, MaxPoint averaged 84 campaigns per customer, compared to just 37 per customer in 2012.The growth in existing customers demand clearly speaks to the ability of MaxPoint to create value for businesses.MaxPoint's growth was recognized by Deloitte as number 40 in the Fast Growing 500 .IPOs come with a large amount of risk from imperfect information to misaligned objectives between buyer and seller.That is why IPO investors need to think more like early-stage investors.Key to early-stage investment strategy - exit strategy.Investors should always be thinking about how and when they leave the stock and what are the likely future outcomes and developments.For MaxPoint, it is an attractive IPO because it offers a potentially great exit opportunity - acquisition.Think about like a large-corporation - theirs value in acquiring a high-growth, strong margin company that currently carries little debt.Take a company like Gannett for example, it is a slow-growth media and marketing conglomerate that has been recently reorganizing and changing focus towards emerging technologies.A company like Gannett could easily acquire MaxPoint and quickly reap benefits from a variety of synergies, plus the additional sales force to propel continued growth.MaxPoint's market valuation post-IPO should come in around $260-$300 million, this would be a small drop in the bucket for the current $1.3 billion in cash and equivalents on hand at Gannett.MaxPoint offers a great opportunity to IPO investors.They sell a great product with fast-growing demand.On top of that, not only have they proven the ability to grow their customer base quickly, but also they have shown drastic increases in same-customer sales.MaxPoint offers a technology that could quickly consolidate a highly fragmented fast growing market.Investors should be very excited for the long-term outlook.On top of this, large media and marketing companies looking to enter segments like MaxPoint's.For these companies, there is a compelling argument to enter a segment by acquiring an already successful company.Source: MaxPoint Interactive An IPO For An Attractive Acquisition
