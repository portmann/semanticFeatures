How Employment Data Are Affecting The U.S. Dollar | Seeking Alpha The U.S. Job Openings and Labor Turnover Survey continued to improve in January.Additionally, real earnings and economic activity are also growing.With a stronger labor market likely leading to tighter monetary policy in coming months, the U.S. dollar should continue to rise.The U.S. dollar continues to move higher as its labor market strengthens.PowerShares DB US Dollar Index Bullish (NYSEARCA: UUP ) is up close to 23% since July, seen below.This week the Job Openings and Labor Turnover Survey was released, giving an alternative view to the U.S. employment picture.One of the widely watched indicators within the report is quits.To many, quits represents the balance of power within the labor market.When quits are high, it signals that laborers feel confident enough to seek other employment, and that optimism is improving.In January, the quits figure grew at an annual pace of 10.87%, above the previous month's reading of 9.74%.Quits have gradually risen since bottoming in 2009, seen below.While the overall level of quits is still below pre-recession levels, the solid pace of growth is promising.Meanwhile, job openings have also increased the last few years as employers expanded payrolls.In January, the job openings figure came in at a 17.97% annual pace, above the previous month's reading of 16%.With strong job openings growth, the figure has almost reached pre-recession totals, seen below.Moreover, the creation of more quality jobs is leading to higher weekly earnings, as I referenced in a previous article .Data provided by the Federal Reserve Lastly, hiring in the U.S. is also approaching decade high growth levels.In January, the hiring figure came in at an annual pace of 7.95% growth, above the previous month's reading of 7.6%.The current pace of growth exceeds that of the previous high in 2005 of 6.5%, signaling just how rapid the expansion in hiring is.With hiring, openings, and quits all trending higher, U.S. consumers are directly benefiting.Real wages, consumer spending, as well as overall economic activity should improve in coming months alongside employment.As the U.S. economy expands, Fed officials will lean towards tightening policy, benefiting the dollar.Source: How Employment Data Are Affecting The U.S. Dollar Disclosure: The author has no positions in any
