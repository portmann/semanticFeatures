Semiconductor ETFs To Watch Should Intel Buy Altera | Seeking Alpha Mar. 31, 2015 7:39 AM ET  |  Includes: ALTR , INTC , PSI , SMH , SOXX , XSD by: Zacks Funds Wall Street is speculating that the world's largest chipmaker Intel Corp. (NASDAQ: INTC ) is in talks to buy fellow chipmaker Altera Corp. (NASDAQ: ALTR ). According to sources, the deal will likely be valued at over $10 billion. The timing and terms of the deal, which might even fall apart, are not known. Intel is seeking new avenues of growth amid the weaker PC sales market, which forced a billion dollar cut in its first quarter sales outlook earlier this month. The market research firm IDC recently widened its expectation for worldwide PC shipments to decline from 3.3% to 4.9% for this year. Further, the rising demand for low-priced chips and new products to power Internet-connected gadgets is driving consolidation in the industry (read: Intel Sales Warning Puts Semiconductor ETFs in Focus ). The acquisition of Altera would open up opportunities for Intel in the programmable computer chip market used in cellular networks, cars, wireless radios and other products. Since about two thirds of Altera's revenues come from the telecom, wireless, and military/aerospace industry, this will help Intel to diversify its revenue stream beyond legacy computer markets. With this, Intel could expand into a number of markets like automotive, industrial and communications while bolstering its presence in data centers as well. The in-focus deal, if announced, would be the largest acquisition in Intel's history and among the biggest in the technology industry in recent years. The latest M&A deals announced in the space are NXP Semiconductors (NASDAQ: NXPI ), which agreed to merge with the smaller peer Freescale Semiconductor (NYSE: FSL ), and Hewlett-Packard (NYSE: HPQ ), which is set to acquire Wi-Fi gear maker Aruba Networks (NASDAQ: ARUN ). Shares of ALTR soared as much as 30% following the news to a four-year high of $45.00. The stock also crushed its average volume figures, as about 14.2 million shares moved hands compared to just 3.1 million on average. Meanwhile, Intel surged as much as 9.7% on elevated volume of more than two times the daily average volume (see: all the Technology ETFs here ). The M&A deal has put the semiconductor corner of the broad technology ETFs in focus over the coming days. Investors seeking to ride out this surging trend in a diversified way could consider the following ETFs. Any of these funds could make for a compelling choice given their Zacks ETF Rank of 1 or 'Strong Buy' rating, suggesting their continued outperformance. Moreover, each of these products gained over 2% on the day of the possible buyout announcement. Market Vectors Semiconductor ETF (NYSEARCA: SMH ) This fund provides exposure to 26 securities by tracking the Market Vectors US Listed Semiconductor 25 Index. Of these firms, Intel takes the top position with 17.4% of assets. From a market cap look, the product focuses on large cap stocks, as these account for more than three fifths of the portfolio. The product manages assets worth $135.3 million and charges 35 bps in annual fees and expenses. It is heavily traded with volume of around 3.4 million shares per day (read: Nasdaq Hits 5,000 on Tech Mergers: Semiconductor ETFs Surge ). iShares PHLX SOX Semiconductor Sector Index ETF (NASDAQ: SOXX ) This ETF follows the PHLX Semiconductor Sector Index and offers exposure to 30 domestic firms. Intel takes the third spot in the basket with nearly 7.65% share. Further, about three-fifths of the portfolio is dominated by large cap stocks while mid caps takes the remainder with just 4% going to small caps. The fund has amassed $546.1 million in its asset base and trades in solid average volume of roughly 296,000 shares a day. The product charges 47 bps in fees a year. PowerShares Dynamic Semiconductors Portfolio ETF (NYSEARCA: PSI ) This fund tracks the Dynamic Semiconductor Intellidex Index, holding 30 securities in the basket with a diversified exposure to various market caps. About 46% goes to small caps, 35% to large caps and the rest to mid caps. Intel occupies the fifth spot with a 4.8% share. The product, with AUM of $78.5 million is often overlooked by investors and hence sees a lower average daily volume of about 43,000 shares. Expense ratio came in at 0.63%. SPDR S&P Semiconductor ETF (NYSEARCA: XSD ) This fund tracks the S&P Semiconductor Select Industry Index, holding 48 stocks in its portfolio. Here, Intel and Altera account for about 2.5% allocation each. The product has a definite tilt toward small cap stocks at 55%, followed by 27% in mid caps and 17% in large caps. The fund is less popular and illiquid with AUM of $174.9 million and average daily volume of about 111,000 shares. It charges 35 bps in fees per year.
