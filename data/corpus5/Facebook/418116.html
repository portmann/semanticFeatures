Who is winning the social media battle for news consumption? - Facebook (NASDAQ:FB) | Seeking Alpha May 29 2016, 01:28 ET | By: SA Eli Hoffmann , SA News Editor 62% of U.S. adults get news on social media, and 18% do so often, according to a new survey by Pew Research Center. In 2012 49% of U.S. adults reported "seeing news" on social media. 66% of Facebook (NASDAQ: FB ) users; 59% of Twitter (NYSE: TWTR ) users; 70% of Reddit users; 31% of Tumblr (NASDAQ: YHOO ) users; and 4% of LinkedIn (NYSE: LNKD ) users say they get news on the site. See more interesting statistics at the source, including intentionality (how many users of each platform went looking for news rather than finding it when searching for something else), and how the change over time maps out across different platforms.
