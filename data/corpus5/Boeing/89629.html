Narrowbody Tuesday On Day 2 Of The Paris Air Show | Seeking Alpha Boeing adds over 200 orders on Day 2 of the Paris Air Show. Aeroflot canceled its order for 22 Dreamliners. Yesterday the Paris Air Show commenced and it was a fruitful day for Boeing (NYSE: BA ) and Airbus ( OTCPK:EADSF / OTCPK:EADSY ). Most noteworthy on Day 1 were the mega orders from Saudia, Garuda Indonesia and Qatar Airways with a combined value of almost $33 billion. Whereas yesterday the airlines were most active, today lessors placed most orders Most orders were for narrowbody aircraft No major roles for COMAC, Embraer and Bombardier today During day 2 of the Parish Air Show 2015 Airbus and Boeing accounted for over 90% of all received orders. The remaining orders went to ATR. Airbus won 37 orders with a value of $5 billion on Day 2 of the air show: Peach ordered 3 Airbus A320ceo aircraft with a value of $300 million Korean Air ordered 31 Airbus A321neo aircraft with a value of $3.7 billion EVA Air ordered 4 Airbus A330-300 aircraft, worth $1 billion After yesterday's mega orders from Garuda Indonesia and Saudia, today was a tranquil but good day for Airbus. The company gathered some orders for their current and upcoming narrowbody aircraft and added an order for the Airbus A330, which means almost a month of production for the Airbus A330. Today was more the day for Boeing to shine. The jet maker won 204 new orders with a catalogue value of $22.1 billion. Lessor AerCap placed an order for 100 Boeing 737 MAX8 aircraft, worth $10.7 billion AVIC International Leasing ordered 30 Boeing 737 MAX8 aircraft, worth $3.2 billion Minsheng Financial Leasing ordered 20 Boeing 737 MAX aircraft and 10 Boeing 737-800 airframes, worth over $3 billion Lessor SMBC ordered 10 Boeing 737 MAX8 aircraft valued at $1 billion Sriwijaya Air ordered 2 Boeing 737-900ER aircraft valued at $200 million After placing an order with Airbus, Korean Air ordered 30 Boeing 737 MAX8 aircraft and 2 Boeing 777-300ERs with a combined value of $3.8 bn Russian Aeroflot cancelled an order for 22 Boeing 787-8 valued at $4.8 bn Boeing had a good day, winning over 200 orders for their current and upcoming Boeing 737, while also adding 2 Boeing 777s to the order book. Aeroflot, which is going through a rough but 'manageable' crisis, canceled its order for the Boeing 787-8 as it no longer suits their needs. Although a significant order loss, it can be expected that customers will be lining up soon to occupy the freed delivery slots. There were no orders for Embraer (NYSE: ERJ ), COMAC and Bombardier ( OTCQX:BDRBF ) today. ATR collected 21 orders for the ATR 72-600 from Cebu Pacific and Braathens Aviation. Figure 2: Ordering parties for Boeing and Airbus (Source: Dhierin-Perkash Bechai) Whereas yesterday 71% of the orders was placed by airlines, today it was exactly the other way around. Korean Air was the biggest ordering airline with a combined order value of $7.6 billion, while lessor AerCap ordered 100 aircraft valued at $10.7 billion. Production rates narrow body aircraft Boeing won over 200 orders for their Boeing 737 on Day 2, while this is a big number the orders only represent about 4 months of the production as Boeing is pushing the production rate beyond 50 per month in 2018. In order to maintain stable order books a book-to-bill ratio of 1 is required. This means that the order inflow matches the number of deliveries. With an expected number of deliveries of over 500 and about 300 orders booked this year, Boeing would need an additional 200 orders to maintain stable order books. In terms of value, Day 1 was the better day. The value per aircraft was $185 million, while on Day 2 the value per aircraft was only $112 million. This also shows that Day 2 was more focused on narrowbody aircraft.
