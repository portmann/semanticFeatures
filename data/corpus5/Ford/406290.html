RSR kingherman - No, it was 63.1% of the Class A stock that rejected the perennial proposal. The shares the Ford family own are separate and distinct from the publicly traded shares. Since Ford went public in 1956, the Ford family has retained 40% voting rights in the form of Class B stock. Edsel Ford established the Ford Foundation in 1936, and when Ford Motor Company went public additional shares of F were given to the foundation, which remains one of the largest foundations in the USA to this day.
