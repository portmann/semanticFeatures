He can tell that there will be an Apple car coming out based on their hiring patterns. They should have embarked upon the project sooner, says Musk. They are on track volume production by 2020. It was a missed opportunity for Apple to have started so late. As for Google ( GOOG / GOOGL ), Musk expects them to license its technology to car companies such as Fiat (NYSE: FCAU ) instead of build cars itself. He said that Google has shown the potential of autonomous transport but they are not a car company. In the years ahead, autonomous cars will become the standard. There are five Chinese-funded electric vehicle startups at over the billion dollar level. Musk likes the activity in this industry. He opened up Tesla's patents in order to help accelerate the industry's development. He wants to get to a million Teslas per year by 2020. There is a lot of room to improve; so far none of the major car companies such as GM (NYSE: GM ) have been able to build a good electric car. Musk also spoke about his rocket competition with Amazon's (NASDAQ: AMZN ) Jeff Bezos. Musk plans to land the first people on Mars in 2025. Oh, and he makes a reasonable argument that we are probably living in a simulation and not base reality. Thoma Bravo is buying Qlik (NASDAQ: QLIK ) for $30.50 in cash. Morgan Stanley (NYSE: MS ) is advising the target and Goldman (NYSE: GS ) is advising the buyer. Ares (NASDAQ: ARCC ) is also helping with the financing. At the request of Sanofi (NYSE: SNY ), Medivation (NASDAQ: MDVN ) set a record date of June 1 for the Consent Solicitation. Medivation is working with JPMorgan (NYSE: JPM ). Alibaba (NYSE: BABA ) announced the terms of its purchase of ordinary shares from SoftBank ( OTCPK:SFTBY ). The company agreed to purchase 27,027,027 shares for $74 per share for an aggregate of $2 billion. Many Tribune Publishing (NYSE: TPUB ) holders plan to withhold votes during today's annual meeting. Gannett (NYSE: GCI ) requested investors withhold their votes in an act of no confidence for the Tribune Publishing board. The Mylan (NASDAQ: MYL ) acquisition of Meda ( OTCPK:MDABY ) was notified to the European Commission on June 1. The Phase 1 review expires on July 6.
