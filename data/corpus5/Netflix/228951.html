Turning to taxes, as I mentioned earlier this year, with a valuation allowance against certain deferred tax assets, the company's combined effective tax rate will be largely equivalent to our cash tax liability and includes amounts payable to the former stockholder. Now our cash liability is a function of many factors, including changes in our expected taxable income for the year, which may be different from reported earnings. Just one example of this that I shared last time was the gain associated with the sale of our campus, which is recognized as taxable income in the year but deferred for reported earnings. To the extent the changes in expected taxable income allow us to benefit from the tax sharing agreement, a portion of that benefit is payable to the former stockholder. As a result of these factors, our reported cash tax liability and therefore our combined effective tax rate from quarter-to-quarter maybe both volatile and look unusual. During the quarter, our provision for income taxes was $2.5 million or an effective tax rate of 20.3%.
