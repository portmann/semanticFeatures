Prior to results being provided to the public after markets close on 8/10/2015 (via the company's quarterly press release), I would like to analyze FSFR's NAV as of 6/30/2015 and provide readers a general direction on how I believe this recent quarter has panned out. Before we begin the company's NAV projection analysis, I wanted to provide readers my current thoughts in regards to FSFR as a possible equity investment. This should help some readers better understand what is currently occurring within FSFR regarding several key metrics and also assist when reading the NAV projection analysis. My current BUY, SELL, or HOLD recommendation for FSFR will be at the bottom of this section of the article. Currently, I believe there are six reasons why FSFR is an attractive equity investment. However, to remain prudent, I also believe there is one "cautionary" factor which would likely "steer" less risk tolerant investors away from currently owning FSFR as a possible equity investment. Let us first discuss the positive factors. First, FSFR trades at an excessively material discount to my projected NAV as of 6/30/2015 (per share figure provided near the end of the article). As of 8/5/2015, FSFR's stock price was $9.10 per share. This calculates to a discount of ($3.28) per share for a price to NAV ratio of 0.7349 (a 26.51% discount). FSFR's discount to NAV has modestly increased over the past several months. I believe most investors should see this excessively material discount to NAV as an attractive opportunity to own FSFR. Second, even though FSFR had an annual forward yield to the company's NAV as of 3/31/2015 of 9.63%., the company had an annual forward yield to its stock price as of 8/5/2015 of 13.19%. I believe this current yield, which is currently in excess of 12.5%, is another positive factor of owning FSFR. Third, FSFR announced several quarters ago it had formed a joint venture ("JV") partnership with members of the Glick Family (GF Funding) to expand the company's investment portfolio. This newly created JV partnership is called "FSFR Glick Family JV LLC" (FSFR Glick JV) and was formed during the fiscal first quarter of 2015. FSFR has continued to state the company expects to generate an annual return in the low teens in regards to this recently formed JV partnership. When fully funded, FSFR Glick JV is expected to generate annual returns approximately 500 basis points ("bps") above FSFR's average return on investment ("ROI"). This is due to the fact management fees are based on the net assets of FSFR Glick JV (not gross assets) which is one strategic advantage. In addition, by utilizing JV partnerships, FSFR can "circumvent" the business development company ("BDC") debt-to-equity leverage ratio threshold of 1.0x while still maintaining the company's Regulated Investment Company ("RIC") status per the Internal Revenue Code ("IRC"). Both parties have committed to provide a combined $100 million of debt and equity to this recently created entity. FSFR will provide $87.5 million and GF Funding will provide the remaining $12.5 million. During the fiscal second quarter of 2015, FSFR was in discussions with several lenders regarding debt financing for FSFR Glick JV. By the end of April 2015, $86.6 million of funding has been provided to invest in a portfolio of senior secured loans. This funding has been provided through equity of $60 million, in accordance with the proportional percentages discussed above, and through FSFR Glick JV's recently secured $200 million revolving credit facility with Credit Suisse AG, Cayman Islands (Credit Suisse). When calculated, management anticipates FSFR Glick JV will have an investment capacity of approximately $300 million. Even though FSFR Glick JV was not fully "ramped up" during the fiscal third quarter of 2015, the benefits of this type of investment structure should begin to have a more material impact towards the latter-half of 2015/early 2016. Management has also stated FSFR will likely have future discussions with other interested third parties to create additional JV partnerships over the next several years. I believe the strategic advantages of these types of JV partnerships should be seen as a positive factor. Fourth, FSFR has recently continued to expand the company's available borrowings. In January 2015, management announced FSFR had closed a $175 million revolving credit facility with Citibank, N.A. (Citi). The Citi Senior Secured Revolving Credit Facility is priced at LIBOR plus 2% - 2.25%. I believe this new revolving credit facility is attractively priced and will allow FSFR to continue to expand the company's investment portfolio over the foreseeable future. Furthermore, in June 2015 management announced FSFR had refinanced the company's previously amended $200 million Natixis/Fifth Third Bank Revolving Credit Facility/Term Loans by closing a $309 million "debt securitization" transaction where FSFR issued $223 million of long-term notes which are secured by the company's underlying portfolio investments. These long-term notes are termed the "2015 Notes" and have five different "classes". The weighted average interest rate associated with the five classes of 2015 Notes is LIBOR plus 2.01% which is basically unchanged when compared to the weighted average of the existing $200 million Natixis/Fifth Third Bank Revolving Credit Facility/Term Loans. Another important note is the debt securitization has a four-year reinvestment period, a two-year non-call period, and a ten-year maturity. As such, FSFR has extended the maturity of a material portion of the company's borrowings at very attractive debt financing rates. Even though an increase in LIBOR will cause an increase to FSFR's cost of funds rate, the company has nearly 100% of its investment portfolio is senior secured, floating-rate debt investments. As such, any increase in interest expense will directly be met by an increase in interest income. In all likelihood, net interest spreads will increase as the "base rate" on all new lower middle market ("LMM") and middle market ("MM") loans will be at a higher stated rate in a rising interest rate environment. Fifth, management has continued to reiterate FSFR does not anticipate issuing shares of common stock below CURRENT NAV for the foreseeable future. Even though I have stated I was against the August 2014 equity offering (due to the fact it was materially dilutive to existing shareholders; at the time I did not own the stock), I am comforted management will not perform any further dilutive actions for the foreseeable future. I believe this notion has been solidified from the recent expansion of FSFR's available debt financing which was discussed earlier. As such, I believe FSFR not performing any dilutive equity offerings for the foreseeable future is another positive factor that should be reflected in valuations. Finally, the material decrease in the price of oil during FSFR's fiscal first quarter of 2015 (and most recently during July 2015) has caused "distress"/speculation to surround most of the oil and gas sector (including parts of the energy sector) regarding falling margins and potential struggling/bankrupt entities. While I would agree markets should have some sort of generalized "cause for concern" within the high-yield debt market, I also believe one needs to "drill down" to each BDC's debt and equity investments to see which portfolio companies could be affected by this event. As of 12/31/2014, only 0.775% of FSFR's portfolio had debt and equity investments within the oil and gas sector (including certain investments in the energy sector which had "oil and gas" characteristics). As of 3/31/2015, only 0.771% of FSFR's portfolio had debt and equity investments within the oil and gas sector. When compared to the ten other BDC peers I currently cover, this was one of the lowest percentages at potential risk for investment depreciation due to falling crude oil prices. As of 3/31/2015, I calculated Apollo Investment Corp. (NASDAQ: AINV ), Main Street Capital Corp. (NYSE: MAIN ), Medley Capital Corp. (NYSE: MCC ), and Prospect Capital Corp. (NASDAQ: PSEC ) had a 15.29%, 5.88%, 6.41%, and 4.74% exposure to the oil and gas sector, respectively (again, including certain investments in the energy sector which had "oil and gas" characteristics). I believe FSFR's continued extremely low exposure to the oil and gas sector (percentage wise) should be seen as a positive sign. Now let us discuss one "cautionary" factor. The one "cautionary" factor which I believe should be discussed is FSFR's dividend. Currently, FSFR has declared monthly dividends of $0.10 per share through August 2015. For the fiscal first quarter of 2015 (quarter ending 12/31/2014), FSFR reported NII of $0.35 per share. With that being said, for the fiscal second quarter of 2015 (quarter ending 3/31/2015), FSFR reported NII of only $0.23 per share. FSFR's net investment company taxable income ("ICTI") for these two quarters was fairly similar to the company's NII. As will be discussed in the analysis below, I am projecting FSFR will report NII of $0.27 per share for the fiscal third quarter of 2015 (quarter ending 6/30/2015). While this would be a nice "bounce back" from the prior quarter, this NII projection would still be slightly - modestly below FSFR's current quarterly dividend distributions rate of $0.30 per share. Depending on one's risk tolerance and time horizon, more cautious investors may want to wait until FSFR declares the next set of monthly dividends before initiating a position in this stock. With that being said, I believe a portion of FSFR's excessively material discount to NAV has already built in the possible minor - modest dividend reduction which could occur as early as September 2015. With that being said, this "potential future event" could still cause some modest short-term volatility. Even with a possible minor - modest dividend reduction, I believe FSFR is an attractive long-term equity investment. My BUY, SELL, or HOLD Recommendation: From the analysis provided above, including additional factors not analyzed within this article, I currently rate FSFR as a HOLD when I believe the company's stock price is trading at a material (at or greater than 10% but less than 15%) discount to NAV as of 6/30/2015 and a BUY when trading at an excessively material (at or greater than 15%) discount to NAV as of 6/30/2015. As such, I currently rate FSFR as a BUY. It should be noted this recommendation is based on a "long-term" time horizon (an outlook of at least one year). For "short-term" investors (an outlook of under one year), I anticipate continued volatility in markets due to speculation surrounding the timing of the Federal Open Market Committee's ("FOMC") initial increase to the Fed Funds Rate and recent global macroeconomic events. Each investor's BUY, SELL, or HOLD decision is based on one's risk tolerance, time horizon, and dividend income goals. My personal recommendation will not fit each investor's current investing strategy.
