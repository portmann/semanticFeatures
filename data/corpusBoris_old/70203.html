Economic Growth And The British Pound | Seeking Alpha Economic activity in the U.K. is rising. Moreover, both consumer spending and sentiment are strengthening. The British pound could benefit as rising consumer spending is aiding overall economic activity, pushing government bond yields higher. The U.K. currency is represented by CurrencyShares British Pound Sterling Trust (NYSEARCA: FXB ). In The first quarter, the economic growth figure came in at annual pace of 2.4%, below the previous quarter's reading of 3.0%, while also missing estimates for 2.5%. Economic growth, however, is currently residing near multi-year highs, seen below. Economic growth in the U.K. has been among the strongest in the developed world the past few years. "The U.K. economy had a bumper 2014 and grew faster than any other in the Group of Seven club of advanced nations. However, a slowdown in activity in recent months led analysts to worry about the powerful headwinds faced by Britain--such as disappointing productivity growth, weak exports and an over-reliance on the services sector," according to MarketWatch. Moreover, much of the strength has been due to stronger consumer spending. In April, the retail sales figure came in at an annual pace of 4.7%, above the previous month's revised reading of 4.0%, while also significantly exceeding estimates for 3.8%. After bottoming in 2009 at -4.0% annual contraction, consumer spending rebounded to current levels, seen below. Low price pressures continue to provide an incentive for more shoppers to spend money. "Low inflation, which we expect to remain below 1 percent for the rest of the year, has given household incomes a much-needed boost and greater spending power. Overall the outlook is bright for firms on the high street, but challenges still remain, especially for food retailers, who are still feeling the heat of stiff price competition from new entrants to the sector," according to Reuters. Finally, as the economy improves, government bond yields in the U.K. have risen. Government 10-year bond yields in the U.K. rallied 50 basis points, from 1.5% to 2.0% over the last month as investors became more bullish on its economy. Economic activity in the region is putting together a strong recovery. As greater consumer spending leads the economy higher, investors are opting to increase the bid for the 10-year U.K. government bond yield. As interest rates rise, more money should rush into the pound, pushing the currency higher in coming months.
