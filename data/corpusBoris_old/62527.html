U.S. House passes transportation spending extension | Reuters WASHINGTON May 19 The U.S. House of Representatives overwhelmingly passed a two-month extension of spending authority for federally funded transportation projects, delaying a decision on longer-term funding until the Highway Trust Fund is depleted in July. The bipartisan 387-35 vote sends the measure to the U.S. Senate for approval. Federal spending authority on road, bridge and rail transit projects is due to expire on May 31.
