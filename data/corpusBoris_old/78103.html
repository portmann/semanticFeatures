The results of the March 31 quarter which is also our fiscal 2015 year-end demonstrates IXYS transformation into a broad-range semiconductor company both organically and through acquisitions. We are seeing strong growth in our IC business as it complements our power semiconductor market penetrations, especially in the consumer market. We have also witnessed the expansion of our sales into Asia. I would like to draw your attention to a few highlights. For the quarter, revenues for the quarter increased sequentially from the December 2014 quarter. Net income for the quarter was $7.8 million, an increase of $7.7 million or 5806% as compared to net income for the same period in the prior fiscal year. Earnings per diluted share in the quarter were $0.24 as compared to $0.0 in the same period last fiscal year. Gross profit margin increased to 30.5%. IXYS declared a $0.035 per share dividend on February 6, 2015. And now for the year. Net revenues were $338.8 million, an increase of $2.4 million from the prior fiscal year. Net income was $23.7 million, an increase of $17.7 million or 292.7% compared to fiscal 2014. Earnings per diluted share were $0.74 for the fiscal year, $0.55 more than the prior fiscal year. Gross profit was $102 million or 30.1% of net revenues as compared to gross profit of $100.2 million or 29.8% of net revenues for the prior fiscal year. Now I will turn to revenue data by regions, market segments and product group. As a percentage of total revenues for the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2015, North America represented 25.6%, Europe and Middle East 27%, and Asia and the rest of the world 47.4%. As a percentage of total revenues for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2015, North America represented 25.7%, Europe and Middle East 29.6%, and Asia and the rest of the world 44.7%. Our revenues by market segment for the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2015 were as follows. Industrial and commercial including renewable energy 44%, communication infrastructure 13%, medical electronics 8%, consumer 17%, transportation including auto and traction 4%, and other 14%. Our revenues by market segment for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2015 were as follows. Industrial and commercial 45%, communication infrastructure 13%, medical electronics 9%, consumer 16%, transportation 4%, and others 13%. Our revenues based on product groups for the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2015 were 64.6% for power semiconductors, 29.7% for integrated circuits which includes microcontrollers, and 5.7% for systems and RF. Our revenue based on product group for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2015 were 64.8% for power semiconductors, 28.2% for integrated circuits, and 7% for system and RF. We are executing on our growth initiatives and diversify into more value-added ICs and power semiconductors in our focused markets. We continue to generate cash which we use to invest in new products and technology. Cash generated has also been shared with our shareholders as dividends. We have a global presence especially in Asia which allows us to leverage the world of IXYS marketing campaign in high-growth markets. We've allocated some of our manufacturing base to lower-cost Asian subcontractors thereby allowing us to produce more products at reduced costs and improved time-to-market performance. I will now turn the call over to Uzi Sasson, President and CFO, who will discuss the financials in more detail. Thanks Nathan. GAAP net income for the quarter ended March 31, 2015 was $7.8 million or $0.24 per diluted share, an increase of $7.7 million from the March 31, 2014 quarter, which show the net income of $132,000, or $0.00 per diluted share. The March 2015 quarter non-GAAP net income, which excludes the impact of charges for amortization of acquired intangible assets and stock compensation, was $9.9 million, or $0.30 per diluted share. This was an increase of $6.3 million, as compared to the non-GAAP net income of $3.6 million, or $0.11 per diluted share, for the same period in the prior fiscal year. GAAP net income for the twelve months ended March 31, 2015 was $23.7 million, or $0.74 per diluted share, as compared to net income of $6 million, or $0.19 per diluted share, for the same period in the prior fiscal year. For the twelve months ended March 31, 2015, non-GAAP net income was $32 million, or $1 per diluted share. This compared favorably to non-GAAP net income of $17.8 million, or $0.56 per diluted share, for the same period in the prior fiscal year. Gross profit for the quarter ended March 31, 2015 was $25.3 million, or 30.5% of net revenues, as compared to the gross profit of $25.4 million, or 28.2% of net revenues, for the same quarter in the prior fiscal year. Our gross profit increased by 2.3 percentage points this quarter over the comparable period in the fiscal year 2014. Gross profit for the twelve months ended March 31, 2015 was $102 million, or 30.1% of net revenues, as compared to the gross profit of $100.2 million, or 29.8% of net revenues, for the same period in the prior fiscal year. R&D spending for the quarter was $6.2 million or 7.5% of net sales as compared to $7.9 million or 8.7% of net sales in the prior year quarter. For the year, R&D spending was $26.7 million as compared to $30.9 million in the prior fiscal year. We expect to continue to spend in the range of $25 million to $30 million a year on R&D. We have become even more selective in our projects and developments as we work to meet the needs of current power semiconductor customers. We are also working to address the growing demand for the MCUs and Internet of things technologies. SG&A expenses were $10.1 million or 12.2% of net sales as compared to $11.3 million or 12.6% of net sales in the prior year quarter. We are pleased by the efforts to decrease SG&A expenses as IXYS is committed to tight cost controls which can potentially lead to higher profitability. CapEx was approximately $1.7 million for the March quarter. Capital expenditures include purchases of manufacturing equipment. Turning to the balance sheet, the ratio of current assets to current liabilities was 3.2. The Company exited the March 31, 2015 quarter with cash and cash equivalents totaling approximately $121.2 million. We generated $16.1 million in cash from operations during the quarter. Adjusted EBITDA which excludes stock based compensation expense was $14 million for the quarter and $52 million for the year ended March 31, 2015. In the quarter ended March 31, 2015, the Company declared $0.035 per share dividend, highlighting the third consecutive quarter of dividends. We returned about $4.3 million to stockholders in fiscal 2015 through dividends. Net accounts receivables at March 31, 2015 were $41 million and DSO was approximately 45 days. As of March 31, 2015, net inventory was $82 million and inventory turns were 2.8x during the quarter. We are pleased that our production efficiencies, low-cost outsourcing and Lean Six Sigma initiatives have resulted in streamlined manufacturing, better logistics and timely delivery to customers. Although we are seeing modest growth in the U.S. and Asia, customers are still reluctant to stock inventory. Rather, customers are purchasing products with short lead times. Our manufacturing efficiencies have allowed us to capture this business. We are equally pleased with the sales efforts that resulted in strong adoption of products across almost all lines with notable growth in the integrated circuit microcontroller businesses. Our core power business in industrial and commercial applications continues to drive our Company forward. While we are upbeat about the future prospects for IXYS, we must be cautious about the near-term headwinds related to global macroeconomic conditions. To that end, we expect revenues in the June 2015 quarter to be in line with revenues of the March 2015 quarter. Can you talk specifically about some steps you're taking to improve your gross margins, and specifically related to capacity, as you're moving some of your products to Asia, do you guys need to reduce capacity internally, how do you think about that balance as you move more and more products to Asia? This is Nathan, I'll answer that. That is a key strategic core action that we take in the Company. Some of our products are very high voltage. There are no foundries around the world that can produce it for us and it contains a very high value for our proprietary know-how. So this kind of technology we like to keep in-house. What we are doing, and again Uzi and the team did a fabulous job in optimizing the wafer fabs that we have, the main silicon wafer fabs that we have, one in Massachusetts, Beverly Maas., one in Germany and one in the U.K., where we keep the proprieted product in-house and utilize the capacity in Massachusetts even to support classical products that we used to make in the facility in Germany – mainly in Germany. What results on that is that we use minimal CapEx to produce advanced technology products that otherwise we should have paid a lot of money in upgrading the fab in Germany for that matter. So we are glad that we have this internal capacity, gives us flexibility. Nonetheless, products or part of the process that is non-proprietary, we are starting to fab out to foundries and this is an ongoing process, and this by itself will reduce our manufacturing cost and improve the gross margin. So for that matter we are very satisfied that we have a solid plan and you'll see the results that as revenues grows with the higher value added product, we are relying less on internal fabs which by sense improve the margins. No, we have – as Uzi said, we also initiated that we have a base power business that it's still there, and that's the comment that Uzi said on the macroeconomics, the industrial market which is mainly affected by infrastructure investment in Asia slowed down a little bit as we know in the last three years, once it will come back our fabs will be utilized to the [till] [ph] and still we see growth in the utilization of our internal fab.
