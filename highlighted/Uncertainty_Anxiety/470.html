doing well in Europe.He also sees negatives for many stocks, but overall the picture looks good.CEO interview - Clean Harbors (NYSE: CLH ) Clean Harbor had a tough year in 2014, but is roaring recently.The management announced a transformation one month ago by spinning off its oil and gas service business along with its energy focus business as a separate public entity.The company also reported solid numbers by posting higher than expected revenues.To find out more about the prospects of the company, Cramer interviewed CEO Alan McKim.McKim said that the U.S. is doing much better now and mentioned that the company is gaining market share recently.McKim is hopeful there will be more investments in the pipeline infrastructure so oil can be carried in a safer way.He said that as the price of crude is coming down, the value of hazardous waste goes down, as a result of which they have to charge customers to maintain adequate spread.He added that the company has an advantage as the Safety-Kleen refineries recycle the waste into a green product which is sold back in the market.Commenting on the spin-off of the oil and gas side of the business, McKim said that he sees a very strong demand for the segment in the future and the spin-off entity will have a strong balance sheet to survive through the gloomy oil period.Cramer has been waiting for the crash in oil price to bring the economy to its knees but it hasn't happened."It is only a matter of time before the over-levered, eyes-too-big-for-their-bank-account oil men go belly up, taking tens of thousands of jobs off the table with their recklessness and rendering boom cities into ghost towns," said Cramer.He added that the ripple effects of low oil prices are not completely visible on the U.S, economy.While some oil companies have reported disappointing numbers, many are also doing well.Companies have cut their spending as they are making less money, but most of them still have borrowing capacity.Although the oil price continues to remain low, 2016 may not be a great year, but so far, many companies have high-grade properties to see them through 2015.Cramer said he hasn't seen much
