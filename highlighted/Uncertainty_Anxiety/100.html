Has Hedging The Indian Rupee Helped U.S. Investors?| Seeking Alpha The Indian rupee has appreciated against the major global currencies in 2015.Volatility of INR has significantly decreased, but forward premium remains high.The Indian rupee (NYSEARCA: INR ) has appreciated against major global currencies this year, including the USD despite wide dollar strength.The strength was mainly due to two factors - (1) lower oil prices that led to less demand for the USD, and (2) good performance of local stock markets.The rupee found its new range at around 61-63 levels in recent times.The Reserve Bank's steps to arrest the rapid fall in the rupee - raising interest rates, restrictions on gold imports etc. - had paid off in the latter half of 2013.US investors in Indian markets would have been very worried about the volatility of the rupee, especially in the latter half of 2013, when the daily change in INR reached ~2-3%.The daily change in forex rates is given below.Despite worries from high volatility, high forward premiums could be preventing investors from hedging their exposures.The high premiums are due to differences in interest rates and inflation rates between India and the US, and high volatility of INR (arising from vulnerability to fund outflows).During 'taper tantrum' times, the premiums had jumped close to 9% per annum.This has fallen to ~7.5% recently, after the rupee stabilized.The above chart compares the contracted rates of the 12-month forward contract, against the spot exchange rates in the month of contract expiry.The chart shows that, on average, forward rates were equal to the spot rates in the month of expiry.But this averaging masks the significant underlying volatility.Hedged investors were at a disadvantage during the latter half of 2008, when the rupee appreciated from ~45/USD to ~40/USD.On the other side, they were at a significant advantage during the global financial crisis, when the INR depreciated from ~40/USD to ~50/USD in 2008-09.Investors who kept their INR positions open benefited ~15% from the appreciation in the rupee in the latter half of 2007 while they suffered significant losses (~20%) during the global financial crisis - exactly the opposite of what happened to hedged investors.In the above chart, we can see that, on average, hedged investors were on par with unhedged investors.But still, the above chart shows significant volatility with a standard deviation of ~16%.Hence, it is very tough to make any concrete conclusion from the above comparison.Our year-end target for INR is 65/USD.Currently, the rupee trades at around 62/USD - roughly 4.5% higher than our year-end target.We believe that, for US investors (with a 12-month time horizon), at the current forward premium of ~7.5% (annualised), it is safe to keep INR positions unhedged.Disclaimer: The information set forth herein has been obtained or derived from sources generally available to the public and believed by the author to be reliable, but the author does not make any representation or warranty, express or implied, as to its accuracy or completeness.The information is not intended to be used as the basis of any investment decisions by any person or entity.This information does not constitute investment advice, nor is it an offer or a solicitation of an offer to buy or sell any security.Source: Has Hedging The Indian Rupee Helped U.S. Investors?Disclosure: The author has no positions in any stocks mentioned, and no plans to initiate any positions within the next 72 hours.(More...)The author wrote this article themselves, and it expresses their own opinions.The author is not receiving compensation for it (other than from Seeking Alpha).The author has no business relationship with any company whose stock is mentioned in this article.We only use your contact details to reply to your request for more information.We do not sell the personal contact data you submit to anyone else.Thank you for your interest in Seeking Alpha PRO We look forward to contacting you shortly for a conversation.Our PRO subscription service was created for fund managers, and the cost of the product is prohibitive for most individual investors.PRO Alerts is our flagship product for individual investors who want to be faster and smarter about their stocks.To learn more about it, click here.If you are an investment professional with over $1M AUM and received this message in error, click here and you will be contacted shortly.We look forward to contacting you when we have an individual investor product ready!
