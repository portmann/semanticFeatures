About: ALPS Sector Dividend Dogs ETF (SDOG) , Includes: VOO by: Tom Vaughan The ALPS Sector Dividend Dogs ETF (NYSEARCA: SDOG ) follows the Dogs of the Dow Theory applied to the S&P 500 Index.SDOG tracks an equal-weighted index of the top-five yielding stocks from each of the 10 market sectors in the S&P 500.This sector spread creates a form of diversification that is sometimes missing in high-dividend ETF funds.SDOG has over $500 million in assets, making closure less likely.Also, SDOG has a 0.40% expense ratio, which is high for a large-cap U.S. stock ETF, but still below the overall average ETF expense ratio of 0.61%.Should you buy SDOG or simply buy a much cheaper S&P 500 Index ETF such as the Vanguard S&P 500 ETF (NYSEARCA: VOO ), which currently has a low expense ratio of 0.05%?Here is a comparison of these two ETF funds: SDOG has paid out 3.41% in the past 12 months versus 1.81% for VOO.If you are looking for tax-advantaged dividend income, you have to score one for SDOG.All of the following analysis of SDOG is hampered by a very short existence.SDOG was launched in June of 2012.Short-term analysis can often give the wrong impression, because there have not been enough market cycles for adequate testing, but such is the state of the fairly new ETF marketplace.Here is a performance chart comparing SDOG to VOO.As you can see, SDOG has outperformed VOO by 1.23% per year on an annualized basis.Score another one for SDOG.During this short time frame, how do these two ETFs compare in terms of risk?This chart shows the risk return profile for SDOG versus VOO.You can see that SDOG has a higher return and a higher risk, as measured by standard deviation.This means that you are getting paid more for your risk, which does not always happen.VOO has a lower expense ratio, but SDOG has a higher dividend, more return at a slightly higher risk during its short history.If I had a time machine, I would go back and buy SDOG versus VOO.Unfortunately, I need to look forward.Would I buy it today?Not yet.I have this dividend ETF on our watch list, because I like the overall concept, but more time needs to elapse before I would risk a purchase of this ETF.My minimum is a 5-year track record.I have been able to create great dividend ETF portfolios with ETFs that have been around for five years or more.It has not been necessary to buy ETFs with shorter track records.There are still too many questions to be answered by SDOG.How will it perform over more market cycles?So far this year, it is down more than VOO.How will it perform in tax efficiency?SDOG is rebalanced quarterly and reconstituted on an annual basis, which creates the possibility of higher taxable payouts.Be patient and keep SDOG on your watch list.It may be a great purchase in a few years.Source: High Dividend ETF Funds: ALPS Sector Dividend Dogs ETF Analyzed Conduct in-depth research on SDOG and 1,600+ other ETFs with SA's ETF Hub ?
