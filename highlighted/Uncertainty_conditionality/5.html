Diversifying Into International Markets - WisdomTree Emerging Markets Equity Income ETF (NYSEARCA:DEM) | Seeking Alpha Financial advisors, fund managers, and media pundits espouse this as a financial doctrine.But are you truly diversified?True diversification means spreading risk among assets, as well as governmental and geographical borders.Not only does investing more globally protect against downside risk to any individual market, but it also provides opportunities for growth otherwise overlooked.The following chart provides a breakdown of global GDP for the past decade, as well as projections for the year 2025, as estimated by The Conference Board.Distribution of World GDP in 2000, 2012 and 2025 Note: GDP shares are converted to U.S. dollars using purchasing power parities.Source: The Conference Board Global Economic Outlook 2014, November 2013 With 81% of the world's economic output created outside of the U.S., opportunities abound.As the world moves toward a more globally integrated market, individual economies are moving more closely in tandem during unusual events (i.e. 2008 Financial Crisis).However, recovery rates and the severity of country-specific recessions can vary widely.By considering foreign markets, you can mitigate against localized risk while also opening an array of new opportunities for growth.ETFs can provide a simple method for diversifying a portfolio internationally.Since ETFs trade just like common stocks on stock exchanges, they often provide the liquidity necessary to make this option less risky than investing in the individual companies that make up the ETF itself.Another added benefit is that like any other common stock, you can make these trades through an online broker in the U.S., which helps avoid high transaction fees and the trouble of opening up a foreign brokerage account.One to consider is the WisdomTree Emerging Markets Equity Income ETF (NYSEARCA: DEM ).This investment is designed to track both the performance and the yield of WisdomTree's Emerging Markets Equity Income Index.This ETF is a fundamentally weighted index and is made up of the highest dividend-yielding stocks from the WisdomTree Emerging Markets Dividend Index.Currently trading at a multi-year low and with a yield around 5.25%, the ETF offers both an attractive level of income, along with the potential for outsized capital gains While the U.S. may be on the verge of raising interest rates , the rest of the world is in the process of lowering rates, in the case of China and India, and even imposing negative interest rates in countries like Sweden, Denmark, and Switzerland.In an investment world where you're being offered little interest on your money, a 5%+ yield is very attractive, and with a P/E value of 8, the fund could double from here before it reaches a valuation anywhere near the S&P 500.Additionally, the expectation for higher interest rates in the U.S. has pushed the value of the U.S. dollar to new highs against most foreign currencies, especially emerging market currencies.This strong dollar benefits these market economies by making goods produced in those countries more affordable to U.S. consumers and importers.As with any investment, there are risks involved, and those risks are higher with this type investment.Some of the DEM's largest holdings are in Russian companies; Gazprom ( OTCQX:GZPFY ), Rosneft ( OTC:RNFTF ), and Lukoil ( OTC:LUKFY ).While there are obvious political risks affecting these companies, they are also some of the most fundamentally undervalued companies in the world currently.The turmoil between Russia and Ukraine and the resulting sanctions against Russia have been ongoing for nearly a year now and priced into the underlying company stock prices.If things don't escalate further, these companies can soar.It's also important to remember that while these companies are some of the fund's larger holdings, there is still the diversification of being invested in companies in other countries, Chinese stocks making up the second largest holding.The current risk/reward ratio looks good to me here.You can always limit downside exposure with a stop loss plan in mind and have the potential for huge upside in a relatively short period of time.And if the investment just holds steady at the current lows, you collect a dividend offering, an unusually high yield in today's investments environment.Source: Diversifying Into International Markets Disclosure: The author has no positions in any stocks mentioned, but may initiate a
