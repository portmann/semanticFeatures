Why RWE Remains Uninspiring - RWE AG ADR (OTCMKTS:RWEOY) | Seeking Alpha RWE is very exposed to negative commodities and now loss making on half of its conventional power plant.Structural over-supply caps power prices going forward.Earnings are still declining, and guidance remains above consensus.Management looks to return to growth but lacks financial flexibility to commit large amounts of growth capex.RWE has published in line results: revenues Eur 48bn (USD 52bn), vs. consensus of Eur 49.8bn (USD 53.5bn), Ebit Eur 4bn (USD 4.3bn), vs. consensus of Eur 4.1bn (USD 4.4bn), adjusted net income Eur 1.28bn (USD 1.38bn), vs. consensus Eur 1.28bn (USD 1.38bn).The big write-offs on power plant are through now, and the speed of downgrades is declining.The company has achieved a positive cash balance ahead of target and stepped up the efficiency program.But, earnings are still in decline and I see pressure on consensus: 2015 guidance is for recurrent net income of Eur 1.1-1.3bn (USD 1.2-1.4bn), the mid point being about 6% below consensus of Eur 1.28bn (USD 1.38bn).Management has confirmed that only about 25-35% of plant delivers an operating result in excess of the company's WACC and 55-65% is cash positive.At current power prices, RWE's generation park is just about cash flow neutral.Power markets have taken another hit with commodities, and even in a scenario commodities recovery, structural oversupply will cap power prices.Net debt still stands at 4.4x Ebitda.The company now longer holds its previous target of 3x net debt/Ebitda.Management asserts it wants to manage its debt differently, because of the changed structure of the liabilities post the sale of DEA.The yield is no longer at a premium to the sector, and it is compromised by the high debt levels.And, sustained yield requires earnings growth.Management has said it is looking to return to growth.But, capex is now restrained close to maintenance levels at c Eur 2bn pa.In total, RWE will spend c Eur 1.5-2bn (USD 1.6-2.1bn) on renenewables growth and the grid businesses.But that will not be enough to compensate for the decline in the conventional business, according to my estimate.The shares trade on a P/E 2015E of 11.4x, still above the sector average of 16x.But that reflects the earnings decline.RWE has underperformed the European utilities sector by 10.1% ytd.I do not see any catalyst for a change in relative performance.Other names in the sector with more positive primary upstream positions gearing may see greater benefit from a commodities recovery.When the cycle turns, growth capex flexibility will be key.Editor's Note: This article discusses one or more securities that do not trade on a major U.S. exchange.Please be aware of the risks associated with these
