How Healthy Is The Japanese Economy?| Seeking Alpha Japanese monetary policy has stabilized in recent months as the economy emerged from recession.Although economic growth remains weak, employment and consumer sentiment are improving.The yen could begin to advance against its developed market peers as investors begin pricing in less additional stimulus in the future.The Japanese economy is improving as economic activity and the labor market strengthen, leading to unchanged policy, benefiting the yen.CurrencyShares Japanese Yen Trust (NYSEARCA: FXY ) is down over 14% since July, shown below.As the Japanese economy accelerated higher, monetary policy has remained steady.In February, policymakers at the Bank of Japan voted to keep its benchmark rate at zero, while staying committed to buying government bonds in an attempt to boost its monetary base to an annual pace of 80 trillion yen, according to Trading Economics.In the bank's minutes, policymakers sounded optimistic about the future, with economic activity rebounding in coming months."Japan's economy has continued its moderate recovery as a trend.Overseas economies -- mainly advanced economies -- have been recovering, albeit with a lackluster performance still seen in part.In this situation, exports have shown signs of picking up.Business fixed investment has been on a moderate increasing trend as corporate profits have improved.Public investment has more or less leveled off at a high level.Private consumption has remained resilient against the backdrop of steady improvement in employment and income situation, although recovery in some areas has been sluggish," according to Trading Economics.Additionally, economic growth has picked up in the region in recent months.In the fourth quarter, the economic growth figure came in at an annual pace of 2.2%, above the previous quarter's revised reading of -2.3%, but missing estimates for 3.7%.Economic growth has proven sporadic over the last few years as an uneven global recovery, and changes in domestic policy, have weighed on exports and spending.Analysts also noted that they are optimistic about the country's growth prospects ."The recovery is still weak, particularly the domestic components.There was almost no growth in capital expenditure and private consumption also remains weak.However, the numbers are within the expectation - we still expect the economy to grow by 1-2% this year," Harumi Taguchi, IHS Global Insight principal economist, told CNBC.Moreover, employment in the country has drastically improved the last few years, potentially aiding consumer spending.In December, the employment to population figure grew by an annual pace of 0.82%, slightly below the previous month's reading of 0.89%.Over the last five years, however, the level of employment has shown strong upward acceleration.With economic activity and the labor market both showing signs of strength, the economic growth figure could outperform in coming months.This should benefit the yen as many investors will price in less monetary policy support in the future.Source: How Healthy Is The Japanese Economy?Disclosure: The author has no positions in any stocks
