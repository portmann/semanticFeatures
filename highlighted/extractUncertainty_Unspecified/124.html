Jaguar February U.S. Sales: Strong Execution Playing Out - Tata Motors Limited (NYSE:TTM) | Seeking Alpha JLR reported strong February US sales growth of +13.8%.Jaguar Land Rover (NYSE: TTM ) reported February US retail numbers that showed +13.8% growth in sales.Despite the supply constraint on key models and the low volume due to the change in the LR2 models, sales remain robust.More importantly, inventory remains even with declining incentives, suggesting JLR's pricing power and premium positioning.Additionally, model mix is improving, which allowed ASP to see a +8% increase in ASP.Rather than experiencing an inventory overstock, JLR's key challenge becomes the low stock level with average wait time being about six months.Nonetheless, I see this as a solid execution with JLR's prior strategy of diverting units to the more profitable market in Asia while avoiding price war in the US.Evidently, this strategy is paying off.I remain bullish on TTM.The company remains my top pick among Indian auto OEMs given its JLR asset.Sprint like a Jaguar.JLR's US sales increased 13.8% to 6.3k, driven by a +23% increase in Land Rover while Jaguar sales declined by -9%.Within the LR segment, Range Rover and RR Sport remain the top two selling brands, with RR Sport sales up +25% while RR Evoque saw +19% increase in sales.Most impressive, RR and LR4 increased sales by 56% and 59% respectively.The LR2 model sales declined by 97%, but that is due to the new model change, a non-event in my view.On the other hand, Jaguar sales declined materially across the brands due to lack of demand.Executing on the right strategy.JLR has historically avoided competing head-on against the other luxury brands in the US by diverting the models to Asia, where it can command a higher ASP while cutting back on incentive offerings in the US.Despite losing some sales in the earlier months, this strategy is evidently paying off.Average LR incentive stands at roughly $480, the lowest among the premium segments, while RR and RR Sport have zero incentives.Despite the low incentives, inventory remains low, highlighting the 1) continued demand for the RR models and 2) pricing power of JLR.Now the challenge for JLR is that average wait time for the RR models is six months.I continue to believe that JLR is pursuing the right strategy in protecting its pricing and margins in the US.TTM now trades at 9x forward P/E and remains attractive given its JLR asset.Source: Jaguar February U.S. Sales: Strong Execution Playing Out Disclosure: The author has no positions in any stocks mentioned,
