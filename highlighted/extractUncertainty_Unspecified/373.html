Swiss Re Is A Solid Blue Chip At A Good Price - Swiss Re Ltd ADR (OTCMKTS:SSREY) | Seeking Alpha Swiss Re (OTCPK: SSREY ) is a solid blue chip reinsurer that might be a nice fit in your portfolio.The stock yields 4.7% and is AA- rated by Standard & Poors.The stock trades at a price to earnings ratio of 9.7 and has a return on equity of 10.5%.Not only does the stock yield 4.7% but it also had a special dividend of CHF 3 ($2.97) and a share buyback program of CHF 1 billion ($.99 billion).Since 1994, the dividend has grown at an annual rate of 10.42%.The stock also trades at a book value of .9, which is pretty inexpensive.These numbers are from the 2014 year end report .Earned premiums grew from CHF 14.5 ($14.35) billion in 2013 to CHF 15.6 ($15.44) billion in 2014.Earnings shrank from CHF 4.444 ($4.4) billion to CHF 3.5 ($3.564) billion due to a restructuring in the life business.The combined ratio for 2014 was a whopping 83.7%.That means for the company kept 16.3 cents for every 83.7 it paid out in claims.That's remarkable.The investment portfolio was $125.4 billion at the end of 2014.55% of this portfolio is cash, short term bonds, and government bonds.It looks like a rise in interest rates will not decrease this portfolio by too much.It receives 40% of its revenues from the Americas, 39% from EMEA (Europe, Middle East, Australia) and 21% from Asia.The company states that the rise of the middle class in Asia will drive home and motor insurance.Low interest rates and tight regulations are keeping reinsurance rates high.Low inflation, reserve releases, and high industry capitalization are helping keep rates low.It will be interesting to see if one of these five changes, especially interest rates.In the past few years, reinsurers have seen a small rise in competition from catastrophe bonds.The bonds work like this: a home builder offers a series of bonds as a private placement that reinsures a property development in Florida against a hurricane.Should the hurricane happen and it costs over say $50 million, the bond holders will be wiped out.In the meantime, the bond holders will receive a nice coupon for the risk that they are taking.Swiss Re points out in a presentation that the spreads of cat bonds have tightened as volume of issuance has increased."Some of this AC (alternative capital) is here to stay, but is unlikely to replace the traditional nat cat reinsurance market.Clients rely on traditional reinsurers for long-term support and services.AC has not been tested by rising credit spreads or large losses."The rise in the Swiss franc could hurt earnings.According to the Wall Street Journal , "The Swiss National Bank 's surprising decision last month to let the franc sharply gain in value has led to expectations that it will hurt many firms based in Switzerland that derive profit in currencies now weaker relative to the franc."Swiss Re has a long track record and a fantastic dividend yield.The stock is cheap based on price to earnings ratio, price to book, and has a good return on equity.I have to lean towards stating that Swiss RE is a buy.Editor's Note: This article discusses one or more securities that do not
