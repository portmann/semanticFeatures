LH , LLTC , MCD , PCYC , REGN , RH , W by: SA Editor Mohit Manghnani The market closed lower on Wednesday, but Cramer still thinks the market and stocks will go higher on any positive news.Citing Wayfair's (NYSE: W ) example, Cramer talked about the stock's rally when it went public in October last year.The stock declined eventually as investors turned negative, but when the company reported a 55% growth on Wednesday, the stock shot up 13%.The pin effects were seen in Restoration Hardware (NYSE: RH ) which gained as it belonged to the same sector as Wayfair.Same was observed in McDonald's (NYSE: MCD ).When the CEO was ousted, the stock gained and kept going higher as a research showed promising future for the company.The pin action was seen in pharma sector also.Bristol-Myers (NYSE: BMY ) ran up 6% after receiving FDA approval for its lung cancer drug and it picked up other big pharma stocks along.Even GoPro (NASDAQ: GPRO ) and Alibaba (NYSE: BABA ) rose on pin actions."I am not sugar coating the battlefield reports.Investors may have won these skirmishes, and yet still lost the battle for the session," said Cramer.He also said the stocks that were stable on Wednesday should be bought on Thursday as the smoke clears.CEO Interview - Honeywell (NYSE: HON ) Cramer said that Honeywell is one of the best-run companies in America, and the Chairman and CEO Dave Cote is a great leader.He interviewed Cote to find out where the company is headed."We have a plethora of new products that will go around the world in almost every single business.When I go back 13 years ago, we had just an empty pipeline.Now every single business has great new products and a lot of it is software based," said Cote.He spoke about how everything is connected today and that consumers will be taking control of their homes, factories, cars etc. via connectivity, where Honeywell is setting a strong footprint.Speaking about competition, he said that China is doing very good and has moved from No. 20 to No. 2 very quickly.If it keeps doing things right, it will be at the top.Commenting on the capital structure, he said the company will be raising dividends, investing in M&A and buying back stock.Cote talked about himself being bearish for the last four years but bullish this year as he sees strength in the economy."So I'm going to make sure that I'm still conservative.But I think the effect of lower oil prices, especially in any oil-importing country, will put a lot of money in consumers' pockets," said Cote.Gilead Sciences (NASDAQ: GILD ) trades at just 12 times earnings and it has been stable above $100.Cramer thinks it is one of the best stocks in biotech.Biogen Idec (NASDAQ: BIIB ) is another stock that Cramer is bullish on.He thinks the stock has room to run higher.Although Celgene (NASDAQ: CELG ) has seen a dip lately, Cramer thinks CEO Robert Hugin is capable of overcoming all challenges.Regeneron Pharmaceuticals (NASDAQ: REGN ) has stalled lately, but it has an anticholesterol drug in its pipeline.Cardiologists are excited about this drug and so is Cramer.BioMarin Pharmaceuticals (NASDAQ: BMRN ) and Isis Pharmaceuticals (NASDAQ: ISIS ) are two stocks in the emerging biotech field that Cramer has his eye on.Lastly, Alkermes Plc (NASDAQ: ALKS ) is another stock that Cramer is bullish on.He thinks the stock is cheap at current prices due the opportunities it has.CEO interview - Halyard Health (NYSE: HYH ) Halyard Health was spun off by Kimberly-Clark (NYSE: KMB ) four months back.The stock reported better than expected earnings on Wednesday but still fell due to the concern the spin-off costs are impacting guidance for 2015.Cramer interviewed CEO Robert Abernathy to find out more."We were laser focused, and we immediately started investing in research and development because research drives our strategy.We delivered a very strong quarter where we had sales and profit growth," said Abernathy.He also said that the company has generated significant cash flow and has about $149M on the balance sheet.Commenting on the strategic use of cash, he said, "In year one, in 2015, we want to continue to get off of those transition services agreements from Kimberly-Clark.In year two, we want to be ready to move rapidly to get some strategic acquisitions done to grow in our medical device category."He added that eventually the company wants to shift to high growth, high margin products.Cramer said that the company has a very low following on the Wall Street, but it has a big room to run, and he thinks now is
