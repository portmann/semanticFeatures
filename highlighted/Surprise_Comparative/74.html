Projections Point To An Overvalued Stock Despite The Tasty London Burger - Shake Shack (NYSE:SHAK) | Seeking Alpha Company beat expectations but sales outlook in-line with low same store sales.Investors may be facing a 0% return for the next 5 years based on management store growth.Friends in London were not familiar with the brand.There has been a lot of hype around Shake Shack (NYSE: SHAK ).As I live in Europe I only had the opportunity to experience the burgers last week in London.With earnings just out, I decided to take a look at the results, the valuation and share my London experience.Results beat expectations for the quarter.Sales and operating profit grew at around 50% with a strong operating margin of 22.3%.However operating margin decreased 40 bps and average weekly sales dropped 3.4%.Investors can expect margins to get worse because of the expansion of non-Manhattan stores.As per the prospectus, Manhattan stores have a 30% operating margin vs 22% for non-Manhattan.See the prospectus here .Average weekly sales are also expected to continue to drop in 2015 as per management's outlook.That outlook is what appears to have drove SHAK's price lower after hours.Analysts expected $161m for 2015 while SHAK's outlook was in-line at $159-163m.Same store sales are expected at the low single digits, while at least 15 more stores will be opened.Same-Shack sales growth in the low single digits.Average weekly sales for domestic company-operated Shacks will continue to decline."At least 10 new domestic company-operated Shacks to be opened throughout the year.""At least five international licensed Shacks to be opened under the Company's current license agreements in the U.K. and Middle East, all of which are scheduled to open towards the end of fiscal 2015."Medium-term Outlook: "Near-term, we are targeting at least 10 new domestic company-operated Shacks annually, with the goal of doubling our domestic company-operated store count in three years and tripling our store count in five years."For the time being it's best to ignore the longer-term outlook and focus on current figures and medium-term.Based on 4Q annualized sales, annualized adjusted EPS, annualized adjusted EBITDA, and book value, the company trades at 12x, 73x, 87x and 132x (using a market cap of $1660m).The company says it will triple stores in 5 years, so if we assume all else equal then sales will be around $418m.This means the stock is trading at 4x 2019 sales.This is quite crude but useful.We have not taken into account the potentially lower margins or sales per store as the company will face these issues as they expand.Not to mention keeping the quality the same and beating competition and health concerns... McDonald's (NYSE: MCD ) is trading at 3.4x sales, Yum Brands (NYSE: YUM ) at 2.6x sales, Chipotle (NYSE: CMG ) at 5x sales, and Starbucks (NASDAQ: SBUX ) at 4.1x sales.So basically if sales grow at these projected levels, and we assume that SHAK is among the high-flying competitors that also trade at around 4x, then SHAK will be fairly valued in 2019 and have the same valuation... so investors today make a 0% return....While in London for a conference, I asked about a dozen friends if they heard of SHAK.My crowd ranged from 20s to 50s.Only the oldest person actually heard of SHAK.So it doesn't look well-known.I asked my friend Perry to join me for lunch at Covent Garden - the shack's location.As you see from the photos, its not a big store.Fortunately we didn't have to sit in the square.The store is divided into 2 sections: the one where you order (shown in photos) and the one where you can sit (not shown).There are probably around 12 tables so its not huge but as people take a quick bite and leave, we found a seat.The service was excellent.After finding a table, a waiter took our buzzer (received after ordering) and brought us our food.The quality was again excellent.I enjoyed my burger and cheese fries.It wasn't cheap, as you can see from the menu and bill, but worth it.My friend Perry also enjoyed the burger but told me there were quite a few similar places.A few nights later I went to a bar/restaurant with friends and had an Angus beef cheeseburger which was around GBP 17 (before fries).This place was again at Covent Garden.So it looks like there is a) competition and b) its priced between McDonald's and other casual dining places.That means that some work will need to be done on the marketing side.That was confirmed by my friends who thought that the name was stupid.They thought it sold shakes but would never think it sold burgers... It's a great burger and there could be a place for SHAK if they can keep the quality consistent and build the brand, but the stock is overpriced.Current shareholders should exit the position, while there is money to be made on the short side once some momentum builds in that direction.Personally, I will be monitoring for a opportunity to short if price breaks lower on strong volume.Source: Projections Point To An Overvalued Stock Despite The Tasty London Burger Disclosure: The author has no positions in any stocks mentioned, but may initiate a short position in SHAK over the next 72 hours.(More...)The author wrote this article themselves, and it expresses their own opinions.The author is not receiving compensation for it (other than from Seeking Alpha).The author has no business relationship with any company whose stock is mentioned in this article.We only use your contact details to reply to your request for more information.We do not sell the personal contact data you submit to anyone else.Thank you for your interest in Seeking Alpha PRO We look forward to contacting you shortly for a conversation.Our PRO subscription service was created for fund managers, and the cost of the product is prohibitive for most individual investors.PRO Alerts is our flagship product for individual investors who want to be faster and smarter about their stocks.To learn more about it, click here.If you are an investment professional with over $1M AUM and received this message in error, click here and you will be contacted shortly.We look forward to contacting you when we have an individual investor product ready!
