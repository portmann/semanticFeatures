Denver-Based Summit Materials Rolls Out IPO - Summit Materials (Pending:SUM) | Seeking Alpha Summit Materials Holding, Inc. filed is trying to raise between $377 million and $422 million at expected share price.Over 22.2 million shares will be offered with an underwriter option for an additional 3.3 million shares.The expected price per share is between $17 and $19.Summit Materials was started in 2009 and moved to the LoDo (lower downtown) area of Denver in 2013.The company currently has $1.06 billion in debt and the proceeds of the IPO will go to pay that down.The company, begun in 2009 by CEO Tom Hill, has been based in the lower downtown area (LoDo) of Denver since 2013.It has been growing steadily with an effective social media strategy , primarily due to more than 30 acquisitions it has made with borrowed funds, and is using the upsurge in the market to file an IPO to pay back some of its current debt of $1.06 billion."The private construction market is beginning to rebound, which we believe signals the outset of a strong growth period in our industry and end markets," the company stated in its filing .The company plans to issue over 22.2 million shares, with an underwriter's option for an additional 3.3 million shares, which are expected to be valued between $17 and $19 per share.If the middle range for share prices is met, the company would have a market value of $1.7 billion , according to Renaissance Capital .According to its website , Summit Materials is "one of the fastest growing heavy-side construction materials companies in the United States, with a 126% increase in revenue between the year ended December 31, 2010 and the year ended December 28, 2013, as compared to an average increase of approximately 17% in revenue reported by our competitors over the same period."Summit employs more than 4,500 people nationwide.It supplies aggregate across the country with a focus on Texas, Kansas, Kentucky, Missouri and Utah.The company provides its customers with heavy-side construction materials and related products through vertical integration.They produce ready-mixed concrete and asphalt paving mix that can either be used by Summit's paving business or sold externally.The company had revenues of $1.2 billion in the last 12 months and a net income of $-6.3 million during the same time frame.Summit claims that it is one of the top 10 aggregate suppliers nationwide.The company said that it sold 1 million tons of cement and over 2.8 million cubic yards of ready-made concrete, as well as 4.3 million tons of asphalt paving material/mix.The current IPO plan will keep three-quarters of the voting rights within the company.The remaining quarter of the voting rights will be held by new shareholders.Although there is nothing sexy about investing in a building material company, it is comparable to investing in stone or gravel, there is a huge need for the material.Summit has a proven track record of success in a market that has been very harsh to its competitors.Increased revenue over the three years between 2010 and 2013 after only four years in business show a marked understanding of how to exploit vertical integration in Summit's business model.Summit understands and uses vertical integration - the arrangement by which the supply chain of a company is also owned by the company - to create a successful and profitable business model.One which shows no signs of faltering.At the expected price, Summit Material will be a good, if unexceptional, stock.Although necessary, it doesn't have either the flash of tech stocks or the high risk/high reward of financial stocks.The company, if it continues its current practices, will continue to show profit and expand the business for the foreseeable
