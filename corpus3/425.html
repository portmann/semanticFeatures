However, they do not point out that Schwab doesn’t charge transaction fees on Schwab ETFs and mutual funds, but they do charge brokerage transaction fees for Vanguard (and many other) ETFs. So trading the Schwab funds in initial purchases, redemptions, and rebalancing will be free unlike many other robo-advisors. Since Schwab ETFs and mutual funds generally have pretty low expense ratios compared to equivalent funds, that seems like a reasonable deal. Keep in mind that nothing will have a lower expense ratio than a Vanguard all-in-one fund to achieve a reasonable diversified portfolio.
