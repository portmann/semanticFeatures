April 6, 2015, concludes the 180-day lockup period on Smart & Final Stores Inc. (NYSE: SFS ). When the lockup period ends for Smart & Final, its pre-IPO shareholders, directors and executives will have the chance to sell their ~55 million shares . The potential for a sudden increase in stock available in the open market may cause a significant decrease in Smart & Final shares. Smart & Final Stores - Sell or Short Recommendation - $14.50 PT Smart & Final Stores is a non-membership, warehouse style food retailer that serves a diverse demographic of business and family customers through its two banners: Smart & Final and Cash & Carry. Currently, the company operates 252 stores in the Western United States and 13 stores in northwestern Mexico. Their merchandising strategy focuses on a broad array of private label products, high quality perishables, products targeted to food service and business markets, and products that come in a wide variety of sizes. Through October 2014, the Smart & Final banner stores produced 75.1 percent of total revenue, and the Cash & Carry banner stores produced 24.9 percent of total revenue. Smart & Final banner stores include 110 legacy stores and 90 Extra! format stores, which are located across California, Nevada and Arizona. The stores offer a large selection of everyday grocery items and fresh perishables, janitorial products and food service packaging materials. The Extra! Stores provide a one-stop shopping experience with a wider selection than the Smart & Final stores. Cash & Carry stores are located throughout Washington, Oregon, Idaho, Nevada and Northern California. In 2011, Smart & Final was purchased by Ares Management for Apollo Global Management for approximately $975 million. Under Apollo's management, Smart & Final upgraded its customer service in an effort to broaden its reach to the general consumer, increased store to 25,000 square feet from 17,000 square feet, tailored inventories to delete low velocity SKUs, and increased its offering of fresh produce. Smart & Final Stores will announce its fourth quarter results on March 18, 2015. It announced its third quarter results on November 12, 2014. Below are the highlights of that report: Comparable store sales growth of 6.6% An increase in net income of 1.9% to $10.2 million President and CEO David Hirz has served in his position since January 2012, and he previously served as President and COO between April 2010 and January 2012. He also serves on the board of directors. His prior experience includes positions at The Kroger Company (NYSE: KR ) and Ralphs Grocery Company. He graduated from California State University, Fullerton. SVP and CFO Richard Phegley has served in his position since May 2001, and he joined Smart & Final as VP and Treasurer in September 1996. His previous experience includes executive positions at Atlantic Richfield Company. He holds an MBA from the University of Southern California. Total supermarket sales in the United States in 2013 topped $620.2 billion . As such, this large market has an extensive list of companies that compete against one another for the retail consumer. These include Albertson's, Kroger, Safeway (NYSE: SWY ), Ralphs, Wal-Mart ( WMT ), Target (NYSE: TGT ) and Vons. In addition, the industry has discounters and warehouse clubs such as Costco (NASDAQ: COST ) and Sam's Club. Foodservice delivery companies include US Foods and Sysco (NYSE: SYY ). Smart & Final's IPO priced at $12 per share, at the low end of the expected price range of $12 to $14. The stock opened on the first day of trading at $13.10 and closed at $12.01. It reached a high of $16.53 on December 30, 2014, and it currently trades at $14.94 (3.5.2015).
