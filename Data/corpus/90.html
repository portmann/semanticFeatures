Bloomberg IPO Index Underperforming With New-Addition BABA | Seeking Alpha Mar. 3, 2015 3:44 AM ET  |  Includes: BABA by: Bespoke Investment Group Alibaba (NYSE: BABA ) was a strong IPO out of the gate, but it has faltered significantly since peaking late last year, and it's dragging down the Bloomberg IPO index that it has a massive weighting in. The Bloomberg IPO index is a cap-weighted index made up of companies that have IPOd over the last year. Because of its massive size compared to most IPOs, Alibaba has a ridiculously high 44% weighting in the index. BABA's size was great for the Bloomberg IPO index when the stock was going up, but its 30% decline from $120 to $84 over the last few months has caused the index to break down and lose out on a significant amount of outperformance versus the S&P 500 over the last two years. Below is a chart showing the performance of the Bloomberg IPO index versus the S&P 500 since the start of 2013. The second chart is simply the spread between the two indexes in the first chart.
